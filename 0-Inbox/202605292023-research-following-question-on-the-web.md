# Research: following question on the web

**“Following” – meanings and usage**

| Part of speech | Core meaning | Typical examples |
|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Preposition** | *after*; indicating something that comes later in time or as a result of something else. | “The weeks **following** the riots were tense.” [1] |
| **Adjective** | Refers to the next item in a list or the one that comes next. | “The **following** day, we met again.” [1] |
| **Noun** (less common) | The things that come after; a succession. | “The **following** were the results of the experiment.” [3] |

### Key points
- As a **preposition**, it functions like “after” or “subsequent to” (e.g., “Oil prices rose **following** the agreement”).  
- As an **adjective**, it precedes a noun to denote the next one in order (e.g., “the **following** chapter”).  
- It can also be used as a **noun** meaning “the things that follow” (e.g., “the **following** are my recommendations”).  

Both Cambridge and Merriam‑Webster list these senses, with Cambridge emphasizing the prepositional use and Merriam‑Webster providing the adjective and noun forms.
