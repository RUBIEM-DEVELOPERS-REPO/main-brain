# Research: Plan the rollout of MFA across all 1200 employees in 30 days. Include phases, owner per phase, and the top 3 risks with mitigations

**MFA Roll‑out Plan – 30 days / 1 200 employees**

| Phase | Days | Scope | Owner | Key Activities |
|-------|------|-------|-------|----------------|
| **1 – Preparation** | Day 1‑5 | Policy, tech stack, pilot group (≈5 % ≈ 60 users) | **Security Lead** (e.g., CISO) | • Finalise MFA policy (methods, fallback, device enrollment)  <br>• Verify MFA provider integration with IdP (Azure AD, Okta, etc.)  <br>• Build communication kit (email, intranet banner, FAQ)  <br>• Create pilot user list and schedule |
| **2 – Pilot & Feedback** | Day 6‑10 | Pilot users + support staff | **IT Operations Manager** | • Enrol pilot users, walk‑through setup (mobile app, hardware token)  <br>• Capture issues (device incompatibility, user confusion)  <br>• Refine documentation & support scripts |
| **3 – Tier‑1 Roll‑out** | Day 11‑20 | First 40 % of workforce (≈480 users) – grouped by department | **Department‑Level IT Leads** | • Bulk‑email invitation with step‑by‑step guide  <br>• Dedicated help‑desk “MFA‑Assist” channel (chat & phone)  <br>• Daily status dashboard (enrolment % , support tickets)  <br>• Auto‑reminders for non‑enrolled users |
| **4 – Full‑Scale Roll‑out** | Day 21‑27 | Remaining 55 % (≈660 users) | **Corporate Communications Lead** + **IT Ops** | • Organization‑wide reminder blast  <br>• Optional “MFA‑Drop‑In” virtual office hours  <br>• Enforce MFA enforcement policy on Day 28 (conditional access block) |
| **5 – Post‑Implementation Review** | Day 28‑30 | All users | **Security Lead** | • Verify 100 % enrollment via IdP reports  <br>• Review support ticket trends, close open cases  <br>• Publish “MFA Success” summary & next‑step security roadmap |

*All owners are accountable for their phase deliverables and must report progress to the **Project Steering Committee** (CISO, HR, Legal).*

---

### Top 3 Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| **1. User resistance / low adoption** | Delays enforcement, increased support load | • Early communication (policy rationale, security benefits) <br>• Pilot success story shared company‑wide <br>• Incentives (e.g., “MFA‑Champion” badge) <br>• Auto‑reminders and a hard cut‑off date (Day 28) |
| **2. Technical incompatibility (old OS, unsupported devices)** | Enrollment failures, security gaps | • Pre‑roll‑out inventory check (via endpoint management) <br>• Provide alternative methods (SMS OTP, hardware token) for legacy devices <br>• Document known incompatibilities and schedule remediation windows |
| **3. Support overload (ticket spikes)** | Service desk burnout, slower issue resolution | • Dedicated “MFA‑Assist” support team for the rollout period <br>• Self‑service knowledge base (FAQ, video walkthrough) <br>• Real‑time monitoring dashboard to re‑allocate staff as needed |
