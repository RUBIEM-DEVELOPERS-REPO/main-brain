**Typical daily / weekly workflow for a Senior Customer Success Manager (NRR‑owner, $50 K‑$500 K ARR accounts, 30‑account portfolio)**  

| Frequency | Core Activity | What the manager actually does (step‑by‑step) | Why it matters |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **Daily** | **Account health monitoring** | 1. Pull the health dashboard (usage, support tickets, product adoption, renewal date). 2. Flag any account whose health score drops < 80 % or shows a usage dip > 10 % week‑over‑week. 3. Add flagged accounts to the “at‑risk” list in the CRM. | Early detection of churn risk keeps Net‑Revenue Retention (NRR) high. |
|           | **Customer outreach** | 1. Send a quick “check‑in” email or Slack message to high‑usage customers (top 10 % of the portfolio). 2. Respond to inbound requests (feature questions, support escalations). 3. Log all interactions in the account record. | Maintains relationship cadence and captures upsell/cross‑sell signals. |
|           | **Collaboration with Product** | 1. Review the product‑feedback queue for any requests from owned accounts. 2. Prioritize requests that affect renewal or expansion. 3. Add high‑impact items to the weekly product‑partner meeting agenda. | Aligns product roadmap with revenue‑critical customer needs. |
|           | **Data hygiene / reporting** | 1. Update ARR, NRR, and churn forecasts in the sales ops sheet. 2. Ensure all meetings (QBRs, health calls) are scheduled in the calendar. | Accurate data feeds the executive dashboard and informs forecasting. |
| **Weekly** | **Quarterly Business Review (QBR) preparation** (held once per quarter, but prep is weekly) | 1. Pull the last 30 days of usage, health, and financial data for the upcoming QBR account. 2. Draft a 5‑slide deck: business outcomes, product usage, ROI, upcoming needs, and expansion opportunities. 3. Share the draft with the account’s executive sponsor for feedback. | Demonstrates value, surfaces expansion opportunities, and reinforces partnership. |
|           | **“Save‑play” execution on at‑risk accounts** | 1. Review the at‑risk list (identified daily). 2. For each account, schedule a dedicated “risk mitigation” call with the customer champion and internal stakeholders. 3. Apply a predefined playbook (e.g., usage training, customized success plan, discount or contract amendment). 4. Record outcome and next steps in the CRM. | Directly targets churn, protecting NRR. |
|           | **Team sync & product partnership** | 1. Attend the weekly CS‑Product alignment meeting (30 min). 2. Present top‑priority feature requests from owned accounts. 3. Capture product commitment dates and communicate them back to customers. | Ensures product roadmap reflects revenue‑critical feedback. |
|           | **Pipeline & expansion review** | 1. Review open expansion/opportunity tickets for the 30 accounts. 2. Update forecasted ARR uplift in the sales pipeline. 3. Coach account‑specific sales reps on positioning next‑step offers. | Drives upsell and cross‑sell, boosting NRR. |
| **Monthly** | **Executive business review (EBR) with leadership** | 1. Aggregate NRR, churn, and expansion metrics for the portfolio. 2. Highlight any systemic issues (e.g., product gaps) and propose remediation. 3. Align on resource needs (training, technical support). | Provides visibility to senior leadership and secures needed resources. |
|           | **Renewal & contract management** | 1. Identify contracts expiring in the next 60 days. 2. Initiate renewal conversations, surface ROI data, and negotiate terms. 3. Log renewal status and any discount approvals. | Directly impacts ARR retention. |

**Key take‑aways**

* **NRR ownership** drives a constant loop of health monitoring, risk mitigation, and expansion activities.  
* **Weekly QBRs** are not a single event; preparation and follow‑up consume regular time each week.  
* **Save‑plays** are a structured, repeatable process applied to any account that slips below health thresholds.  
* **Product partnership** is an ongoing cadence (weekly meetings, feedback triage) to turn customer requests into roadmap items.  
* Managing **30 accounts** means balancing high‑touch (executive calls, QBRs) with automated health checks and CRM discipline.
