# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak

**Tone:** *Neutral* – the request is procedural and does not convey emotion.  

**Underlying need:** The user wants a ready‑to‑use “quick‑reply” framework that they can copy‑paste when handling any incoming customer email. They are looking for a concise, step‑by‑step template that:

1. Names the tone.  
2. Extracts the real need (often hidden behind the literal ask).  
3. Opens with an acknowledgement that matches the tone.  
4. Provides a concrete solution and a firm commitment date.  
5. Highlights any churn‑risk or expansion‑signal language for follow‑up.

---

### Ready‑to‑Copy Customer‑Reply Template  

```
Hi [Customer Name],

**Acknowledgement (tone‑matched)**  
Thank you for reaching out – I understand you’re [briefly restate the emotion/concern, e.g., “frustrated with the delay” / “curious about the new feature”].

**Underlying need**  
It sounds like what you really need is [state the real need you’ve identified, e.g., “a faster way to export your reports” or “clarification on how the new permission model works”].

**Solution & commitment**  
I’ve [what you’ll do right now, e.g., “opened a ticket with our engineering team” / “located the documentation you need”] and will have an update for you by **[specific date, e.g., May 31, 2026]**.

**Next steps**  
- [Step 1: what the customer should do, if anything]  
- [Step 2: what you’ll do next]  

Please let me know if anything else comes up in the meantime.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Success, NeuroWorks
```

**How to use**

| Step | What to fill in |
|------|-----------------|
| 1️⃣  | Insert the customer’s name. |
| 2️⃣  | Choose the tone word (frustrated, confused, excited, neutral) and mirror it in the opening sentence. |
| 3️⃣  | Summarize the *real* need you’ve identified (not just the literal request). |
| 4️⃣  | State the concrete action you’ll take and attach a **specific calendar date**. |
| 5️⃣  | List any short‑term actions for the customer and yourself. |
| 6️⃣  | Sign off with your name and “NeuroWorks” (our AI‑workforce platform). |

---

### Flagging Signals  

- **Churn‑risk language** – look for phrases like “considering other solutions,” “not getting value,” or “thinking of canceling.” If any appear, add a follow‑up note in your CRM: *“High churn risk – schedule a 30‑min success review.”*  
- **Expansion‑signal language** – watch for interest in additional modules, “Would love to try X feature,” or “Can we scale this to more teams?” Log a **upsell opportunity** and set a discovery call within the next week.
