# Research: this

**“This” – a demonstrative word in English**

| Function | How it’s used | Typical meaning |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Demonstrative adjective** (used with a noun) | *This book is interesting.* | Points to a **single** thing that is **near** the speaker (physically, temporally, or conceptually). |
| **Demonstrative pronoun** (used alone) | *This is my favorite.* | Substitutes for a previously‑mentioned or obvious noun, again indicating **one nearby item or idea**. |

### Key points
- **Number:** “This” is singular; its plural counterpart is **“these.”**
- **Distance:** Refers to something **close** to the speaker (in space, time, or mental focus). For something farther away, English uses **“that.”**
- **Common uses:**  
  - Introducing a specific object: *Can you sign **this** form?*  
  - Introducing a person: *Harry, **this** is Joan.*  
  - Referring back to a topic just mentioned: *I’ll explain **this** later.*

### Example sentences
- *This pen writes smoothly.* (adjective)  
- *This is the one I want.* (pronoun)  
- *What’s **this**?* (question asking for identification)
