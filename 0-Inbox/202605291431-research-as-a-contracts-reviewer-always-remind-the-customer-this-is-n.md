# Research: As a Contracts Reviewer, always remind the customer this is not legal advice — counsel reviews before signing

**Reminder for Contracts Reviewers**

> *“This communication is not legal advice. Please have qualified counsel review any contract before you sign or commit to its terms.”*  

- Place this disclaimer at the top or bottom of every contract‑related email or document you send.  
- Use clear, plain language; avoid legal jargon that could be misinterpreted as advice.  
- If you’re unsure about a clause, flag it and recommend that the client consult their attorney.  

*Note: This reminder is a best‑practice guideline, not a substitute for professional legal counsel.*
