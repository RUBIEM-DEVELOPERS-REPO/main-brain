# Research: That missed the mark — try a different approach. Lean harder on real sources, follow the playbook structure (verdict / tier-tags / cohort / segmentation / etc. as the skill demands)

**Verdict**  
WordWall is a solid, mid‑tier ICT‑platform for building interactive English‑language worksheets for 7‑grade learners. It delivers clear lexical‑skill gains while remaining easy for teachers to adopt, but it lacks the breadth of advanced gamification found in newer Russian‑market alternatives (FlikTop, Interacty, Core).  

**Tier‑Tags**  
- **Tier 2 – Established ICT tool** (widely used in Russian secondary schools)  
- **Tag A – Lexical‑competence focus**  
- **Tag B – Low‑to‑moderate authoring complexity**  

**Cohort**  
- **Primary target:** 7‑grade students in supplemental English classes (≈12‑13 y/o).  
- **Secondary users:** Classroom teachers and after‑school instructors who need ready‑made, printable or online activities.  

**Segmentation**  
| Segment | Needs | WordWall fit | Competing service | Relative strength |
|---------|-------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Standard public school** | Quick‑setup, alignment with FOS (Federal State Educational Standards) | ✔︎ Pre‑made templates, auto‑scoring | FlikTop (more customisation) | Simplicity > flexibility |
| **Private language centre** | Differentiated tasks, branding | ✔︎ Custom colour/theme, embed videos | Interacty (gamified mechanics) | Adequate but less engaging |
| **Remote / blended learning** | Real‑time monitoring, analytics | ✖︎ Limited reporting | Core (real‑time dashboards) | WordWall weaker on data |
| **Low‑resource schools** | Free or low‑cost access | ✔︎ Free tier covers basic question types | All three have free tiers, but Core’s free plan is more limited | Cost‑effective |

**Evidence‑based rationale**

1. **Pedagogical impact** – The study of WordWall use in a 7‑grade English program reports that the platform improves lexical competence by providing automated, repeatable tasks and allowing teachers to tailor material to age‑specific needs【1】.  
2. **Advantages identified** – The same source notes three core benefits that align with the cohort’s needs:  
   - *Individualisation*: teachers select content matching learners’ age and proficiency.  
   - *Automation of control*: tasks are auto‑checked, easing formative assessment.  
   - *Re‑usability*: students can revisit exercises for reinforcement.【1】  
3. **Comparison with Russian services** – A separate review of three domestic interactive‑worksheet creators (FlikTop, Interacty, Core) outlines their feature sets (question types, storytelling, gamified mechanics, real‑time tracking)【2】. WordWall matches only basic question types (matching, fill‑in‑the‑blank, multiple choice) and lacks the richer game‑like elements and analytics of Interacty and Core.  

**Recommendations for implementation**

- **Start‑up phase** – Deploy WordWall’s ready‑made lexical games (e.g., “Match‑the‑picture”, “Fill‑the‑gap”) to quickly boost engagement.  
- **Data‑driven refinement** – Supplement WordWall with a simple spreadsheet log to capture completion rates, since the platform itself offers limited analytics.  
- **Scaling** – For schools that later require deeper gamification or real‑time monitoring, consider a phased migration to Interacty (for game‑based tasks) or Core (for blended‑learning dashboards).  

**Bottom line** – WordWall delivers reliable lexical‑skill practice for 7‑grade English learners with minimal learning curve, making it a Tier 2 choice for most Russian supplemental‑education settings. For institutions seeking richer interactivity or analytics, a hybrid approach that pairs WordWall with a more advanced Russian service is advisable.
