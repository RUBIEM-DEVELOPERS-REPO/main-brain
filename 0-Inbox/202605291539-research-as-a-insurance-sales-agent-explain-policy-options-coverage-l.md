# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, explain policy options, coverage limits, premiums, and exclusions

**Insurance‑Sales‑Agent Overview – What You Need to Explain to a Client**

| Element | What to Cover | Typical Details (examples) |
|---------|---------------|----------------------------|
| **Policy options** | Show the main lines of protection you offer and how they can be combined. | • **Auto / Motorcycle** – liability, collision, comprehensive, glass‑break, roadside assistance.<br>• **Home** – building, contents, earthquake, pipe‑burst, theft, flood.<br>• **Health / Medical card** – preventive check‑ups, dental (≈ 50 % coverage), speech/physiotherapy, psychological support.<br>• **Boat, Truck, Agricultural** – civil liability, damage, assistance, optional cargo or equipment cover.  (All of these are listed on the COSMOTE platform)【1】 |
| **Coverage limits** | State the maximum amount the insurer will pay for each type of loss. | • **Liability** – e.g., €1 M per accident for bodily injury.<br>• **Collision/Comprehensive** – up to the market value of the vehicle or a pre‑agreed sum‑insured.<br>• **Home** – rebuild cost or market value of the dwelling, plus a separate limit for contents.<br>• **Health** – annual caps for dental, physiotherapy, etc.  (Limits are selectable when the client customises the package)【1】 |
| **Premiums** | Explain how the price is calculated and what influences it. | • **Risk factors** – age, driving record, location, claim history, building age, security systems.<br>• **Coverage choices** – higher limits or added riders raise the premium.<br>• **Discounts** – multi‑policy bundles, online purchase, coupon codes (e.g., “MAGENTA MOMENTS”), low‑claim bonuses.<br>• **Payment options** – monthly, quarterly, or annual; some insurers allow automatic debit.  (COSMOTE advertises “different payment methods” and “price guarantee” for online quotes)【1】 |
| **Exclusions** | Clarify what is *not* covered so the client has realistic expectations. | • **Intentional damage or fraud**.<br>• **Wear‑and‑tear, mechanical breakdown (unless a specific breakdown rider is added).**<br>• **Acts of war, nuclear incidents, or civil unrest** (often excluded in property policies).<br>• **Pre‑existing medical conditions** for health cards unless disclosed.<br>• **Uninsured drivers** – some policies limit coverage for damage caused by drivers without insurance.  (Standard exclusions are mentioned in the coverage descriptions on the platform)【1】 |

### How to Present This Information

1. **Start with the client’s needs** – ask about vehicle type, home value, health concerns, etc.  
2. **Show a side‑by‑side comparison** using the online tool (COSMOTE’s “compare policies” feature) so the client can see how each option changes the premium in real time【1】.  
3. **Highlight the most relevant coverages** and the corresponding limits that match the client’s risk profile.  
4. **Explain the cost drivers** (risk factors, optional riders, discounts) and the available payment schedules.  
5. **Read out the key exclusions** for each line so there are no surprises later.  
6. **Offer a bundled quote** – bundling auto, home, and health often yields a 10‑40 % discount, as advertised by the provider【1】.  

### Quick Script Example

> “Based on your 2018 sedan and a €250 k home, we can start with a **basic auto liability** (€1 M limit) and **home building** cover at the full rebuild cost. Adding **collision** and **glass‑break** raises the auto premium by about 15 %, while a **health card** with dental and physiotherapy adds €30 / month. If you bundle all three, you’ll get a 20 % discount on the total premium. Please note the policy does **not** cover mechanical breakdowns unless you add a separate breakdown rider, and it excludes damage from war or nuclear events.”
