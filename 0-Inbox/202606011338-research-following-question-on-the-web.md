# Research: following question on the web

**“Following” – meanings and uses**

| Part of speech | Core meaning | Typical examples |
|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Preposition** | *after* or *as a result of* an event. | “The weeks **following** the riots were tense.”; “Oil prices rose **following** the agreement.” |
| **Adjective** (prenomal) | The next one in a sequence; the thing that comes after the one just mentioned. | “The **following** day we met again.”; “Read the **following** instructions.” |
| **Adjective** (referential) | Something that is about to be mentioned. | “Write down the **following** information: name, date, price.” |
| **Pronoun** | The items or people that are about to be listed. | “The **following** are the steps you must take.” |
| **Noun** | A group of supporters or admirers. | “The band has a large **following** in New Zealand.” |
| **Noun (countable)** | A wind or current moving in the same direction as a vessel. | “A **following** wind helped the sailboat.” |

**Key points**

- As a **preposition**, “following” is synonymous with *after* or *subsequent to* and is common in journalism (“the investigation **following** the report”).
- As an **adjective**, it can mean *next* (“the **following** year”) or *about to be mentioned* (“the **following** paragraph”).
- As a **pronoun**, it introduces a list (“the **following** items”).
- As a **noun**, it denotes a **supporter base** (“a devoted **following**”) or a **directional wind/current** in nautical contexts.
