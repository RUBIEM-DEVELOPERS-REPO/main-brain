# Research: crisp problem statement plus 3 measurable success metrics for a 'save chat as template' feature.

**Problem statement**  
Users of the chat platform repeatedly recreate the same conversation flow (e.g., onboarding scripts, support FAQs, sales pitches). Because each instance must be rebuilt manually, teams waste time, introduce inconsistencies, and struggle to propagate updates. A **“Save chat as template”** feature would let a user capture a finished conversation, name it, and reuse it later as a pre‑filled draft that can be edited or sent unchanged. This directly tackles the “lack of strategic alignment” that the video notes as a major cause of wasted effort and mis‑aligned work [1].

**Three measurable success metrics**

| # | Metric | How it is measured | Target (example) |
|---|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Template‑creation rate** – % of active users who create at least one chat template per month | (Number of users who saved a chat ÷ Total active users) × 100 | ≥ 15 % within 3 months of launch |
| 2 | **Reuse efficiency gain** – average reduction in time to start a repeat conversation | (Average time to compose a new repeat chat – Average time to insert a saved template) ÷ Average time to compose a new repeat chat × 100 | ≥ 40 % time saved per repeat chat |
| 3 | **Template‑driven consistency score** – proportion of repeat conversations that match the saved template without manual edits | (Number of reused templates sent unchanged ÷ Total reused templates) × 100 | ≥ 80 % of reused templates unchanged within 6 weeks |

These metrics give a clear, quantifiable view of adoption (Metric 1), productivity impact (Metric 2), and the strategic benefit of aligning repeated work to a single, vetted source (Metric 3), directly addressing the alignment problem highlighted in the source material [1].
