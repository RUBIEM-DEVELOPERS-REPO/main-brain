# Research: insurance sales agent client needs assessment and suitable product recommendations

**Insurance‑Agent Client Needs Assessment + Product‑Fit Process**

| Step | What to do | Why it matters (evidence) |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| **1. Prepare the tools** | • Have a written **Client Needs Assessment (CNA) form** ready.<br>• Be contracted and trained on at least one carrier in each product line you plan to recommend. | A CNA is useless if you can’t deliver a solution; the author stresses “pin down at least one competitive carrier… before you start asking the questions”【1†L13-L20】. |
| **2. Set the stage** | Open the conversation with a script that frames the assessment as a safety‑check, not a sales pitch (e.g., “Your drug plan is right where it needs to be… I’d love to ask a few more questions to make sure we’re not leaving anything uncovered”). | Clients are more receptive when they understand the purpose and see you’re protecting their interests【1†L1-L8】. |
| **3. Conduct the full CNA** | • Ask every question on the form before discussing any product.<br>• Take notes, but keep the flow; don’t jump to a solution mid‑assessment. | Stopping halfway “disrupts the entire flow of your appointment” and makes the presentation feel forced【1†L13-L16】. |
| **4. Identify and prioritize needs** | Review the answers and rank needs by urgency (e.g., life‑insurance gap, long‑term‑care concern, retirement income shortfall). | The assessment often uncovers multiple needs; you should focus on the most pressing first to avoid overwhelming the client【1†L27-L33】. |
| **5. Match needs to products** | • Pull the carrier(s) you’re contracted with that fit each need (e.g., annuity for retirement income, AIG for final‑expense, Aetna for cancer insurance).<br>• Use your FMO or product‑support team for any gaps in expertise. | The author relies on a support team to “find the best product fit” so you don’t have to be an expert on every line【1†L21-L24】. |
| **6. Present a tailored recommendation** | • Summarize the client’s situation, the key need, and the specific product that meets it.<br>• Explain how the product solves the need, using plain language and cost/benefit highlights. | A clear, need‑driven story makes the recommendation feel like a solution, not a sales push. |
| **7. Set next steps** | • Agree on a follow‑up meeting to address secondary needs.<br>• Update the CNA after any life‑event (beneficiary change, health change, etc.). | “Do a CNA with every client at every appointment” because circumstances change and new needs emerge【1†L9-L12】. |
| **8. Document & follow‑up** | Record the assessment, product recommendation, and client’s decision in your CRM. Schedule a check‑in (6‑12 months) to reassess. | Ongoing assessments keep the relationship current and open future cross‑sell opportunities【2†title】. |

### Quick Script Example
> “Your Medicare Supplement looks solid, but I have a few additional questions to make sure we haven’t missed anything important. This will only take a few minutes and helps us protect you from any gaps that might show up later.”

### Tips for Success
* **Practice** the CNA with friends or family before the real call (role‑play builds confidence)【1†L19-L22】.  
* **Customize wording** to sound natural; agents who tweak questions see better results【1†L17-L18】.  
* **Don’t try to close every need in one meeting** – prioritize and schedule follow‑ups to avoid “selling everything” perception【1†L27-L33】.

Following this structured, evidence‑based workflow lets you uncover genuine gaps, match them to the right insurance products, and build trust while maximizing sales opportunities.
