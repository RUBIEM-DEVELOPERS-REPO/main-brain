# Research: this

**“This” – definition and usage**

*This* is a demonstrative word that can function as a **determiner** or **pronoun**. It points to a specific person, object, idea, or situation that is near in space, time, or thought to the speaker.

| Role | How it’s used |
|------|---------------|
| **Determiner** | Placed before a noun to specify it: “Can you sign **this** form?” |
| **Pronoun** | Stands alone for a previously‑mentioned noun: “**This** is the one I want.” |
| **Introductory** | Introduces a person to another: “Harry, **this** is Joan.” |
| **Temporal** | Refers to a point in time: “By **this** time tomorrow, we’ll be finished.” |

**Key points**

- *Singular*: **this** (vs. **these** for plural).  
- Used for things that are **close** to the speaker (physically, conceptually, or temporally).  
- Often followed by a noun (“this book”) or used alone when the referent is clear from context.  

*Sources*: Cambridge Dictionary entry for “this” explains its role as a determiner and pronoun and gives example sentences [1]. Merriam‑Webster provides the same definition and usage notes [2].
