**Answer – Typical daily / weekly workflow for a Senior Customer Success Manager (NRR‑owner)**  

*Because the vault contains no specific notes on this role, the outline below is built from general best‑practice patterns for senior CSMs who manage $50 K–$500 K ARR accounts, run quarterly business reviews (QBRs), execute “save” plays, and partner with product on feature requests. If you need a more detailed, NeuroWorks‑specific process, let me know and I can pull the relevant internal documentation.*  

| Frequency | Core Activities | Goal / Output |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Daily** | • Review dashboard for health scores, usage metrics, and NRR impact across the 30‑account portfolio.<br>• Respond to inbound client tickets, Slack/Email, and escalations (especially from at‑risk accounts).<br>• Log all client interactions in the CRM (notes, next steps, sentiment tags).<br>• Identify any new feature requests or product gaps raised in conversations and add them to the product‑partner backlog. | Keep a real‑time pulse on account health; ensure no client issue goes unnoticed; capture actionable insights for product. |
| **Every 2 days** | • Run “save‑play” triage: prioritize accounts flagged as “at‑risk” (e.g., declining usage, support spikes, payment issues).<br>• Draft a short outreach plan (call, executive sponsor email, value‑reinforcement material). | Early intervention to prevent churn and protect NRR. |
| **Weekly** | • **Account‑level check‑ins** (30 min–1 h) with each high‑touch client (focus on top‑10 revenue accounts).<br>• **Internal sync** with Sales, Finance, and Support to align on renewal dates, upsell opportunities, and risk flags.<br>• **Product partnership meeting** (30 min) to review aggregated feature requests, share client use‑case data, and prioritize roadmap items.<br>• Update the **NRR forecast** in the revenue ops sheet and flag any variance. | Maintain strong relationships, surface cross‑functional issues, and keep the revenue forecast accurate. |
| **Bi‑weekly** | • Conduct **mid‑quarter health reviews** for at‑risk accounts: present usage analytics, ROI calculations, and a tailored success plan.<br>• Document outcomes and next steps in the account plan. | Re‑engage slipping accounts before the formal QBR. |
| **Monthly** | • **Quarterly Business Review (QBR) preparation** (even though QBRs are weekly in the JD, many orgs run them monthly for each account): gather KPI trends, success stories, and ROI metrics; build a slide deck.<br>• **Run the QBR** (30‑45 min) with the client’s executive sponsor and internal stakeholders; review past performance, discuss upcoming initiatives, and negotiate renewal/expansion.<br>• Capture agreed actions and update the account plan. | Demonstrate value, align on future goals, and drive renewal/expansion. |
| **Quarterly** | • **Full‑scale QBR** (if not already weekly) – deeper strategic session covering long‑term roadmap, product roadmap alignment, and partnership opportunities.<br>• Review **NRR results** for the quarter, identify win/loss drivers, and present findings to leadership. | Strategic alignment and performance reporting. |
| **Ad‑hoc / As‑needed** | • **Escalation handling** – join calls with Support or Engineering for critical incidents.<br>• **Executive business reviews** with C‑level contacts for top‑tier accounts.<br>• **Training / Enablement** – run webinars or workshops to boost product adoption. | Resolve high‑impact issues quickly; deepen executive relationships; drive adoption. |

**Key Metrics Tracked**

- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** – target ≥ 115 % for the portfolio.  
- **Health Score** – composite of usage %, support tickets, and NPS.  
- **Churn Risk Index** – number of accounts in “save” stage.  
- **Feature Request Velocity** – time from client request to product acknowledgment.
