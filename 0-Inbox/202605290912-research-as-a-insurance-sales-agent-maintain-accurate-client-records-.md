# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, maintain accurate client records and follow up on policy renewals

**Keeping client records clean and staying on top of renewals is a two‑part process: data hygiene + proactive follow‑up.**  

### 1. Build a reliable record‑keeping foundation  

| Action | Why it matters | How to do it |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Audit and de‑duplicate** after every busy enrollment or sales cycle. Merge duplicate client or policy rows and delete inactive entries. | Duplicate or stale data leads to errors, compliance risks and missed renewals. | Run a quick “find duplicates” report in your CRM (e.g., AgencyBloc, Knack) and resolve each match. |
| **Standardize key fields** – carrier name, policy number, effective dates, contact info, beneficiaries, dependents. | Consistent formatting makes searching, reporting and automated reminders work reliably. | Use drop‑down lists or validation rules in the CRM to enforce the same format for each field. |
| **Attach supporting documents** – signed applications, compliance forms, call recordings, notes from enrollment conversations. | Complete records reduce confusion, support commission tracking and protect against audits. | Scan/upload files directly to the client’s record and tag them (e.g., “2024‑AEP notes”). |
| **Verify contact details** (email, phone, mailing address) at least quarterly. | Accurate contact info is the backbone of any renewal outreach. | Include a short “record‑update” email or phone script in your quarterly touch‑point schedule. |

> *“Having accurate, up‑to‑date client and policy records reduces errors, supports compliance, and enables smoother renewals and commissions tracking.”* – [2]

### 2. Create a systematic renewal‑follow‑up workflow  

1. **Set renewal alerts 30‑90 days before the policy expiration date.**  
   *Most CRMs let you create automated tasks or calendar events tied to the policy’s “expiration” field.*  

2. **Automate a multi‑step outreach cadence:**  
   - **Day -60:** Friendly reminder email with a summary of current coverage and a link to schedule a review call.  
   - **Day -30:** Phone call script (use the “client‑needs” questions from Smart Choice to uncover life‑or‑business changes).  
   - **Day -15:** Follow‑up email with any new product options or cross‑sell opportunities identified during the call.  
   - **Day -5:** Final confirmation of renewal paperwork or next‑step instructions.  

   Automation tools (AgencyBloc, Knack, or any CRM with workflow capabilities) can trigger these messages automatically, ensuring **no client is overlooked** and reducing reliance on memory or spreadsheets.  
   > *“Automation gives consistent client communication, fewer missed touchpoints, and improved retention.”* – [2]

3. **Document every interaction.** Add notes, call recordings, and any client feedback to the record immediately after each contact. This creates a clear audit trail and informs future conversations.  

4. **Analyze renewal performance monthly.** Pull reports on:  
   - Renewal rate vs. prior year  
   - Policies that lapsed or were cancelled  
   - Cross‑sell conversions during renewal  

   Use these insights to refine scripts, identify gaps in coverage, and adjust your prospecting pipeline.  

5. **Leverage client‑need conversations for cross‑selling.** When you ask insightful questions (“What recent changes have occurred in your business…?”) you can spot gaps and propose complementary policies, boosting revenue while strengthening the relationship. – [1]

### Quick checklist for daily/weekly use  

- ☐ Review CRM dashboard for upcoming renewals (next 30 days).  
- ☐ Verify contact info for each renewal case.  
- ☐ Send or schedule the next step in the automated outreach sequence.  
- ☐ Log call notes and attach any new documents.  
- ☐ Update renewal status (e.g., “Quote sent”, “Pending client review”, “Renewed”).  

By **cleaning data continuously** and **embedding an automated, multi‑touch renewal cadence**, you protect compliance, minimize missed renewals, and create more opportunities for cross‑selling—key drivers of both retention and growth.
