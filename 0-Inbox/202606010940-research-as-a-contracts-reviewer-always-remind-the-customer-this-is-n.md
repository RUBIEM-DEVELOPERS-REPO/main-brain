# Research: As a Contracts Reviewer, always remind the customer this is not legal advice — counsel reviews before signing

**Reminder (always include this first)**  
*The information below is for general informational purposes only and does **not** constitute legal advice. Before signing any contract, you should have the document reviewed by qualified legal counsel.*  

---  

### Why a reminder is needed
- As a Contracts Reviewer your role is to flag potential issues, not to give binding legal opinions.  
- Only a licensed attorney can assess enforceability, interpret jurisdiction‑specific statutes, or advise on risk mitigation.  

### How to phrase the reminder
> “Please note that the comments I’m providing are for informational purposes only and do not constitute legal advice. You should have this contract reviewed by your own legal counsel before signing.”

### Quick checklist for reviewers
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Identify** | Highlight clauses that may be unusual or risky (e.g., force‑majeure, indemnity, termination). |
| **2. Flag** | Add a comment noting the clause and that a lawyer should review it. |
| **3. Insert reminder** | Use the phrasing above at the top or bottom of your review. |
| **4. Document** | Record the date, reviewer name, and that the reminder was provided. |

By consistently adding the disclaimer, you protect both yourself and the client from the misconception that your review substitutes for legal counsel.
