**Neuroworks – Current Status (July 2025)**  

| Aspect | What’s happening now | Source |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| **Product positioning** | Neuroworks is Natus’ flagship EEG software, described as “the world’s most widely accepted EEG software” with a reputation for an easy‑to‑use interface and advanced functionality. | 【2】 |
| **Recent technological milestones** | • 2001 – spike & seizure detection<br>• 2002 – dual‑SleepWorks PSG<br>• 2005 – first wireless EEG amplifier<br>• 2015 – high‑channel Quantum amplifier<br>• 2024 – launch of **autoSCORE**, the first AI‑based model for comprehensive clinical EEG analysis and interpretation. | 【1】 |

| **Integration & multimodality** | As of July 2025, Neuroworks now **synchronises seamlessly with the Moberg CNS Monitor**. The partnership is highlighted as a major step toward “data‑driven patient care” and is already being used in critically ill patients. | 【1】 |
| **Market reach** | Introduced in 1999, Neuroworks remains the most widely used EEG platform globally, trusted in routine, ambulatory, ICU, newborn, and animal EEG applications. | 【1】 |
| **Strategic direction** | Natus is continuing to expand Neuroworks’ functionality through AI (autoSCORE) and multimodal integration (Moberg CNS), positioning the software as the core of comprehensive neuro‑monitoring solutions rather than a stand‑alone EEG tool. | 【1】 |

### Bottom line
Neuroworks is a mature, market‑leading EEG platform that is actively evolving. The 2024 AI‑driven autoSCORE and the July 2025 integration with Moberg’s CNS Monitor represent the latest pushes to make Neuroworks the central hub for multimodal neuro‑critical monitoring, while version 9.1 adds new long‑term monitoring capabilities. The product remains widely deployed across hospitals and research settings worldwide.
