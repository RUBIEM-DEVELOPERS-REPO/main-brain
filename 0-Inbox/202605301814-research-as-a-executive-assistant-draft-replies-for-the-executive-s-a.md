# Research: As a Executive Assistant, draft replies for the executive's approval — never pretend to be them

**Draft Reply Templates for Executive Approval**  
*(All drafts are written from the Executive Assistant’s perspective and are intended for the executive’s review and sign‑off. They never impersonate the executive.)*

---

### 1. Meeting Confirmation  

> **Subject:** Confirmation – [Meeting Title] – [Date]  
>   
> Dear [Recipient Name],  
>   
> Thank you for proposing the meeting on **[Date]** at **[Time]** (‑[Time Zone]). I have reserved a conference room (or virtual link) and added the agenda items you suggested. Please let me know if any adjustments are needed.  
>   
> Best regards,  
> **[Your Name]** – Executive Assistant to **[Executive’s Name]**

### 2. Travel Itinerary – Approval Needed  

> **Subject:** Review – Upcoming Travel Itinerary ( [Destination] )  
>   
> Hi [Executive’s Name],  
>   
> Please review the attached itinerary for your trip to **[City]** ( [Departure Date] – [Return Date] ). Highlights include:  
> - **Flight:** [Airline & Flight #] – [Departure Time] / [Arrival Time]  
> - **Hotel:** [Hotel Name] – [Check‑in/out]  
> - **Ground transport:** [Details]  
>   
> Let me know if you’d like any changes or additional arrangements. Once approved, I will forward the details to the travel team.  
>   
> Thanks,  
> **[Your Name]**

> Thank you for understanding.

> Best,

**How to Use These Drafts**  
1. **Insert specific details** (names, dates, times, links).  
2. **Send to the executive** for a quick glance and any wording tweaks.  
3. **After approval**, copy the final version into the appropriate email client and send.  

These templates follow the guidance that the assistant *drafts* the reply and *never pretends* to be the executive. Feel free to adapt tone and length to match your executive’s preferred style.
