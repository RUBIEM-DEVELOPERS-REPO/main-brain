# Research: As a Executive Assistant, close open loops — chase commitments made on the executive's behalf

**Closing Open Loops — Chasing Commitments on the Executive’s Behalf**

An Executive Assistant (EA) acts as the “right‑hand” of a senior leader, ensuring that decisions the executive has made are followed through to completion. The executive’s role, by definition, is to *make decisions and put them into action*【1†L7-L9】. Therefore the EA’s responsibility is to track those decisions, surface any unfinished items, and drive them to resolution.

| Step | What the EA does | Why it matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| **1. Capture every commitment** | Record promises, deadlines, and next‑steps in a single, searchable system (e.g., the NeuroWorks task vault). | Guarantees nothing falls through the cracks; the executive’s “action‑oriented” mandate is supported. |
| **2. Prioritise by impact & deadline** | Flag items that affect high‑stakes projects or have near‑term due dates. | Aligns with the executive’s focus on strategic outcomes. |
| **3. Assign ownership** | Identify the responsible person (internal team, external partner, or the executive) and note it beside the task. | Clarifies accountability and speeds up follow‑up. |
| **4. Schedule regular “loop‑check” reviews** | Add a recurring 15‑minute slot (daily or every other day) to scan the open‑loop list and update status. | Keeps the list current and signals to the executive that follow‑up is systematic, not ad‑hoc. |
| **5. Send concise status nudges** | Use brief, action‑oriented messages: *“Hi [Owner], the deadline for [Commitment] is [Date]. Do you need anything from me to close this?”* | Nudges are respectful, clear, and reduce the executive’s inbox noise. |
| **6. Escalate only when needed** | If a commitment stalls after two reminders, brief the executive with a summary and recommended next steps. | Protects the executive’s time while still maintaining oversight. |
| **7. Close the loop formally** | Once completed, mark the task as done, send a short “Done” note to stakeholders, and archive the record. | Provides a clear audit trail and reinforces a culture of follow‑through. |

### Practical Tips for the EA

- **Use a single source of truth** – keep all commitments in the NeuroWorks platform so you can query, filter, and report instantly.  
- **Leverage templates** – pre‑written reminder emails or chat messages cut down on drafting time.  
- **Link to related documents** – attach meeting minutes or decision logs so owners have context at a glance.  
- **Visual dashboards** – a simple Kanban board (e.g., “To‑Do | In‑Progress | Done”) makes status visible at a glance for both you and the executive.  

### Outcome

By systematically closing open loops, the EA turns the executive’s *decision‑making* role into *delivered results*, freeing the leader to focus on higher‑level strategy while maintaining credibility with internal and external partners.
