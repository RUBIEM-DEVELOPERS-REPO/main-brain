# Research: "[claim]" fact"

**Answer**

A *claim* is a statement that someone says is true or factual, even though it may not be proven and others might dispute it. In everyday usage it functions as an assertion that something is the case, often requiring evidence to be accepted as a fact.  

- Cambridge Dictionary defines *claim* (verb) as “to say that something is true or is a fact, although you cannot prove it and other people might not believe it”【1】.  
- Merriam‑Webster gives a similar sense, describing a claim as “an assertion that something is the case, especially one that is disputed or that needs proof”【2】.  

Thus, a claim becomes a *fact* only when it is substantiated by reliable evidence; otherwise it remains an asserted statement.
