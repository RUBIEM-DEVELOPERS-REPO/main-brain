# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak

**Tone:** Neutral (the request is procedural, not emotional).  

**Underlying need:** A clear, step‑by‑step draft that (1) names the customer’s tone, (2) surfaces the real need behind their words, (3) replies with an appropriate acknowledgment, resolves that need, and includes a firm date for any promised action, and (4) flags any churn‑risk or expansion signals.  

**Draft reply**

> Hi [Customer Name],  
> 
> Thanks for reaching out – I hear you’re looking for [brief restatement of the literal ask]. What’s most important to you right now is [the underlying need you identified, e.g., “getting the report back on time so you can meet your internal deadline”].  
> 
> I’ve scheduled the work to be completed by **Wednesday 15 May 2026** and will send you the final file directly to this thread. If anything changes on your end, just let me know and I’ll adjust accordingly.  
> 
> Please feel free to reply if you have any other questions or if there’s anything else we can do to help.  

**Flag:** No churn‑risk language detected; the customer’s tone is neutral and the request is routine, so no immediate expansion signal either.
