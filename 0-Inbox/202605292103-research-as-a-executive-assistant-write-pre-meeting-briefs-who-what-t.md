# Research: As a Executive Assistant, write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer.

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer
Description: Persona-derived starter task for "Evie"

**Pre‑Meeting Brief – Executive Assistant (Evie)**  

| Element | Content | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | The meeting participant(s) are *executives* – senior individuals who make decisions and put them into action in a business or government context.  They may be “senior executives” or “the executive” of a department/organisation. | Cambridge Dictionary defines an executive as “someone in a high position, especially in business, who makes decisions and puts them into action”【1】; Wikipedia notes the executive branch “oversees administration of the state”【2】 |
| **What they want** | • **Clarity on options** – executives need concise, decision‑ready information. <br>• **Alignment with strategic goals** – any proposal must tie to the organisation’s high‑level objectives. <br>• **Actionable next steps** – they expect a clear recommendation and a brief implementation plan. |
| **The ask** | *“Give me a recommendation on X (e.g., a vendor, a project scope, a policy change) and the rationale in ≤ 3 bullet points, plus the top‑line impact on cost, timeline, and risk.”*  This mirrors the executive’s preference for succinct, outcome‑focused briefings. |
| **Recommended answer** | 1. **State the recommendation** in a single sentence. <br>2. **Provide 2‑3 supporting points** (e.g., ROI, alignment with strategy, risk mitigation). <br>3. **Summarise impact**: cost estimate, timeline, and key risk. <br>4. **Next step**: schedule a follow‑up decision call or assign a point‑person. <br>*(Structure follows the executive’s need for quick, actionable insight.)* |

**How to use this brief**  
1. **Identify the executive** (name, title, decision‑making authority).  
2. **Gather the specific topic** they need a decision on.  
3. **Populate the template** with the four rows above, keeping each cell ≤ 2‑3 sentences.  
4. **Deliver** via email or a short slide deck no longer than one page.  

*Note:* If you need more context (e.g., the executive’s current priorities), check the vault for any existing meeting notes or strategy documents before finalising.
