# Research: neuroworks and give me a summary

**NeuroWorks – Overview**

NeuroWorks is Natus Medical’s flagship EEG software platform. It is marketed as a comprehensive, modular solution for clinical neurophysiology, supporting a wide range of recording and analysis needs:

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **EEG Acquisition** | Handles routine, ambulatory, long‑term, and ICU EEG, as well as aEEG for newborns and animal studies. |
| **Signal Processing & Review** | Provides real‑time display, automated artifact detection, quantitative EEG (qEEG) trends, and advanced seizure‑detection algorithms. |
| **Integration** | Works with Natus hardware (e.g., EEG amplifiers, intracranial pressure monitors) and can import/export data in standard formats (EDF, BDF, etc.). |
| **Reporting & Documentation** | Built‑in report templates, customizable annotations, and the ability to generate PDF or HL7‑compatible reports for electronic health records. |
| **Additional Modalities** | Includes modules for EMG/NCS/EP, polysomnography (PSG), and neurocritical‑care monitoring, allowing a single workstation to serve multiple neuro‑diagnostic services. |
| **Support & Training** | Natus offers remote support, service programs, and educational resources through Natus Academies. |

The platform is multilingual (English, French, German, Italian, Spanish) and is positioned for use in hospitals, sleep labs, research centers, and even point‑of‑care settings. Its modular design lets users add or upgrade specific functionalities (e.g., long‑term monitoring, ICU EEG) without replacing the entire system.
