# Multi-perspective research: vendor quotes and recommend one. Normalise to 12-month TCO including onboarding + training + implementation. Build a comparison matrix on cost / risk / fit / implementation / support / exit-cost (with

## Topic statement
The request is to gather vendor quotations, normalize them to a 12‑month total cost of ownership (including onboarding, training and implementation), and recommend a single vendor by building a comparison matrix that scores each option on cost, risk, fit, implementation effort, support and exit‑cost. The evidence provided consists of generic definitions of “vendor” from mainstream, critical, practitioner and recent perspectives, without any actual quotation data, methodology guidance or case‑study details.

## Perspectives  

### mainstream  
The mainstream sources only define what a vendor is and list generic vendor types (manufacturers, retailers, wholesalers, service providers) [1][2][3]. They do not discuss how to collect quotes, calculate 12‑month TCO, or construct a comparison matrix.  

### critical  
The critical perspective supplies a website unrelated to procurement (a Greek classifieds site) and repeats the same generic vendor definitions found in the mainstream sources [4][1][2]. No criticism of quotation‑comparison methods or identification of pitfalls is offered.  

### practitioner  
The practitioner sources again repeat the Investopedia and Wikipedia definitions of vendor and provide no practical guidance, templates or real‑world examples for normalising costs or scoring vendors [1][2][3].  

### recent  
The recent perspective mirrors the earlier definitions and adds no new information about recent best‑practice, tools or data sources for building the requested matrix [1][2][3].  

## Cross‑cutting themes
- All perspectives treat “vendor” as a generic supply‑chain term and list the same four basic categories (manufacturer, retailer, wholesaler, service provider) [1][2][3].  
- None of the sources provide actual vendor quotes, cost‑normalisation formulas, risk‑assessment frameworks or matrix templates.  

## Open questions
- **Where can concrete vendor quotations be obtained?** No perspective identifies data sources or procurement processes.  
- **What methodology should be used to normalise disparate cost elements (licensing, onboarding, training, implementation) to a 12‑month TCO?** No guidance is offered.  
- **How should risk, fit, implementation effort, support quality and exit‑cost be quantified and weighted?** Perspectives are silent on scoring criteria.  
- **Which vendor ultimately best satisfies the combined criteria?** Without data or a scoring model, no recommendation can be made.  

## Bottom line
The available evidence only supplies textbook definitions of “vendor” and does not address any of the substantive steps required to collect quotes, normalize them to a 12‑month total cost of ownership, or evaluate vendors across cost, risk, fit, implementation, support and exit‑cost. Consequently, no informed recommendation can be produced from the current sources; additional, domain‑specific procurement data and methodological guidance are needed to fulfil the task.
