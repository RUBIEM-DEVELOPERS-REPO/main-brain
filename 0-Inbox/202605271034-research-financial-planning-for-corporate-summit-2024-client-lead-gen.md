# Research: financial planning for corporate summit 2024 client lead generation strategy

**Financial‑Planning Checklist for a 2024 Corporate Summit + Lead‑Generation Playbook**

| Phase | Key Actions | Why it matters (evidence) | Quick‑Tip |
|-------|-------------|---------------------------|-----------|
| **1. Budget & ROI Forecast** | • Set a summit‑specific budget (venue, speakers, tech, marketing). <br>• Model expected revenue from new client contracts and cross‑sell opportunities. | A clear financial plan lets you justify spend and measure success after the event. | Use a simple spreadsheet with “cost × expected conversion rate” to set a target ROI (e.g., 150 % breakeven). |
| **2. Define Target Audience** | • Profile the ideal prospect (e.g., high‑net‑worth executives, CFOs, HR heads). <br>• Align summit topics (wealth‑preservation, ESG investing, retirement planning) with their pain points. | Targeted content drives higher‑quality leads, a pattern shown in advisor surveys where referrals and niche‑focused outreach outperform generic tactics【1】. | Draft a one‑page “Ideal Attendee” sheet and share it with sales, marketing, and speakers. |
| **3. Build a Referral Engine** | • Ask existing clients to invite peers; offer a modest “thank‑you” incentive (e.g., exclusive briefing). <br>• Create a referral script for staff to use in pre‑summit calls. | Client referrals generate the highest‑quality leads for advisors (Kitces 2024 survey)【1】. | Include a short “We’re looking for 10 new partners this year” line in newsletters and invitation emails. |
| **4. Leverage Professional Lead‑Gen Services** | • Subscribe to a vetted service (e.g., SmartAsset AMP) that delivers pre‑qualified prospect lists and automated outreach. <br>• Integrate the feed with your CRM for real‑time tracking. | Specialized services automate the heavy‑lifting of prospect acquisition, freeing advisors to focus on relationship‑building【1】. | Test a 30‑day pilot and compare cost‑per‑lead to organic channels. |
| **5. Social‑Media Amplification** | • Run LinkedIn Sponsored Content promoting the summit’s keynote topics. <br>• Use targeted hashtags and a custom landing page to capture registrations. | 40 % of advisors added clients via social media; LinkedIn and Facebook were top performers【1】. | Mirror the language of high‑performing posts (keywords identified through audience research) for better click‑through rates. |
| **6. Content‑Driven Nurture** | • Post‑event, send a “Highlights & Next Steps” email series with recorded sessions, a CTA to schedule a discovery call, and a referral ask. <br>• Add a short “Ask a Planner” webinar for attendees who didn’t convert. | Ongoing nurture converts summit interest into closed business and fuels a pipeline of referrals. | Automate the series in your email platform; track open/response rates to refine messaging. |
| **7. Measure & Optimize** | • KPI dashboard: registration cost, lead‑to‑meeting conversion, meeting‑to‑client conversion, and net new AUM. <br>• Conduct a post‑summit debrief to adjust budget allocations for the next event. | Quantifying results validates the financial plan and highlights the most efficient lead sources. | Set a “break‑even” threshold (e.g., 5 new clients at $200k AUM each) and compare actuals. |

### Immediate Next Steps (First 2 Weeks)

1. **Finalize budget & ROI targets** – lock in venue cost and set a minimum $‑return goal.  
2. **Create the Ideal Attendee profile** – circulate to sales and speaker teams.  
3. **Launch a referral outreach** – email current clients with a personal invitation link and incentive.  
4. **Enroll in a lead‑gen service** (SmartAsset AMP) and sync the feed to your CRM.  
5. **Set up LinkedIn ad campaign** – use the attendee profile keywords from the research.  

By combining disciplined financial planning with the proven lead‑generation tactics—referrals, professional lead services, and social‑media outreach—you can both control summit costs and maximize the pipeline of high‑quality prospects for 2024.
