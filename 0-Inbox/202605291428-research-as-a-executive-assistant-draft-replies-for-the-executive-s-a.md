# Research: As a Executive Assistant, draft replies for the executive's approval — never pretend to be them

**Executive‑Assistant Draft‑Reply Guide**  
*(All drafts are written for the executive’s review and sign‑off – the assistant never signs as the executive.)*  

---

## 1. Core Principles (based on the definition of “executive” as a senior decision‑maker)  

| Principle | Why it matters | How to apply it |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Authority + Clarity** – an executive is “someone in a high position … who makes decisions and puts them into action”【1†L1-L5】 | The reply must convey that the final decision rests with the executive, not the assistant. | Use phrasing such as “I will forward your request to [Executive Name] for final approval.” |
| **Brevity & Professional Tone** – executives are busy senior leaders. | Keeps the communication efficient and respects the executive’s time. | Lead with the purpose in one sentence, then give only essential details. |
| **Explicit Call‑to‑Action** – the executive’s role is to act on the request. | Makes it clear what the recipient should expect next. | End with “Please let me know if you need any further information before the executive reviews.” |
| **Never Impersonate** – the assistant must never sign or speak as the executive. | Maintains transparency and avoids misrepresentation. | Sign the draft as “Prepared by [Your Name], Executive Assistant” and add “For review by [Executive Name]”. |

---

## 2. Template Library  

### A. Meeting Request / Calendar Invite  
```
Subject: Proposed Meeting – [Topic] – [Proposed Date/Time]

Hi [Recipient],

[Executive Name] would like to discuss [brief purpose] and has asked me to check your availability.  

Proposed slots:  
- [Option 1] – [Date] at [Time]  
- [Option 2] – [Date] at [Time]  

Please let me know which works best, or suggest alternatives, and I’ll confirm with [Executive Name].

Thank you,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
```

### B. Approval Needed for Document / Proposal  
```
Subject: Review Required – [Document Title]

Dear [Recipient],

Attached is the draft of [document/proposal] that [Executive Name] intends to sign.  
Please review the highlighted sections and provide any comments by **[deadline]** so the executive can make a final decision.

If no changes are needed, simply reply “Approved” and I will forward the final version to [Executive Name] for signature.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
```

### C. Declining an Invitation (on behalf of the executive)  
```
Subject: Re: Invitation to [Event]

Hi [Organizer],

Thank you for the invitation to [event]. Unfortunately, [Executive Name] has a scheduling conflict and will be unable to attend.

We appreciate the opportunity and hope to collaborate on future events.

Kind regards,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
```

### D. Follow‑up After a Meeting  
```
Subject: Follow‑up – Action Items from [Meeting Title]

Hi Team,

Thank you for a productive discussion on [date]. Below are the agreed‑upon action items and the executive’s next steps:

1. **[Task]** – Owner: [Name] – Due: [Date]  
2. **[Task]** – Owner: [Name] – Due: [Date]  

Please confirm receipt and let me know if any clarification is needed before [Executive Name] proceeds.

Regards,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
```

### E. Request for Information / Data  
```
Subject: Information Request – [Specific Data]

Dear [Colleague],

[Executive Name] is preparing a briefing on [topic] and needs the latest figures for [specific metric]. Could you please send the requested data by **[deadline]**?

Your prompt assistance is appreciated.

Thank you,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
```

---

## 3. Quick‑Check Checklist (before sending any draft)

1. **Is the executive’s name correctly spelled?**  
2. **Is the tone professional and concise?**  
3. **Does the draft end with a clear next step?**  
4. **Is the signature “Prepared by … Executive Assistant” and not the executive’s?**  
5. **Have any confidential attachments been double‑checked for proper clearance?**  

---

### How to Use This Guide
1. **Select the appropriate template** for the situation.  
2. **Fill in the placeholders** (e.g., names, dates, topics).  
3. **Run the checklist** to ensure compliance with the core principles.  
4. **Send the draft to the executive** for final approval before distribution.  

*All drafts are ready for the executive’s review; the assistant never pretends to be the executive.*
