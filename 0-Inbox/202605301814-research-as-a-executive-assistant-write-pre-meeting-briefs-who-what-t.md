# Research: As a Executive Assistant, write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer

**Pre‑meeting brief template (Executive Assistant)**  

| Section | What to include | Why it matters |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Who** | • Name, title, department of each participant<br>• Brief background (recent projects, key responsibilities)<br>• Decision‑making authority (e.g., final approver, influencer) | Gives you and the meeting leader a quick mental picture of the audience and their stakes. |
| **What they want** | • Primary objective each attendee is trying to achieve (e.g., “secure budget for Q3 marketing push,” “clarify timeline for product launch”) <br>• Any known concerns or constraints they have expressed beforehand | Anticipating their goals lets you steer the agenda toward outcomes they care about and pre‑empt objections. |
| **The ask** | • Specific decision or action you need from the group (e.g., “approve $150 k spend,” “sign off on revised roadmap”) <br>• Supporting data points or documents that will be referenced | A clear, concise ask keeps the meeting focused and makes it easy for participants to say “yes” or provide the needed feedback. |
| **Recommended answer** | • Your suggested response or decision (based on prior analysis, stakeholder input, and company policy) <br>• Key rationale (benefits, risks, alignment with strategy) <br>• Any follow‑up steps required after the meeting | Provides the meeting leader with a ready‑to‑present position, saving time and demonstrating preparedness. |

---

### How to use this template

1. **Gather information** – Pull the latest notes from the vault (e.g., recent project updates, stakeholder maps) and any relevant emails or documents.  
2. **Fill each row** – Keep bullet points short (1‑2 lines) and factual.  
3. **Validate** – Double‑check that the “ask” matches the meeting’s official agenda and that the “recommended answer” aligns with current policy or budget limits.  
4. **Distribute** – Send the brief to the meeting organizer and participants at least 24 hours before the meeting, attaching any supporting files referenced.

### Example (hypothetical)

| Who | What they want | The ask | Recommended answer |
|-----|----------------|---------|--------------------|
| **Jane Doe, VP Marketing** | Secure funding for Q3 digital campaign; wants measurable ROI. | Approve $120 k budget. | Approve $120 k; tie spend to KPI dashboard that tracks CPL and conversion rates. |
| **John Smith, Finance Director** | Ensure spend stays within FY budget; needs cost‑benefit justification. | Sign‑off on budget allocation. | Approve with quarterly review clause; highlight projected 15 % lift in revenue. |
| **Emily Chen, Product Lead** | Align campaign timing with product launch; needs clear go‑live date. | Confirm launch date and messaging sync. | Set launch for 15 Oct; coordinate messaging calendar with marketing. |
