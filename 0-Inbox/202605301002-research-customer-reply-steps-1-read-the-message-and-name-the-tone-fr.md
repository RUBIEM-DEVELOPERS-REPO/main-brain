# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak

**Tone:** Neutral (the message is a procedural request, no emotion expressed).

**Underlying need:** The customer wants a quick, concrete example of how to apply the “customer‑reply” process – i.e., a ready‑to‑use draft that follows the four steps without generic “macro‑speak”.

**Draft reply** (include date for any commitment):

> Hi [Customer Name],  
>   
> Thanks for sending the outline. I’ve put together a concise reply that follows the exact steps you asked for:  
>   
> 1️⃣ **Tone:** Neutral – the message is informational, not urgent or emotional.  
>   
> 2️⃣ **Underlying need:** You need a clear, actionable response that shows we understand the request and can deliver on it promptly.  
>   
> 3️⃣ **Reply:**  
>   
> > *Hi [Name],*  
> >   
> > *I’ve reviewed your request and can confirm we’ll have the report ready by **Wednesday, June 12 2026**. I’ll send the final version to you directly and will follow up on Thursday to ensure everything looks good.*  
> >   
> > *Please let me know if there’s anything else you’d like included.*  
> >   
> > *Best, [Your Name]*  
>   
> 4️⃣ **Flag:** No churn‑risk language detected; the commitment to a specific delivery date (June 12) is an expansion‑signal that demonstrates reliability and may encourage further collaboration.

Let me know if you’d like any tweaks or additional details!
