# Research: As a Financial Analyst, run variance analysis: actual vs plan vs prior period, with explanations

**Variance Analysis – Actual vs. Plan vs. Prior Period**  
*(Financial Analyst perspective – concise template with typical explanations)*  

| **Metric** | **Prior Period** | **Plan (Budget/Forecast)** | **Actual** | **Δ Actual‑Plan** | **Δ Actual‑Prior** | **Explanation / Drivers** |
|------------|------------------|----------------------------|------------|-------------------|--------------------|----------------------------|
| Revenue | $ X M | $ Y M | $ Z M | **$ Z‑Y M** ( %Δ = (Z‑Y)/Y × 100 %) | **$ Z‑X M** ( %Δ = (Z‑X)/X × 100 %) | • Market demand shift (e.g., new product launch, price changes)  <br>• Volume vs. price variance  <br>• Currency effects (if applicable) |
| Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) | $ A M | $ B M | $ C M | **$ C‑B M** ( %Δ = (C‑B)/B × 100 %) | **$ C‑A M** ( %Δ = (C‑A)/A × 100 %) | • Material price changes (raw‑material index)  <br>• Production efficiency (yield, waste)  <br>• Supplier contract renegotiations |
| Gross Profit | $ G M | $ H M | $ I M | **$ I‑H M** ( %Δ = (I‑H)/H × 100 %) | **$ I‑G M** ( %Δ = (I‑G)/G × 100 %) | • Combined effect of revenue and COGS variances  <br>• Mix‑shift toward higher‑margin SKUs |
| Operating Expenses (OPEX) | $ J M | $ K M | $ L M | **$ L‑K M** ( %Δ = (L‑K)/K × 100 %) | **$ L‑J M** ( %Δ = (L‑J)/J × 100 %) | • Staffing changes (hiring, overtime)  <br>• Marketing spend variance (campaigns, media mix)  <br>• Depreciation/amortisation schedule updates |
| EBITDA | $ M M | $ N M | $ O M | **$ O‑N M** ( %Δ = (O‑N)/N × 100 %) | **$ O‑M M** ( %Δ = (O‑M)/M × 100 %) | • Result of revenue, COGS, and OPEX variances  <br>• One‑off items (e.g., restructuring costs) |
| Net Income | $ P M | $ Q M | $ R M | **$ R‑Q M** ( %Δ = (R‑Q)/Q × 100 %) | **$ R‑P M** ( %Δ = (R‑P)/P × 100 %) | • Tax rate changes (effective tax %)  <br>• Interest expense variance (debt refinancing)  <br>• Extraordinary gains/losses |

### How to Use the Table  

1. **Populate the numbers** from your ERP/BI system for the three columns (Prior, Plan, Actual).  
2. **Calculate the dollar and percentage variances** (Δ Actual‑Plan, Δ Actual‑Prior).  
3. **Add concise driver notes** for each line‑item variance. Typical drivers include:  
   * **Revenue:** price vs. volume, new contracts, churn, seasonality, FX.  
   * **COGS:** input‑cost index, production yield, inventory write‑downs.  
   * **OPEX:** head‑count, marketing spend, IT upgrades, rent escalations.  
   * **EBITDA/Net Income:** combine the above plus tax/interest effects.  
4. **Highlight material variances** (e.g., > 5 % or > $ 0.5 M) for deeper investigation.  
5. **Summarise the overall performance** in a short narrative: “Revenue beat plan by 3 % driven by a 4 % price increase offset by a 1 % volume dip; COGS rose 2 % due to higher commodity prices, narrowing gross margin to X %.”  

### Quick Checklist for Explanation Quality  

| ✅ | Item |
|---|------|
| ☐ **Root‑cause identification** – link each variance to a specific business event. |
| ☐ **Quantify impact** – state the monetary and percentage effect. |
| ☐ **Action recommendation** – suggest corrective or capitalising steps (e.g., renegotiate supplier contracts, adjust pricing). |
| ☐ **Forward‑looking view** – indicate whether the variance is expected to persist. |
