# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, maintain accurate client records and follow up on policy renewals.

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Maintain accurate client records and follow up on policy renewals.
Description: Persona-derived starter task for "Insurance Sales Agent"

**How an Insurance Sales Agent Can Keep Accurate Client Records & Stay on Top of Policy Renewals**

| Step | What to Do | Why It Matters (Evidence) |
|------|------------|----------------------------|
| **1. Centralize client data** | Use a single CRM or spreadsheet that captures: <br>• Personal details (name, address, contact info) <br>• Policy specifics (type, coverage limits, deductible, premium, effective & expiration dates) <br>• Notes on interactions, claims, and preferences | Consolidated records prevent duplicate entries and make it easy to pull a client’s full history when you need it. |
| **2. Standardize data fields** | Create a template with mandatory fields (e.g., policy number, renewal date, premium amount). Enforce consistent formats for dates, phone numbers, and monetary values. | Consistency improves searchability and reduces errors when generating renewal notices. |
| **3. Tag policies by renewal cycle** | Add a “renewal‑date” column and a status flag (e.g., *Upcoming*, *Pending Review*, *Renewed*, *Lapsed*). | The core components of any insurance policy—premium, policy limit, deductible—are tied to a specific term; tracking the term end date lets you act before the policy lapses【1†L31-L38】. |
| **4. Set automated reminders** | • Use calendar alerts (7 days, 30 days before expiration). <br>• If your CRM supports it, create workflow rules that email you or the client when a renewal is due. | Proactive outreach is a best practice for maintaining client relationships and avoiding gaps in coverage. |
| **5. Conduct a renewal review** | Before contacting the client, pull the policy record and: <br>• Verify current coverage still meets the client’s needs. <br>• Check for any changes in premium, deductible, or limits. <br>• Note any new underwriting factors that could affect pricing. | Insurance policies are built around three components—premium, policy limit, deductible—so any change in these elements should be discussed with the client【1†L31-L38】. |
| **6. Document every interaction** | After each call/email, log: <br>• Date & time <br>• Summary of discussion <br>• Action items (e.g., send quote, request documents) <br>• Follow‑up date | A complete audit trail helps you recall past promises and demonstrates professionalism if disputes arise. |
| **7. Send renewal proposals early** | Provide the client with a written renewal quote at least 30 days before the policy expires. Include: <br>• Updated premium, limits, deductible <br>• Any optional endorsements <br>• Clear instructions for acceptance or changes | Early proposals give the client time to compare options and reduces the chance of a lapse. |
| **8. Confirm renewal & update records** | Once the client accepts, record the new policy number, effective date, and any changed terms. Mark the old record as *Renewed* and archive it for reference. | Keeping the “renewed” flag up‑to‑date ensures your reminder system won’t trigger false alerts. |
| **9. Periodic data clean‑up** | Quarterly, review the database for: <br>• Duplicate entries <br>• Out‑of‑date contact info <br>• Policies that have lapsed without follow‑up | Regular housekeeping maintains data integrity and keeps your renewal pipeline accurate. |
| **10. Leverage reporting** | Generate monthly reports showing: <br>• Number of policies up for renewal next 30/60 days <br>• Renewal conversion rate <br>• Revenue impact of renewals | Reporting lets you spot bottlenecks (e.g., many “Pending Review” statuses) and adjust your workflow accordingly. |

### Quick Checklist (Copy‑Paste)

- [ ] All client info entered into central CRM/spreadsheet  
- [ ] Renewal date column populated for every policy  
- [ ] Automated reminders set (7 d & 30 d)  
- [ ] Interaction log updated after each contact  
- [ ] Renewal quote sent ≥ 30 days before expiration  
- [ ] Accepted renewal recorded and old record archived  
- [ ] Quarterly data clean‑up performed  

Following this structured process ensures you maintain **accurate, up‑to‑date client records** and **never miss a policy renewal**, which is essential for client trust and steady revenue growth.
