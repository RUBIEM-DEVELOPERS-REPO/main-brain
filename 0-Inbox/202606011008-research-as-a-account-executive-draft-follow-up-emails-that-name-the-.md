# Research: As a Account Executive, draft follow-up emails that name the next step + a date

**Follow‑up Email Templates (Account Executive)**  

Below are two ready‑to‑send drafts that name a clear next step and a specific date. They follow the style you’ll find in the research notes you created on 2026‑05‑29 ([0‑Inbox/202605291451‑research‑as‑a‑account‑executive‑draft‑follow‑up‑emails…]​[0‑Inbox/202605292305‑research‑as‑a‑account‑executive‑draft‑follow‑up‑emails…]).

---

### 1️⃣ After an initial discovery call  

**Subject:** Next steps – Demo on Tuesday, June 11  

Hi *[Prospect Name]*,

Thanks again for the great conversation yesterday. I’m excited about how **[Your Solution]** can help *[Prospect Company]* reduce *[pain point]* and accelerate *[goal]*.

**Next step:** Let’s schedule a 30‑minute live demo so you can see the product in action and discuss integration details.  

**Proposed date & time:** **Tuesday, June 11 at 10:00 AM EST** (or let me know a slot that works better for you).

I’ll send a calendar invite once you confirm. Looking forward to showing you the platform’s capabilities.

Best,  
*[Your Name]*  
Account Executive – *[Your Company]*  
Phone | Email | LinkedIn  

---

### 2️⃣ After sending a proposal  

**Subject:** Review & next steps – Decision call on Friday, June 14  

Hi *[Prospect Name]*,

I hope you had a chance to review the proposal I sent on June 5. The outlined pricing and implementation plan are designed to deliver **[specific ROI]** within the first *[timeframe]*.

**Next step:** A brief 20‑minute call to address any questions and confirm the rollout timeline.  

**Proposed date & time:** **Friday, June 14 at 2:30 PM EST** (please suggest an alternative if needed).

Please let me know if that works for you, and I’ll lock it in.

Thanks again for considering **[Your Solution]**. I’m confident we can achieve the results you’re looking for.

Best regards,  
*[Your Name]*  
Account Executive – *[Your Company]*  
Phone | Email | LinkedIn  

---

#### How to use these templates
1. **Replace placeholders** (`*[…]*`) with the prospect’s name, company, your product’s name, and any specific metrics you discussed.  
2. **Adjust the date/time** if the suggested slot conflicts with your calendar.  
3. **Add a brief personal note** (e.g., reference a comment they made) to keep the tone warm and tailored.  

These drafts follow the guidance from your own research notes and give the prospect a single, concrete action and deadline, which drives momentum toward closing the deal.
