# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, explain policy options, coverage limits, premiums, and exclusions

**Insurance‑Sales‑Agent Overview – What You Need to Explain to a Prospect**

| Topic | What to Cover | Typical Examples (from Pakistani insurers) |
|-------|---------------|--------------------------------------------|

| **Premiums** | • **How they’re calculated** – age, gender, health status, sum‑insured, term length, occupation, lifestyle. <br>• **Payment frequency** – monthly, quarterly, annually, or “pay‑first‑few‑years‑only” (e.g., State Life’s 3‑year‑pay term). <br>• **Factors that raise/lower cost** – no‑claim bonuses, discounts for bundles, higher deductibles. | Adamjee’s quote screen shows a “TOTAL PREMIUM” field that updates as the limit and age are selected [1]. ### How to Present This to a Client
1. **Start with their needs** – ask about assets, health status, family dependents, travel habits, etc.  
2. **Match needs to policy options** – e.g., a young family may benefit from a joint‑life/health bundle; a frequent traveler may need a travel‑plus‑medical rider.  
3. **Show the coverage‑limit ladder** – illustrate how a higher limit raises the premium but reduces out‑of‑pocket risk.  
4. **Explain the premium schedule** – highlight any “pay‑first‑years‑only” or discount options that fit their cash‑flow.  
5. **List the key exclusions** – use plain language and give concrete examples (e.g., “if you have a heart condition diagnosed before the policy starts, it will be excluded from health coverage”).  
6. **Answer questions & provide a quote** – use the insurer’s online calculator (Adamjee) or the agent portal (State Life) to generate a personalized premium figure.

> **Bottom line:** Policy options, limits, premiums and exclusions are all inter‑dependent. By walking the prospect through each element—showing concrete limits (e.g., Rs 500 k hospitalization), explaining how age and coverage affect the premium, and being transparent about what is *not* covered—you build trust and help the client choose the right protection.
