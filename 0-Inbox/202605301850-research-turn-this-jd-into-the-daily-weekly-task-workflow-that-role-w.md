**Senior Customer Success Manager – Daily / Weekly Workflow**

| Frequency | Core Activity | Goal / Output | Typical Steps |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Daily** | **Account Health Check** | Spot early warning signs; keep NRR on track | • Review dashboard for the 30‑account portfolio (usage, health scores, support tickets, upcoming renewals). <br>• Flag any accounts that dip below the health threshold (e.g., < 80 %). <br>• Add at‑risk accounts to the “Save Play” queue. |
|           | **Customer Touches** | Build relationships, surface value | • Send a brief “check‑in” email or Slack message to 5–7 accounts (rotate the list). <br>• Respond to inbound customer queries within SLA (usually ≤ 4 h). |
|           | **Internal Sync** | Align with product, sales, support | • Attend the 15‑min stand‑up with the CS Ops lead to share at‑risk accounts and any blockers. <br>• Update the shared “Feature Request” board with new customer ideas. |
|           | **Data Hygiene** | Keep CRM accurate for forecasting | • Log all interactions, outcomes, and next steps in the CRM (e.g., NeuroWorks CS Hub). <br>• Ensure account plans are current (objectives, success metrics, escalation contacts). |
| **Weekly** | **Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Prep** (for accounts scheduled that week) | Demonstrate value, drive expansion | • Pull the last 30 days of usage, health trends, and ROI metrics. <br>• Draft a customized agenda (wins, upcoming roadmap, upsell/cross‑sell opportunities). <br>• Share the draft with the account’s executive sponsor for feedback. |
|           | **QBR Execution** | Strengthen partnership, identify growth | • Host the 45‑60 min video call (or in‑person if local). <br>• Review health score, usage trends, and any open tickets. <br>• Present roadmap items and collect product feedback. <br>• Agree on next‑step actions and update the account plan. |
|           | **Save‑Play Execution** (for flagged accounts) | Reduce churn, improve NRR | • Prioritise the top 3‑5 at‑risk accounts. <br>• Run a “save play” meeting: review pain points, propose quick‑win solutions, involve product or engineering as needed. <br>• Document the outcome and next‑step timeline. |
|           | **Feature‑Request Collaboration** | Influence product roadmap | • Consolidate all customer‑submitted requests from the week. <br>• Score them (impact, frequency, strategic fit). <br>• Meet with the Product Manager to discuss feasibility and timeline. <br>• Communicate status back to customers. |
|           | **Pipeline Review** | Forecast renewals & expansions | • Update the renewal forecast for the 30‑account portfolio. <br>• Identify accounts with > 90 % renewal probability and those needing a renewal strategy. <br>• Share insights with the Sales Enablement lead. |
|           | **Team Knowledge Share** | Spread learnings, improve playbooks | • Contribute a short “win‑or‑loss” note to the CS knowledge base (NeuroWorks CS Wiki). <br>• Participate in the weekly CS round‑table to discuss challenges and best practices. |

### Typical Weekly Rhythm (example)

| Monday | • Health‑score dashboard review <br>• Quick check‑ins (5 accounts) <br>• Stand‑up |
| Tuesday | • Deep dive on at‑risk accounts (save plays) <br>• Feature‑request triage |
| Wednesday | • Prepare QBR decks for accounts scheduled Thursday/Friday |
| Thursday | • Conduct QBRs (2–3 accounts) <br>• Follow‑up email with action items |
| Friday | • Renewal pipeline update <br>• CS knowledge‑share post <br>• End‑of‑week recap with manager |

### Key Metrics Tracked

- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** – target ≥ 110 % for the $50K‑$500K ARR segment.  
- **Health Score** – maintain ≥ 80 % for > 90 % of accounts.  
- **Save‑Play Success Rate** – close ≥ 70 % of at‑risk accounts.  
- **Feature‑Request Conversion** – % of customer requests that reach product roadmap.  
- **QBR Attendance & Satisfaction** – ≥ 90 % attendance, CSAT ≥ 4.5/5.

> **Note:** The above workflow is assembled from standard Customer Success best practices; the NeuroWorks vault does not contain a pre‑written template for this role, so the answer is based on industry norms rather than internal documentation.
