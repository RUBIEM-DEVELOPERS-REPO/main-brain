# Research: As a Data Analyst, write findings memos with the chart, the takeaway, and the recommended action

**Findings‑Memo Template (Data‑Analyst Style)**  

| Section | What to Include | Why it Matters |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Title & Context** | • Brief, descriptive title (e.g., “Q2 2024 Customer‑Churn Analysis”)  <br>• Date, author, audience, and project reference | Sets scope and lets readers locate the memo quickly. |
| **Key Chart** | • One high‑impact visual (bar, line, scatter, heat‑map, etc.) that directly answers the core question <br>• Caption that states what the chart shows, the metric, and the time frame <br>• Source footnote (data set, query, refresh date) | Visuals convey trends faster than tables; a caption makes the insight self‑contained. |
| **Takeaway** | • One‑sentence headline that summarizes the insight (e.g., “Churn rose 12 % YoY after the pricing change”). <br>• One‑paragraph explanation of the pattern, including any statistical significance or confidence level if applicable. | Gives busy stakeholders the “so what” without digging through the chart. |
| **Recommended Action** | • Specific, measurable next step(s) (e.g., “Pilot a 10 % discount for at‑risk customers in the next 4 weeks”). <br>• Owner(s) and target deadline. <br>• Expected impact (e.g., “Projected to reduce churn by 3 %”). | Turns insight into execution; assigning responsibility and timeline drives follow‑through. |
| **Methodology (optional)** | • Data sources, cleaning steps, and analytical techniques used. | Provides transparency and reproducibility for technical audiences. |
| **Appendix (optional)** | • Additional charts, raw tables, or code snippets. | Allows deeper dive without cluttering the main memo. |

### How to Use the Template
1. **Select the most compelling chart** that answers the business question.  
2. Write a **concise takeaway** that directly references the chart’s trend.  
3. Propose a **single, actionable recommendation** tied to the insight, naming the responsible team and a realistic deadline.  
4. Fill in the supporting sections (title, methodology, appendix) as needed.
