**Lane 1 – What a “polymath” is**

A polymath (or polyhistor) is a person whose knowledge spans many different subjects and who can draw on that breadth to solve specific problems.  The term comes from Greek *poly* “many” + *manthanein* “to learn.”  Historically it is linked to the Renaissance ideal of the “universal man,” exemplified by figures such as Leonardo da Vinci and Benjamin Franklin, who combined deep expertise in several fields with a wide‑ranging curiosity [1].  Modern scholarship (e.g., Robert Root‑Bernstein) distinguishes polymaths from specialists (deep but narrow) and dilettantes (broad but superficial), emphasizing that true polymaths achieve both breadth and a level of proficiency in at least some domains [1].

**Lane 2 – The “Polymath” maths‑practice platform**

“Polymath” is also the name of a free, teacher‑built classroom app for practising mathematics.  It uses an adaptive algorithm to give each student personalised questions, letting them work either collaboratively in a timed class game or independently on their own devices.  Teachers can monitor progress through the “Relate” dashboard, set ability levels, and invite students to practice at home via a unique code.  The platform is marketed for primary‑age learners (roughly elementary school) and is designed to be non‑competitive, encouraging whole‑class engagement while still tailoring difficulty to individual needs [2].
