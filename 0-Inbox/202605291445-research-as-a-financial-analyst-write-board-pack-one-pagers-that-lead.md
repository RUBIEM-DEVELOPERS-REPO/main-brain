# Research: As a Financial Analyst, write board-pack one-pagers that lead with the number, then the why

**Board‑Pack One‑Pager Template – “Number → Why”**  

| Section | What to put | How it reads |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **1️⃣ Key Metric (the number)** | The single figure that best captures the decision’s impact (e.g., *Revenue growth = +12 % YoY*, *Cash‑conversion cycle = 45 days*, *Cost‑to‑serve reduction = $3.2 M*). | **12 % YoY revenue growth** |
| **2️⃣ Why it matters** | One‑sentence explanation linking the metric to strategic objectives, risk, or value creation. Use verbs that convey direction (*drives*, *protects*, *enables*). | **Why?** It validates the new pricing model and lifts EBITDA margin by 150 bps, supporting our 2027 profitability target. |
| **3️⃣ Supporting context (optional)** | • Trend line (last 3‑6 periods)  <br>• Benchmark vs. peers or plan  <br>• Key driver(s) behind the number | • +4 % Q‑on‑Q, outpacing the 2 % industry average. <br>• Driven by 8 % uplift in subscription renewals. |
| **4️⃣ Action / Decision needed** | Clear ask for the board (approve, defer, monitor). Keep it to one line. | **Approve the incremental marketing spend** to sustain the renewal lift. |
| **5️⃣ Risks / Caveats** | One bullet on the biggest upside/ downside that could swing the number. | **Risk:** Churn could rise if price elasticity is higher than modeled. |

### Why This Structure Works
1. **Instant impact** – Executives scan for numbers first; leading with the metric grabs attention and frames the conversation.  
2. **Logical flow** – The “why” immediately follows, turning a raw figure into a story that ties to strategy, risk, and ROI.  
3. **Brevity** – One‑pager limits cognitive load; each section is a single line or short bullet, making the deck easy to digest in board meetings.  

### Quick Writing Checklist
- **Select ONE headline number** that changes the board’s view.  
- **Quantify the “why”** in < 20 words; tie to strategic KPI (growth, margin, cash, risk).  
- **Use active verbs** (“drives”, “protects”, “enables”).  
- **Add only one supporting visual** (sparkline or bar) if space permits.  
- **End with a single, explicit decision request**.  

> *Example:*  
> **12 % YoY revenue growth** – **Why?** It confirms the new pricing tier is resonating, adding $15 M to EBITDA and moving us 2 pts closer to the 2027 target. **Decision:** Approve $2 M incremental sales enablement budget to sustain momentum.
