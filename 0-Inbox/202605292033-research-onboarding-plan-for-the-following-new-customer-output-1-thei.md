# Research: onboarding plan for the following new customer. Output: 1) Their goal in their words. 2) Week 1: 'first value' moment + concrete steps to get there. 3) Week 2-4: habits to form + checkpoints. 4) Day-30 success criteria — what they'll be doing if onboarding worked. 5) Health signals to watch + intervention plan if they slip.

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Onboarding plan for a new customer

**1️⃣ Customer Goal (in their own words)**  
> *“We want our new hires to become productive, engaged members of the team within the first month, feeling confident using our tools, aligned with our culture, and ready to contribute to key projects without needing constant hand‑holding.”*  

---

## 2️⃣ Week 1 – The **First‑Value** Moment  

| Goal | Concrete Steps | Success Indicator |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Show a quick win** – the new hire should complete a real‑world task that demonstrates impact. | 1. **Pre‑arrival prep** – IT provisions email, VPN, and a ready‑to‑use NeuroWorks workspace; a welcome kit and a dedicated “buddy” are assigned (check‑list from LumApps guide)【1†L84-L92】. <br>2. **Day‑1 kickoff** – brief welcome video, intro to team, and a 30‑min “first‑task briefing” that ties the role to a current project. <br>3. **Guided hands‑on** – buddy walks the new hire through creating their first NeuroWorks “team” and runs a simple automation (e.g., schedule a daily summary). <br>4. **Deliverable** – by end of Day 5 the hire publishes a short status report in NeuroWorks that is reviewed by the manager. | The manager receives the report, acknowledges the completed automation, and the new hire reports “I was able to deliver something useful on day 5.” |
| **Establish connection & culture** | • 15‑min informal coffee with the manager and the wider squad (virtual or on‑site). <br>• Share the company’s values deck on NeuroWorks and ask the hire to pick one value they’ll embody this week. | The hire can name the chosen value and explains a concrete behavior they’ll practice. |

---

## 3️⃣ Weeks 2‑4 – Building Habits & Check‑points  

| Habit | Weekly Action | Check‑point (Owner) |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Daily tool fluency** – use NeuroWorks for task capture, knowledge vault, and AI‑assisted drafting. | Log at least one “knowledge‑capture” entry per day; run one AI‑generated draft (email, brief, or workflow). | Buddy reviews the log every Friday; manager signs off on quality. |
| **Weekly sync** – align on goals & feedback. | 30‑min one‑on‑one with manager + 15‑min peer demo of a NeuroWorks feature used that week. | Manager records progress in the onboarding dashboard (LumApps analytics). |
| **Cross‑team networking** – build relational capital. | Attend one cross‑functional stand‑up or lunch‑&‑learn (virtual). | Buddy confirms attendance and notes any follow‑up actions. |
| **Self‑directed learning** – deepen platform expertise. | Complete one micro‑learning module from NeuroWorks “Learning Hub” (≈20 min). | Learning completion badge appears in the user profile; manager notes competency gained. |

**Key Milestones** (end of week 4)  
- **Independent workflow**: the hire creates, schedules, and monitors a multi‑persona NeuroWorks workflow without buddy assistance.  
- **Cultural fit**: can articulate how their work supports the company’s mission and one core value.  
- **Feedback loop**: provides constructive feedback on the onboarding experience (survey in NeuroWorks).  

---

## 4️⃣ Day‑30 Success Criteria  

| What they’ll be doing | Evidence |
|-----------------------|----------|
| **Running autonomous projects** – launching at least one end‑to‑end NeuroWorks team (e.g., research + drafting + email dispatch). | Completed workflow visible in the platform’s activity log. |
| **Mentoring the next newcomer** – acting as a buddy for a later hire or leading a short onboarding demo. | Buddy‑assignment record in the onboarding tracker. |
| **Metrics ownership** – reviewing their own performance dashboard (tasks completed, AI‑draft quality, knowledge‑vault contributions). | Dashboard shows ≥ 80 % of target KPIs met (see LumApps analytics). |
| **Cultural champion** – regularly shares a “value‑in‑action” story in team meetings. | Meeting minutes or Slack post captured in NeuroWorks. |

---

## 5️⃣ Health Signals & Intervention Plan  

| Signal (early warning) | Why it matters | Intervention (within 2 days) |
|------------------------|----------------|------------------------------|
| **Low daily log activity** (< 1 entry) | Indicates tool friction or lack of habit. | Buddy schedules a 15‑min “tool‑tips” session; provide a quick‑start cheat sheet. |
| **Missed weekly sync** | Risks misalignment & isolation. | Manager sends a calendar invite with agenda; if repeated, assign a peer‑coach. |
| **Negative feedback on culture fit** (survey score < 3/5) | May signal disengagement. | Conduct a 30‑min “values conversation” with senior leader; adjust onboarding content to highlight relevant stories. |
| **Stagnant workflow progress** (no new NeuroWorks team created by week 3) | Shows operational block. | Escalate to IT for access review; run a focused training on workflow builder. |
| **High ticket volume to support** (≥ 3 issues/week) | Indicates usability problems. | Create a “FAQ” page in NeuroWorks; schedule a group Q&A. |

**Monitoring** – Use the LumApps onboarding analytics dashboard to track daily logs, training completions, and satisfaction scores; set automated alerts when any metric falls below the 80 % threshold【1†L124-L132】.
