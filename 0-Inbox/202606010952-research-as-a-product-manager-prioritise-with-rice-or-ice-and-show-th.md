# Research: As a Product Manager, prioritise with rice or ice — and show the score, not just the verdict

**Prioritisation framework (RICE / ICE)**  

| Feature / Idea | Reach | Impact | Confidence | Effort (person‑weeks) | **RICE Score** = (R × I × C) ÷ E | ICE Score = (I × C) ÷ E |
|----------------|-------|--------|------------|-----------------------|--------------------------------|-----------------------|
| *Example A*    | 5 k users / quarter | 4 × baseline | 80 % | 2 pw | **10 000** | **1.6** |
| *Example B*    | 2 k users / quarter | 5 × baseline | 70 % | 1 pw | **7 000** | **3.5** |
| *Example C*    | 10 k users / quarter| 2 × baseline | 90 % | 4 pw | **4 500** | **0.45** |

*How the scores are calculated*  

- **Reach** – number of users (or customers, transactions, etc.) that will be affected in a given time‑frame.  
- **Impact** – multiplier of the expected benefit (1 = baseline, 2 = twice as valuable, etc.).  
- **Confidence** – probability that the estimates are correct, expressed as a percentage.  
- **Effort** – total person‑weeks (or person‑months) required to deliver.  

**RICE score** = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort  
**ICE score** = (Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort  

The higher the score, the higher the priority. In the table above, *Example B* has the highest ICE score, indicating it delivers the greatest value per unit of effort, while *Example A* ranks highest on the full RICE metric because of its larger reach.

### How to apply this in NeuroWorks (RUBIEM) product planning  

1. **Identify candidate work items** – new persona modules, parallel‑team dispatch, scheduled‑task engine, email bridge, etc.  
2. **Estimate each dimension** using data from the vault (usage analytics, stakeholder interviews, sprint velocity).  
3. **Plug the numbers into the formulas** above to obtain a numeric priority.  
4. **Sort by score** and feed the ordered list into the sprint‑planning board.
