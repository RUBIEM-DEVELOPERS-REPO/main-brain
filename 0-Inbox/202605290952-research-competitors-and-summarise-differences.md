# Research: competitors and summarise differences

**Types of competitors and how they differ**

| Competitor type | What it is | Typical focus | Key difference from other types |
|-----------------|------------|---------------|---------------------------------|
| **Direct competitor** | Offers the same or a nearly‑identical product/service to the same target market (e.g., Pepsi vs. Coca‑Cola) | Head‑to‑head pricing, feature parity, brand positioning | Competes for the *exact* same customers; rivalry is visible in price wars and product‑feature battles. |
| **Indirect competitor** | Sells a different product that satisfies the same customer need (e.g., Chipotle vs. Chick‑fil‑A) | Substitutes or alternatives that fulfill a similar desire | Threat comes from *substitution* rather than duplication; the competition is about meeting the same need in a different way. |
| **Local competitor** | Operates in the same geographic area or region | Tailors offerings to local regulations, culture, and consumer preferences | Geographic proximity creates a “local” market share battle; they often have deeper knowledge of local nuances. |
| **Global competitor** | Operates across multiple countries or continents | Leverages scale, extensive resources, and economies of economies of scale | Competes on breadth of reach, brand‑global recognition, and cost advantages that smaller or local firms can’t match. |
| **Strategic/Market‑position competitor** (derived from the “product & service,” “pricing,” and “marketing” dimensions) | Differentiates through innovation, premium pricing, or aggressive marketing | Uses unique value propositions—patented tech, superior distribution, brand prestige—to carve a niche | Their advantage is *how* they compete (innovation, brand perception, cost leadership) rather than just *what* they sell. |

### Summary of differences  

1. **Product similarity** – Direct competitors duplicate the core offering; indirect competitors provide alternatives that meet the same consumer need.  
2. **Geography** – Local competitors focus on a specific region and can react quickly to local trends, while global competitors rely on scale and cross‑border resources.  
3. **Competitive strategy** – Companies may compete by lowering price (cost leadership), adding features/innovation (differentiation), or building a premium brand image. The chosen strategy shapes the nature of the rivalry regardless of product or location.  

Understanding which of these categories a rival falls into helps a firm decide whether to fight on price, innovate, localize, or leverage global efficiencies. (Sources: Finance Strategists definition of direct/indirect, local and global competitors; Unkover’s four‑type framework)
