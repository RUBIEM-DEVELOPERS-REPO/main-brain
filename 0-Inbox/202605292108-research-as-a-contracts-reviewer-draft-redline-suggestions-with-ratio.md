**Draft Redline Suggestions (with Rationale)**  

Below is a set of typical contract‑review redline comments you can paste into a Word/Google Doc “Track Changes” view.  Each suggestion is followed by a brief rationale so the author understands *why* the change is being proposed.

| Clause (as‑written) | Suggested Redline | Rationale |
|---------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1. Scope of Services**  <br>“The Provider shall list the hotel on Google Hotel Center and may include a free‑booking link.” | **Delete “free‑booking link”** and replace with **“standard booking link that complies with Google Hotel Center policies”**. | Google’s Hotel Center explicitly distinguishes *free‑booking links* (which bypass the platform’s commission) from standard links and provides specific rules for them【1】.  Using a “free‑booking link” could violate Google’s terms and expose the client to termination or penalties. |
| **2. Commission**  <br>“Provider will receive a 0 % commission on any reservation made through a free‑booking link.” | **Add a cap:** “Provider will receive a commission of **X %** on all reservations, **subject to a minimum of Y %** for any free‑booking link that complies with Google’s policy.” | Even if a free‑booking link is allowed, Google expects a commission on bookings that originate from its platform.  Clarifying the rate prevents the provider from inadvertently forfeiting revenue【1】. |
| **3. Compliance with Platform Policies**  <br>“Provider shall comply with all applicable laws.” | **Insert:** “and shall comply with Google Hotel Center’s Terms of Service, including its rules on free‑booking links and attribution requirements【1】.” | Directly referencing the platform’s policy makes the compliance obligation explicit and gives the client a concrete standard to audit against. |
| **4. Termination**  <br>“Either party may terminate with 30‑day notice.” | **Add:** “If either party breaches Google Hotel Center policies (e.g., misuse of free‑booking links), the non‑breaching party may terminate immediately without notice.” | Mis‑use of free‑booking links can lead to immediate suspension by Google, so the contract should allow swift termination to limit exposure. |
| **5. Indemnification**  <br>“Provider indemnifies Client against all claims arising from the Services.” | **Clarify:** “including any claims, fines or penalties imposed by Google for non‑compliance with its booking‑link rules【1】.” | Tying indemnity to platform‑specific risks ensures the provider bears responsibility for any breach of Google’s free‑booking link policy. |
| **6. Reporting & Auditing**  <br>“Provider shall provide monthly performance reports.” | **Add:** “including a reconciliation of all bookings generated via free‑booking links versus standard links, and evidence of compliance with Google’s guidelines【1】.” | Enables the client to verify that the provider is not inadvertently violating Google’s terms, which could affect revenue and platform standing. |
| **7. Force Majeure**  <br>“Neither party shall be liable for delays caused by events beyond its control.” | **Insert:** “including suspension of Google Hotel Center services or changes to Google’s free‑booking link policy.” | Recognizes that platform policy changes are an external factor that could impact the ability to fulfill contractual obligations. |

### How to Apply the Redlines
1. **Enable Track Changes** in your editor.  
2. **Copy each “Suggested Redline”** into the appropriate clause, using the *strike‑through* for deletions and *underline* for insertions.  
3. **Add the rationale** as a comment attached to the change (most editors let you insert a comment bubble).  

### Quick Reference Checklist
- ☐ Verify that any “free‑booking link” complies with Google’s current policy【1】.  
- ☐ Ensure commission language reflects the fact that Google may still collect a fee on free‑booking links.  
- ☐ Include explicit references to Google’s Terms of Service in the compliance clause.  
- ☐ Add termination and indemnity provisions that cover platform‑policy breaches.  
- ☐ Request regular reporting that distinguishes free‑booking from standard bookings.  

> **Note:** The only source available for platform‑specific guidance is Google’s Hotel Center Help page on free‑booking links【1】.  If additional details (e.g., exact commission percentages) are needed, request clarification from the provider or consult the latest Google policy documentation.
