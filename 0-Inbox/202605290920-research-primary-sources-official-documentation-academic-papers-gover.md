# Research: primary sources official documentation academic papers government regulatory filings press releases raw datasets

**Primary sources** are original, first‑hand materials that provide direct evidence about a topic, without interpretation or analysis by later writers. In research and documentation they include items such as official documentation, academic papers reporting original experiments, government regulatory filings, press releases, and raw datasets. The Cambridge Dictionary defines *primary* as “main; most important” and gives examples that illustrate its use for things that are the original or chief source of information, e.g., “The Red Cross's primary concern is to preserve and protect human life” and “The money I earn is extra, my husband's job is our primary source of income”【1】. By extension, a *primary source* is the main, original source of data or evidence on which further analysis or secondary reporting is built.
