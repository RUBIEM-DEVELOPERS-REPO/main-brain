However, the web sources outline general, evidence-based strategies for improving AI-workforce and technology platforms to better support workers:

*   **Reduce Cognitive and Work Burden:** AI platforms should focus on reducing the cognitive and workload burden of users rather than solely focusing on cost savings or quality. This is achieved by designing and deploying AI with direct end-user input, implementing mechanisms to identify and reduce bias, and actively measuring and monitoring the platform's impact on user workload [1].
*   **Mitigate Workplace Stress:** Rapid AI adoption has introduced new workplace stressors, with many workers feeling pressured to use AI or fearing they will fall behind [2]. Platforms should be designed to ease this transition, potentially incorporating social and psychological supports to help users navigate technological shifts [2].
*   **Support Upskilling and Retraining:** AI platforms should facilitate both "reskilling" (gaining skills for new roles) and "upskilling" (enhancing skills for current roles) [2]. Training and platform workflows should be tailored to "frontier" jobs (entirely new roles created by AI) and "retooled" jobs (existing occupations where the required skills are changing due to AI integration) [2].
