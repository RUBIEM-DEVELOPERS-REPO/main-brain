# Research: As a Executive Assistant, write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer

**Pre‑Meeting Brief Template – Executive Assistant**

| Section | What to Include | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **1. Meeting Title & Date** | • Clear title (e.g., “Q2 Marketing Budget Review”)  <br>• Date, time, location (or video link) | Sets the context instantly for all participants. |
| **2. Attendees (Who)** | • Names, titles, and brief role in the meeting (e.g., *Jane Doe – VP of Marketing – decision‑maker on spend*).  <br>• Any external guests and their affiliation. | Helps the executive know who will be speaking, who holds authority, and who may need follow‑up. |
| **3. Objective (What they want)** | • One‑sentence purpose of the meeting (e.g., “Obtain approval for the Q2 digital‑ad budget”).  <br>• Key outcomes the requester hopes to achieve (e.g., “Commit $500k for paid‑social, align on KPI targets”). | Aligns everyone on the desired end‑state and prevents scope drift. |
| **4. Background & Context** | • Summary of relevant data, prior decisions, and any pending items.  <br>• Attach or link to supporting documents (slides, reports). | Gives the executive a quick refresher without having to dig through files. |
| **5. The Ask (Specific request to the executive)** | • Precise decision or action needed (e.g., “Approve budget allocation” or “Provide feedback on proposed media mix”).  <br>• Preferred format of the response (sign‑off, verbal confirmation, written comment). | Makes it easy for the executive to say *yes* or *no* and know exactly what is expected. |
| **6. Recommended Answer / Suggested Decision** | • Your analysis of options, risks, and benefits.  <br>• A concise recommendation (e.g., “Approve the $500k budget with a 10 % contingency”). | Demonstrates that you’ve done the legwork and gives the executive a ready‑made answer. |
| **7. Next Steps & Follow‑Up** | • Immediate actions after the meeting (e.g., “Send revised budget to finance by 5 pm”).  <br>• Who is responsible for each step. | Ensures accountability and a clear path forward. |
| **8. Logistics & Prep** | • Any required pre‑reads (list with links).  <br>• Technical setup (dial‑in, screen‑share). | Guarantees a smooth, on‑time start. |

---

### How to Use the Template

1. **Gather information** from the meeting organizer, relevant documents, and the executive’s calendar.  
2. **Fill each section** concisely (bullet points, no more than 2‑3 lines per item).  
3. **Attach supporting files** at the end of the brief or embed links to the vault (`_knowledge/...`).  
4. **Send** the brief at least 24 hours before the meeting, with a short email note:  
   > “Hi [Executive], please see the attached pre‑meeting brief for tomorrow’s [title]. Let me know if you need anything else.”  

---

#### Quick Example

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Meeting** | Q2 Marketing Budget Review – 30 May 2026, 10:00 am, Zoom |
| **Attendees** | Jane Doe – VP Marketing (decision‑maker) <br>John Smith – Finance Director (budget sign‑off) |
| **Objective** | Secure approval for $500k Q2 digital‑ad spend. |
| **Background** | FY‑23 spend $4.2M; Q1 ROI 12 %; proposal attached (slide 3). |
| **The Ask** | Approve $500k budget, with 10 % contingency. |
| **Recommendation** | Approve – aligns with FY‑24 growth target; risk mitigated by contingency. |
| **Next Steps** | Finance to update forecast by 5 pm 31 May; marketing to launch campaign 15 Jun. |
| **Prep** | Review slide deck (link) and FY‑24 targets (PDF). |

Use this structure for every upcoming meeting and you’ll give your executive the clarity, context, and confidence to make fast, informed decisions.
