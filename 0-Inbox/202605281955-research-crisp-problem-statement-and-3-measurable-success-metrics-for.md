# Research: crisp problem statement and 3 measurable success metrics for: an in-app daily digest that summarizes a user's unread notifications

**Alignment check — required before responding.** The user's request names concrete elements (counts, people, dates, scale numbers, named sections, named steps, deliverable shape). The final answer MUST address each one. If N items are asked for, produce N. If

**Problem statement**  
Create an in‑app daily digest that automatically compiles a concise summary of each user’s unread notifications and delivers it once per day. The digest should surface the most relevant items, reduce notification fatigue, and increase the likelihood that users act on pending messages.

**Success metrics** (aligned with the KPI guidance for new‑feature measurement)  

| Metric | How to calculate | Target (example) | Why it matters |
|--------|------------------|------------------|----------------|
| **Daily Active Digest Users (DADU)** – number of unique users who open the daily digest each day | Count of users who view the digest / total users with at least one unread notification | ≥ 30 % of the eligible user base within 4 weeks of launch | Directly measures adoption and whether the digest is being noticed (a core “usage KPI” from [1]) |
| **Digest‑Driven Action Rate** – proportion of unread notifications that are acted on after the digest is sent | (Actions taken on notifications that were unread at the time of the digest) ÷ (total unread notifications at digest time) | ≥ 15 % increase vs. baseline (pre‑digest) within 6 weeks | Shows the digest’s effectiveness at converting visibility into user action, the ultimate product goal |
| **Average Session Duration for Digest View** – time users spend on the digest screen | Total time spent on the digest page ÷ number of digest views | ≥ 45 seconds per view (or a 20 % lift over baseline session length) | Longer sessions indicate that users find the summary useful and are engaging with the content, matching the “session duration” KPI recommended in [1] |

These three metrics are **measurable, specific, relevant, and actionable**—the exact criteria highlighted for feature‑success KPIs in the AB Tasty guide [1]. Tracking them from launch will let the product team quickly determine whether the daily digest meets its objectives or needs iteration.
