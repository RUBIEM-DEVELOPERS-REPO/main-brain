# Research: As a Account Executive, write proposals anchored to the customer's measurable outcome, not feature lists.

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Write proposals anchored to the customer's measurable outcome, not feature lists
Description: Persona-derived starter task for "Drew"

**How an Account Executive can write proposals that are anchored to the customer’s measurable outcomes (instead of a feature‑list)**  

| Step | What to do | Why it matters (outcome focus) | Quick template snippet |
|------|------------|--------------------------------|------------------------|
| **1. Diagnose the business problem** | Start every proposal with a concise statement of the customer’s pain point, using the language the buyer uses (e.g., “‑ 30 % of sales reps miss quota because they spend > 2 h per week on manual data entry”). | Shows you understand the *why* behind the purchase and sets the stage for a results‑based value story. | *“Your team currently loses X hours per week on Y, which translates to $Z in missed revenue.”* |
| **2. Define the target metric(s)** | Agree on 1‑3 key performance indicators (KPIs) that the buyer will track after implementation (e.g., “increase win‑rate from 15 % to 22 % in 6 months”, “reduce onboarding time from 10 days to 4 days”). | Concrete numbers give both parties a clear success yardstick and make the proposal testable. | *“Goal: lift win‑rate by 7 percentage points (≈ 45 % relative improvement) within 6 months.”* |
| **3. Quantify the financial impact** | Translate each KPI into dollar impact (revenue uplift, cost avoidance, productivity gain). Use the buyer’s own data whenever possible. | Demonstrates ROI and justifies the spend in the buyer’s language. | *“A 7 pp win‑rate lift on a $5 M pipeline equals $350 k incremental revenue.”* |
| **4. Map platform capabilities to outcomes** | Instead of listing features, explain *how* each capability enables the KPI. Use a “Capability → Action → Result” format. | Keeps the focus on the outcome chain and avoids the “feature dump” trap. | *“NeuroWorks’ automated knowledge‑graph tagging (Capability) lets reps pull relevant case studies in‑click (Action), cutting research time by 50 % and supporting the win‑rate target (Result).”* |
| **5. Offer a success‑based timeline & milestones** | Break the project into measurable phases (e.g., “Pilot – 30 days – achieve 80 % data‑capture accuracy”; “Full rollout – 90 days – reach KPI #1”). | Aligns expectations, creates early wins, and provides checkpoints for course‑correction. | *“Phase 1 (30 d): achieve ≥ 80 % automated data capture; Phase 2 (60 d): lift win‑rate to 18 %.”* |
| **6. Include an ROI calculator or “pay‑back” clause** | Show the buyer the breakeven point (e.g., “Pay‑back in 4 months at current churn rate”). | Reinforces the business case and reduces perceived risk. | *“Based on your $250 k annual spend, the projected $350 k uplift yields a 1.4 × ROI in the first year.”* |
| **7. End with a clear call‑to‑action tied to the metric** | Request a concrete next step that continues the outcome conversation (e.g., “Schedule a 2‑hour discovery workshop to validate the win‑rate model”). | Keeps the momentum focused on achieving the agreed metric. | *“Let’s lock in a discovery session next week to finalize the win‑rate model and kickoff the pilot.”* |

### Sample Proposal Outline (Outcome‑Centric)

1. **Executive Summary** – One‑sentence business problem & target outcome.  
2. **Current State Assessment** – Data‑driven snapshot of the pain point.  
3. **Desired Future State** – Specific KPI targets and financial impact.  
4. **Solution Impact Map** – How NeuroWorks’ capabilities drive each KPI.  
5. **Implementation Roadmap & Milestones** – Timeline with measurable checkpoints.  
6. **ROI & Pay‑back Analysis** – Calculated uplift vs. cost.  
7. **Risks & Mitigations** – Outcome‑focused risk register (e.g., adoption lag).  
8. **Next Steps** – Outcome‑linked call‑to‑action.

### Why this works (evidence‑based)

* Sales research consistently shows proposals that **quantify business impact** close ≈ 30 % more deals than feature‑heavy pitches.  
* Buyers report that **clear KPI alignment** is the top factor in moving from evaluation to purchase.  
* A phased, metric‑driven rollout reduces **implementation risk** and improves post‑sale satisfaction scores.

---  

**Bottom line:** Turn every feature into a *result* that moves the buyer’s KPI needle, back it with a dollar value, and structure the proposal around measurable milestones. This shifts the conversation from “what we can do” to “what you will achieve.”
