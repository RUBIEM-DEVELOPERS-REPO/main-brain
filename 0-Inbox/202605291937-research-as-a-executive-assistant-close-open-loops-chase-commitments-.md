# Research: As a Executive Assistant, close open loops — chase commitments made on the executive's behalf

**Closing Open Loops & Chasing Commitments – A Quick‑Start Guide for an Executive Assistant**

| What you need to do | Why it matters (for the executive) | How to do it (practical steps) |
|---------------------|------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **1. Capture every commitment** | Guarantees nothing falls through the cracks; the executive can rely on you to “own” follow‑up. | • After meetings, calls, or emails, immediately log the action in a single “Commitments” list (e.g., a Notion table or the NeuroWorks task board). <br>• Include *who* made the commitment, *what* was promised, *when* it’s due, and any supporting documents. |
| **2. Prioritise by impact & deadline** | Not all loops are equal; the executive’s time is limited. | • Tag each item with a priority level (High/Medium/Low) and a due‑date. <br>• Use the executive’s calendar to surface high‑impact items that align with upcoming strategic milestones. |
| **3. Assign ownership & set reminders** | Clear responsibility prevents “someone else will do it” paralysis. | • If the commitment is the executive’s, schedule a reminder in their calendar (e.g., “Review contract — due May 31”). <br>• If it belongs to a stakeholder, send a concise “action‑required” email that restates the ask, deadline, and any needed resources. |
| **4. Track status in real‑time** | Gives the executive a single‑pane‑of‑glass view of open loops. | • Update the task board each time you receive a status change (e.g., “In progress”, “Completed”, “Blocked”). <br>• Use NeuroWorks’ “status” field so the executive can filter for “Open” vs. “Closed”. |
| **5. Proactively follow‑up** | Keeps momentum and shows you’re guarding the executive’s agenda. | • For items approaching their deadline, send a polite nudge (e.g., “Just checking on the draft you mentioned – do you need anything from me?”). <br>• Log each follow‑up attempt so you can escalate if needed. |
| **6. Close the loop & archive** | Signals completion and frees mental bandwidth. | • Once the commitment is fulfilled, mark it “Closed”, add a brief note of the outcome, and move the record to an archive folder for future reference. <br>• Send a short “Done” notification to the executive and any stakeholders. |
| **7. Review weekly** | Prevents accumulation of stale items. | • At the end of each week, run a “Open Loops” report. <br>• Highlight any overdue items and propose next steps or escalation paths. |

### Key Principles (derived from the executive role definition)

*An “executive” is a senior decision‑maker who expects actions to be turned into results* — Cambridge Dictionary defines an executive as “someone in a high position … who makes decisions and puts them into action”【1】. Your role is to translate the executive’s strategic decisions into concrete, tracked actions and ensure they are completed.

### Quick‑Reference Checklist (copy‑paste into your task manager)

```
- [ ] Log new commitment (who, what, when, docs)
- [ ] Tag priority & deadline
- [ ] Assign owner & set reminder
- [ ] Update status after each change
- [ ] Send follow‑up 2 days before due
- [ ] Mark as Closed & archive
- [ ] Weekly Open‑Loops review
```
