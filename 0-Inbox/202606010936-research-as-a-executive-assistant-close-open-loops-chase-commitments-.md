# Research: As a Executive Assistant, close open loops — chase commitments made on the executive's behalf

**How an Executive Assistant (EA) can close open loops and chase commitments on the executive’s behalf**

1. **Identify every pending commitment**  
   * Pull together all sources of “open loops” – email threads, meeting notes, calendar invites, task‑tracking tools, and the EA’s own inbox.  
   * Tag each item with a clear description, the responsible party, and the original due date.

2. **Prioritise by impact and urgency**  
   * Use a simple matrix (high‑impact / high‑urgency, etc.) to decide which loops need immediate follow‑up and which can wait.  
   * Align the priority list with the executive’s strategic objectives so the EA is chasing the items that matter most to the leader.

3. **Create a living “Commitment Tracker”**  
   * A shared spreadsheet, Kanban board, or NeuroWorks task list (if you use the platform) works well.  
   * Columns typically include: Commitment, Owner, Original Due Date, Follow‑up Date, Status, and Notes.

4. **Craft concise follow‑up messages**  
   * Reference the original request, restate the expected outcome, and ask for a concrete update or new deadline.  
   * Keep tone courteous and action‑oriented; e.g., “Hi [Name], just checking on the status of [commitment] discussed on [date]. Could you let me know if we’re on track for [date] or need to adjust?”

5. **Schedule reminder checkpoints**  
   * Set calendar reminders for the EA to revisit each commitment before its due date.  
   * If a response is still missing, send a polite second reminder and, if needed, escalate to the executive with a brief summary.

6. **Document outcomes promptly**  
   * When a commitment is fulfilled, mark it as “Closed” in the tracker and note any relevant deliverables.  
   * If a commitment is delayed, record the new timeline and any reasons provided.

7. **Report a concise status to the executive**  
   * Provide a weekly (or as‑needed) snapshot: total open loops, high‑priority items, any blockers, and actions taken.  
   * This keeps the executive informed without requiring them to chase each item themselves.

8. **Build a habit loop for future commitments**  
   * After each meeting, immediately log new action items in the tracker.  
   * Encourage meeting participants to confirm responsibilities and deadlines on the spot, reducing the chance of new “open loops” forming.

---

### Why this matters for an **executive**  

An executive is “someone in a high position … who makes decisions and puts them into action” [1]. Because their time is at a premium, the EA’s systematic approach to closing loops frees the executive to focus on strategic decisions rather than chasing downstream tasks. By acting as the “central hub” for commitments, the EA ensures that promises made on the executive’s behalf are fulfilled, maintaining credibility and operational momentum.
