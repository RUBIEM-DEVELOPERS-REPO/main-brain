# Research: As a Executive Assistant, triage inbox into act-now / read-later / ignore — with explicit reasoning

**Executive‑Assistant Inbox Triage Framework**  
*(Act‑Now | Read‑Later | Ignore – with explicit reasoning)*  

| Category | When to use it | Reasoning (why it belongs in this bucket) | Typical actions |
|----------|----------------|--------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Act‑Now** | • The email contains a **time‑sensitive request** (e.g., meeting scheduling, deadline‑driven deliverable, travel approval). <br>• It requires a **decision that only the executive can make** (budget sign‑off, strategic direction). <br>• It involves **critical operational impact** (legal notice, security alert, client‑facing issue). | • Delaying could cause missed deadlines, reputational risk, or financial loss. <br>• The executive’s authority is needed to move the matter forward. | • Flag/highlight in the inbox. <br>• Forward to the executive with a concise “Action required” note. <br>• Add to today’s task list or calendar. |
| **Read‑Later** | • The email is **informational** (industry news, internal updates, non‑urgent reports). <br>• It contains **background material** for a future project or decision. <br>• It is a **low‑priority request** that can be handled after immediate matters (e.g., routine status check, non‑urgent meeting invite). | • No immediate impact on current priorities, but the content may be valuable for strategic planning or future reference. <br>• Allows the executive to focus on higher‑urgency items first. | • Move to a “Read‑Later” folder or label. <br>• Schedule a time block (e.g., “Review updates – Friday 10 am”). <br>• Summarize key points in a weekly digest if needed. |
| **Ignore** | • The email is **spam, bulk marketing, or clearly irrelevant** to the executive’s role. <br>• It is a **duplicate** of an already‑handled request. <br>• It is a **personal message** addressed to the executive’s personal account (unless explicitly requested). | • Processing it wastes time and distracts from mission‑critical work. <br>• Ignoring prevents inbox clutter and keeps focus on actionable items. | • Delete or archive. <br>• If a sender repeatedly sends irrelevant mail, consider adding to a block list. |

### How to Apply the Framework in Practice
1. **Quick Scan (≤ 30 seconds)** – Look for:  
   * **Deadlines or dates** in the subject line or first paragraph.  
   * **Keywords**: “urgent”, “ASAP”, “approval needed”, “meeting request”.  
   * **Sender**: internal senior staff, external high‑value client, or known spam source.  

2. **Assign a label** (Act‑Now / Read‑Later / Ignore) using the email client’s tagging system.  

3. **Add a one‑sentence rationale** in the email’s notes field or in the forward to the executive (e.g., “Act‑Now – client needs contract signed by 2 pm tomorrow”). This makes the triage decision transparent and audit‑able.  

4. **Review the “Read‑Later” folder** at a scheduled time (e.g., start of each day) to prevent items from slipping through.  

5. **Periodically clean the “Ignore” folder** to ensure no false positives remain.
