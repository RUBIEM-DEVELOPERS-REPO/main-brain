# Research: As a Head of AI, oversee the development, testing, deployment, and monitoring of ai models and ai-enabled tools

**What a Head of AI typically oversees**

| Area | Core responsibilities | Why it matters |
|------|-----------------------|----------------|
| **Strategy & Road‑mapping** | • Define the AI vision aligned with business goals.<br>• Prioritize projects, create a multi‑year roadmap, and secure budget. | Sets direction and ensures AI work delivers measurable value. |
| **Model Development** | • Lead data‑science and engineering teams in designing, prototyping, and iterating models.<br>• Enforce reproducibility (code, data versioning, experiment tracking). | Guarantees that models are built on solid, auditable foundations. |
| **Testing & Validation** | • Establish rigorous validation pipelines (unit tests, integration tests, bias/fairness checks, performance benchmarks).<br>• Conduct peer‑reviews and external audits where needed. | Prevents hidden defects, regulatory issues, and unintended harms. |
| **Deployment & Ops (MLOps)** | • Choose and configure CI/CD for model serving (containers, orchestration, monitoring).<br>• Manage model registries, roll‑outs, canary releases, and rollback procedures. | Enables reliable, scalable delivery of AI‑enabled tools to production. |
| **Monitoring & Governance** | • Implement real‑time metrics (latency, drift, accuracy, usage).<br>• Set alerts, automated retraining triggers, and compliance reporting (privacy, security, ethical guidelines). | Detects degradation early and ensures ongoing compliance. |
| **Team & Culture** | • Hire, mentor, and retain talent across data science, engineering, and product.<br>• Foster a culture of experimentation, documentation, and continuous learning. | Drives long‑term capability and innovation. |
| **Stakeholder Alignment** | • Communicate progress, risks, and ROI to executives, product owners, and external partners.<br>• Translate technical outcomes into business impact. | Keeps AI initiatives visible and funded. |

**Key processes to institutionalize**

1. **Model lifecycle governance** – a documented workflow that moves a model from idea → data collection → training → validation → deployment → monitoring → retirement.  
2. **MLOps platform** – standardized tooling (e.g., version‑controlled pipelines, artifact stores, feature stores) that the whole organization uses.  
3. **Risk & ethics review** – a checklist for data privacy, bias, explainability, and regulatory compliance before any production release.  
4. **Feedback loops** – mechanisms for users and downstream systems to report issues, which feed back into retraining or model improvement cycles.
