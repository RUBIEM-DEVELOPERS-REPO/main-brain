# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak

**Tone:** Neutral (the request is procedural, not emotionally charged).  

**Underlying need:** The customer wants a ready‑to‑send response that (1) acknowledges the client’s message, (2) solves the real problem behind the literal ask, and (3) includes a concrete commitment date – all without corporate‑speak or macro language.

**Draft reply**

> Hi [Customer Name],
> 
> Thanks for reaching out. I understand you’re looking for [brief restatement of the real need – e.g., “the latest status on the data‑export you requested”].  
> 
> Here’s what we’ll do: [concise action plan – e.g., “I’ll generate the export and send it to you as a secure link.”] I’ll have this ready for you by **Wednesday, June 5 2026**.
> 
> If anything changes or you need anything else in the meantime, just let me know.  
> 
> Best,  
> [Your Name]  
> Customer Success, NeuroWorks  

**Churn‑risk / expansion signal**  
- No negative language was detected in the request, so no churn risk flag is needed.  
- If the underlying need involves a new feature request or additional capacity, that could be an upsell opportunity – note it for the account team.
