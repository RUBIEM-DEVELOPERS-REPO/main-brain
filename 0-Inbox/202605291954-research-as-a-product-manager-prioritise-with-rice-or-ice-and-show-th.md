# Research: As a Product Manager, prioritise with rice or ice — and show the score, not just the verdict

**Prioritisation using the RICE framework (Reach · Impact · Confidence · Effort)**  

| Feature / Idea | Reach (users / period) | Impact (value / scale 1‑3) | Confidence (%) | Effort (person‑weeks) | **RICE Score** = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort |
|----------------|------------------------|----------------------------|----------------|----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Example A**  | 5 000                  | 2                          | 80 %           | 4 weeks              | (5 000 × 2 × 0.80) ÷ 4 = 2 000 |
| **Example B**  | 1 200                  | 3                          | 60 %           | 2 weeks              | (1 200 × 3 × 0.60) ÷ 2 = 1 080 |
| **Example C**  | 8 000                  | 1                          | 90 %           | 8 weeks              | (8 000 × 1 × 0.90) ÷ 8 = 900   |

*The table shows how each component contributes to a numeric RICE score, allowing you to rank ideas objectively rather than relying on a simple “yes/no” verdict.*

### How the score is calculated
1. **Reach** – estimated number of users (or transactions) the feature will affect in a given time‑frame.  
2. **Impact** – the degree of benefit per user, usually on a 1‑3 (or 1‑5) scale.  
3. **Confidence** – how certain you are about the Reach and Impact estimates, expressed as a percentage.  
4. **Effort** – total person‑weeks (or person‑months) required to design, build, test and launch.  

The formula **RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort** yields a single number; higher scores indicate higher priority.

### Why use a score instead of a verdict
- **Objectivity:** Numbers reduce bias that can creep into “gut‑feel” decisions.  
- **Comparability:** Different initiatives can be placed on the same scale, even if they differ in scope or type.  
- **Transparency:** Stakeholders can see the assumptions behind each ranking and discuss them openly.  

### Practical tip for a Product Manager
1. **Gather data** for Reach (analytics, market research) and Effort (engineering estimates).  
2. **Agree on Impact** levels with the team (e.g., 1 = minor, 2 = moderate, 3 = significant).  
3. **Assign Confidence** based on data quality (high confidence ≈ 80‑90 %).  
4. **Calculate** the RICE score in a spreadsheet; sort descending to see the priority order.  

> **Note:** The evidence provided defines “product” in a general sense (e.g., a good or service created for customers) [1][2][3], but does not contain a specific RICE methodology. Consequently, the calculation method above follows the standard RICE definition used in product management literature, but the exact numeric values must be supplied by your own data.
