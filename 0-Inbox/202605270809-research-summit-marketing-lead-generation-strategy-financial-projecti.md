## 2. Financial‑Projection Template (Excel‑style)

| **Metric** | **Assumption (editable)** | **Formula** | **Result** |
|------------|---------------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Total Marketing Spend** | $ _____ (e.g., $30,000) | – | – |
| **Cost per Lead (CPL)** | $ _____ (e.g., $30) | =Total Spend / Leads | – |
| **Leads Generated** | =Total Spend / CPL | – | – |
| **Lead‑to‑MQL Conversion** | % _____ (e.g., 20 %) | =Leads × %MQL | – |
| **MQL‑to‑SQL Conversion** | % _____ (e.g., 25 %) | =MQL × %SQL | – |
| **SQL‑to‑Closed‑Won Conversion** | % _____ (e.g., 15 %) | =SQL × %Win | – |
| **Average Deal Size** | $ _____ (e.g., $12,000) | – | – |
| **Revenue Forecast** | =Closed‑Won × Deal Size | – | – |
| **ROI** | =(Revenue – Spend) / Spend | – | – |

**How to use it at the summit**

1. Print the table on a hand‑out or share a Google Sheet link.  
2. Walk through a quick example:  
   *Spend $30 k → CPL $30 → 1,000 leads → 20 % MQL (200) → 25 % SQL (50) → 15 % win (7.5 deals) → $12 k avg deal → **$90 k revenue** → **200 % ROI**.*  
3. Invite participants to plug their own numbers in the editable cells.
