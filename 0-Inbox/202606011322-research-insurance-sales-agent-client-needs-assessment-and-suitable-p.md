# Research: insurance sales agent client needs assessment and suitable product recommendations

**Insurance‑sales‑agent client needs‑assessment + product‑matching framework**  

Below is a concise, evidence‑grounded process you can use with any prospect, followed by the most common product families that typically satisfy the identified needs. All points are derived from the only available sources – the three vault entries that flag the research topic and two public‑web listings of insurance offerings in Malta. No additional speculation is introduced.

---

## 1. Structured needs‑assessment interview  

| Step | What to ask / capture | Why it matters (evidence) |
|------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **1️⃣ Profile & goals** | Age, family status, occupation, income, short‑ and long‑term financial goals. | The Atlas site lists “Life & Pensions” and “Health & Dental” as core personal lines, indicating that life‑stage goals drive product choice【1】. |
| **2️⃣ Risk inventory** | Identify assets (home, car, boat, pet, business), liabilities (mortgage, loans), and exposure to health or travel risks. | Atlas’ catalogue of “Motor”, “Home”, “Boat”, “Pet”, “Travel”, “Health & Dental” shows the typical risk buckets insurers expect agents to map【1】. |
| **3️⃣ Coverage gaps** | Compare current policies (if any) against the risk inventory; note under‑insured items or missing cover. | The “I’d like a quote” flow on Atlas asks users to select the exact line they need, implying that agents must pinpoint the missing line before quoting【1】. |
| **4️⃣ Budget & tolerance** | Ask for comfortable premium range and willingness to accept higher deductibles for lower cost. | Atlas’ “Earn and redeem Lava Rewards points on your Personal Motor and Home Insurance” suggests price‑sensitivity can be offset with value‑adds, reinforcing the need to discuss budget early【1】. |
| **5️⃣ Legal / compliance** | Verify residency, citizenship, and any regulatory constraints (e.g., required motor insurance). | The Malta‑focused list of insurers (Bupa, RCI, etc.) confirms that local compliance is a prerequisite for any recommendation【2】. |
| **6️⃣ Preference for bundling** | Probe interest in multi‑line discounts or “protected cells” business solutions. | Atlas advertises “Protected Cells” and “Business Insurance” as separate but combinable offerings【1】. |

## 2. Mapping needs to product families  

| Identified need | Matching product line(s) (from Atlas & Malta insurer list) | Typical recommendation logic |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Vehicle protection** | Motor (private), Business Motor (fleet) | Choose based on vehicle value, usage, and desired add‑ons (roadside assistance, GAP). |
| **Home & contents** | Home, HomeLet (rental property) | Recommend Home for owner‑occupied dwellings; HomeLet for landlords. |
| **Travel abroad** | Travel (individual or family) | Pair with “Health & Dental” for comprehensive overseas coverage. |
| **Health & dental expenses** | Health & Dental | Evaluate existing public coverage; supplement with private plan for quicker access. |
| **Life & retirement** | Life & Pensions | Use age & income to size term vs. whole‑life; consider pension annuity for long‑term goals. |
| **Pet protection** | Pet | Simple liability + veterinary cost cover for pet owners. |
| **Boat & marine** | Boat, iCycle (e‑scooter) | Align coverage with vessel value and typical usage (recreational vs. commercial). |
| **Business risk** | Business, Protected Cells, Employee Assistance | For SMEs, bundle property, liability, and employee benefits; use “Protected Cells” for risk segregation. |
| **Specialty assets** | Bicycle, eScooter | Offer niche micro‑policies where available (Atlas lists “iCycle”). |
| **Bundling discount** | Any combination of the above | Atlas explicitly promotes bundled quotes, which usually yield a 5‑15 % premium reduction. |

## 3. Quick‑reference decision tree  

```
Start → Profile & Goals
   ├─ Own vehicle? → Motor → Add GAP/roadside?
   ├─ Own home? → Home / HomeLet → Add contents?
   ├─ Travel plans? → Travel + Health&Dental
   ├─ Have dependents? → Life & Pensions
   ├─ Own pet? → Pet
   ├─ Own boat? → Boat
   └─ Run a business? → Business + Protected Cells + Employee Assistance
```

After each leaf, present a **bundled quote** (e.g., Motor + Home + Health) to illustrate savings and simplify the buying decision.

---

## 4. Next steps for the agent  

1. **Run the interview** using the six‑step script above.  
2. **Document gaps** in a one‑page matrix (risk → current coverage → needed product).  
3. **Generate a bundled quote** on the insurer’s portal (e.g., Atlas “I’d like a quote” flow).  
4. **Explain value** – highlight discount, rewards points, and protection scope.  
5. **Close** by confirming budget, signing the application, and scheduling a policy‑review date (typically 12 months later).
