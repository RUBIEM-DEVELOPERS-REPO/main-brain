# Research: best practices for insurance sales agents maintaining client records and following up on policy renewals

**Best‑practice checklist for insurance agents – client records &amp; renewal follow‑up**

| Area | What to do | Why it works | How to implement |
|------|------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Centralised, up‑to‑date records** | Log every interaction (call, email, text, meeting) in your agency‑management system (AMS) immediately after it occurs. | Creates a “shared brain” so any team member can pick up the conversation and keep the experience personal. [1] | Use the AMS notes field; set a 5‑minute rule to record details before moving on. |
| **Standardised data fields** | Capture core client data (contact preferences, policy numbers, key dates, personal milestones) in consistent fields. | Guarantees that automation and reporting pull the right information every time. | Build custom fields (e.g., “Preferred channel”, “Birthday”, “Last policy review”) and make them required on new client entry. |
| **Automate routine touch‑points** | Schedule renewal reminders, billing notices, and policy‑document deliveries to fire automatically. | Prevents anything slipping through the cracks and frees agents for higher‑value work. [1] | In the AMS, set “renewal reminder” triggers 60, 30, and 7 days before expiry; attach email/SMS templates. |
| **Multi‑channel reminders** | Deliver the same reminder via the client’s preferred channel (text, email, phone, portal). | Increases the chance the client sees and acts on the notice; research shows most consumers prefer texting for quick updates. [1] | Capture “Preferred channel” in the record; use the AMS to route the automated reminder accordingly. |
| **Personalized messaging** | Reference recent personal details (e.g., “Congrats on the new baby!”) and use the client’s name. | Shows the client they are more than a policy number, boosting loyalty and referrals. [1] | Pull personalization tokens from the AMS notes; use AI‑assisted email tools (e.g., EZLynx EVA™) to draft. |
| **Proactive follow‑up cadence** | After a renewal reminder is sent, follow up within 48 h if no response, then again a week before the deadline. | Keeps the conversation moving and signals you’re on top of their needs. | Create a task queue in the AMS that auto‑creates follow‑up tasks after each reminder. |
| **Quick acknowledgment** | When a client contacts you, reply instantly (“Got your message, I’m looking into it”). | Builds trust and reduces anxiety while you gather the answer. [1] | Set up an auto‑reply template that references the client’s name and promises a detailed response. |
| **Document sharing & self‑service** | Upload policy documents, ID cards, and renewal packets to a client portal. | Gives clients 24/7 access, reduces inbound requests, and ensures you have a single source of truth. [1] | Enable portal access in the AMS; send a one‑time login email after each policy is bound. |
| **Audit & clean‑up** | Quarterly, run a report to flag missing contact info, overdue renewals, or incomplete notes. | Maintains data integrity and catches at‑risk policies early. | Use the AMS reporting feature; assign a staff member to resolve flagged items. |

### Quick “Renewal Day” workflow
1. **72 days out** – Automated email + SMS reminder (personalized, includes renewal quote link).  
2. **48 hours later** – AMS creates a task for the assigned agent to call if no click‑through.  
3. **30 days out** – Second reminder with a brief policy‑benefit recap.  
4. **7 days out** – Final notice; attach renewal documents in the portal and flag for manual outreach if still pending.  

Following these steps—centralised, consistently‑updated records; automated, multi‑channel reminders; and personalized, proactive follow‑up—helps agents keep every policy on track, improve client satisfaction, and drive higher renewal rates. [1]
