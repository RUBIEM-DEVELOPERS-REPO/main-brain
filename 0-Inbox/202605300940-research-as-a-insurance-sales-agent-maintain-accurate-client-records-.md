# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, maintain accurate client records and follow up on policy renewals

**How an Insurance Sales Agent Can Keep Accurate Client Records and Stay on Top of Policy Renewals**

| Step | What to Do | Why It Helps |
|------|------------|--------------|
| **1. Centralise all client data** | • Use a single CRM or the NeuroWorks “vault” to store contact info, policy numbers, coverage details, premium amounts, and key dates. <br>• Tag each record with the product type (e.g., motor, health, life) and renewal frequency. | A single source of truth prevents duplicate entries and makes it easy to pull reports. |
| **2. Standardise data entry** | • Create a template (fields: Name, DOB, Address, Phone, Email, Policy # , Effective Date, Expiry Date, Premium, Beneficiaries, Notes). <br>• Enforce mandatory fields and use drop‑down lists for status (Active, Lapsed, Pending Renewal). | Consistency speeds up searching and reduces errors when you later generate renewal reminders. |
| **3. Automate renewal alerts** | • Set a recurring reminder 30 days before each policy’s expiry. <br>• Use the platform’s scheduled‑task feature to send an email or SMS to the client and a follow‑up task to you. | Early alerts give you enough time to discuss changes, collect documents, and avoid lapses. |
| **4. Track communication history** | • Log every call, email, or meeting in the client’s record, noting the outcome and any action items. <br>• Attach scanned documents (e.g., signed proposals, ID copies). | A complete audit trail helps you recall past discussions and demonstrates compliance if an audit occurs. |
| **5. Review and clean the database regularly** | • Run a monthly “stale‑record” report to flag clients with no activity for > 6 months. <br>• Verify contact details via a quick phone check or an automated verification service. | Keeping the database current reduces wasted outreach and improves conversion on renewal offers. |
| **6. Use reporting for proactive outreach** | • Generate a “Renewals Due This Week” report. <br>• Prioritise high‑value policies or those with upcoming premium changes. | Focused outreach maximises renewal rates and revenue. |
| **7. Secure the data** | • Apply role‑based access controls so only authorised staff can edit client records. <br>• Back up the vault daily and encrypt sensitive fields (e.g., SSN, bank details). | Protects client privacy and meets regulatory requirements. |
| **8. Document the process** | • Write a short SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) that outlines each of the steps above. <br>• Store the SOP in the same vault for easy reference and onboarding of new agents. | Consistency across the team leads to higher quality service. |
