# Research: following question on the web

**“Following” – meanings and usage**

| Part of speech | Core meaning | Typical examples |
|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Preposition** | *after*; indicating something that comes later in time or as a result of something else. | “The weeks **following** the riots were tense.” (Cambridge) |
| **Preposition** | *subsequent to*; used to show a cause‑effect relationship. | “Major changes are expected **following** the agreement.” (Cambridge) |
| **Adjective** | Refers to the next item in a list or the one that comes next in time. | “The **following** day, morning, etc., is the next one.” (Cambridge) |
| **Noun** (less common) | A group of supporters or admirers. | (Not covered in the supplied sources.) |

**Key points**

- As a **preposition**, *following* always points forward in time (“after”) and can also imply a result (“as a consequence of”).  
- As an **adjective**, it signals the item that comes next (“the following chapter”).  
- The word can function as a **noun** meaning “supporters,” but this sense was not detailed in the provided definitions.
