# Research: Plan the rollout of MFA across all 1200 employees in 30 days. Include phases, owner per phase, and the top 3 risks with mitigations

**Proposed 30‑day MFA rollout plan (based on the generic definition of a “plan” as a set of decisions about how to do something in the future) [1]**

| Phase (Days) | Key Activities | Owner |
|--------------|----------------|-------|
| **Phase 1 – Preparation (Days 1‑5)** | • Inventory all user accounts and devices  <br>• Choose the MFA technology (e.g., authenticator app, hardware token)  <br>• Draft communication templates and support FAQs | **IT Security Lead** |
| **Phase 2 – Pilot (Days 6‑12)** | • Enroll a representative sample (≈5 % ≈ 60 users)  <br>• Conduct live training webinars  <br>• Capture issues and refine enrollment scripts | **Pilot‑Program Manager** |
| **Phase 3 – Tier‑1 Rollout (Days 13‑20)** | • Enroll all non‑critical staff (≈30 % ≈ 360 users)  <br>• Provide step‑by‑step self‑service guides  <br>• Operate a dedicated help‑desk for MFA queries | **Operations Manager** |
| **Phase 4 – Full Deployment (Days 21‑27)** | • Enroll remaining staff (≈65 % ≈ 780 users)  <br>• Enforce MFA via policy push (e.g., conditional access)  <br>• Monitor compliance dashboards daily | **Compliance Officer** |
| **Phase 5 – Stabilisation & Review (Days 28‑30)** | • Verify 100 % enrollment and successful log‑ins  <br>• Conduct post‑implementation survey  <br>• Document lessons learned and hand‑off to ongoing support | **Program Sponsor** |

### Top 3 Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters (based on the idea that a plan can “rarely go according to plan”) | Mitigation |
|------|------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| **1. Low user adoption / enrollment failures** | Users may ignore enrollment emails or encounter device incompatibility, causing the rollout to fall short of the 30‑day target. | • Run mandatory pre‑enrollment reminders (email + Teams/Slack). <br>• Offer multiple MFA options (app, SMS, hardware token) to accommodate device diversity. <br>• Provide live “drop‑in” support sessions during the pilot and Tier‑1 phases. |
| **2. Increased help‑desk volume** | A sudden surge in MFA‑related tickets can overwhelm support staff, delaying resolution and eroding confidence. | • Staff a temporary “MFA support squad” drawn from the pilot team. <br>• Publish a self‑service knowledge base (FAQs, video walk‑throughs). <br>• Track ticket trends and re‑allocate resources in real time. |
| **3. Service disruption from mis‑configured MFA policies** | Incorrect conditional‑access rules could lock out users or break integrated applications, impacting business continuity. | • Use the pilot phase to test policy enforcement on a sandbox environment. <br>• Implement a rollback window (24 h) for any policy change. <br>• Conduct daily compliance checks and have the Compliance Officer sign‑off before moving to the next phase. |

*Note:* The evidence supplied only defines “plan” as a set of future decisions [1]; there are no specific sources detailing MFA rollout best‑practice. The above structure extrapolates a typical phased implementation using that generic definition.
