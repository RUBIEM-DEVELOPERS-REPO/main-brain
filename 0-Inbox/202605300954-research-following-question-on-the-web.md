# Research: following question on the web

**“Following” – short definition**

*Part of speech*: mainly a **preposition** (also used as an adjective).  

*Core meaning*: **“after” or “subsequent to” something in time or order**. It introduces the thing that comes later or is a result of a previous event.  

*Typical uses*  

| Form | Example | Sense |
|------|---------|-------|
| **preposition** | “The weeks **following** the riots were tense.” | “after the riots” |
| **preposition** | “Oil prices are certain to rise **following** the agreement.” | “as a result of / after” |
| **adjective** (before a noun) | “The **following** day, we left early.” | “the next” |

*In Czech* the word is translated as **“následující”** (adjective) or **“po” / “po něčem”** (preposition), conveying the same idea of something that comes later or is consequent [3].  

So, “following” essentially signals a temporal or logical succession: something that occurs **after** or **as a consequence of** the referenced event.  

**Sources**: Cambridge Dictionary entry for “following” explains the prepositional use and gives examples of “after” and “subsequent to” [1]; Czech‑English dictionary shows the translation “následující” and related meanings [3].
