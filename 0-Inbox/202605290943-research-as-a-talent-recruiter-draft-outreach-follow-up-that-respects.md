# Research: As a Talent Recruiter, draft outreach + follow-up that respects candidate time

**Outreach + Follow‑up Templates that Respect a Candidate’s Time**  

*(All wording is grounded in the dictionary definition of “talent” as a natural skill or ability — a person who “has a natural skill or ability to be good at something” [1].  By acknowledging the candidate’s talent up‑front, you signal that you value their limited time and only reach out when the role truly matches their abilities.)*  

---

### 1️⃣ Initial Outreach (≈ 100 words)

**Subject:** Quick note about a role that matches your [skill/field] talent  

Hi **[First Name]**,

I’m **[Your Name]**, a recruiter at **[Company]**. I came across your profile and was impressed by your **[specific talent/achievement]** — the kind of natural ability that stands out in our industry [1].  

We have a **[role title]** that could let you leverage that talent while keeping the hiring process short and transparent. If you have 10 minutes for a brief call this week, I’d love to share why this might be a good fit.

Thanks for considering, and I’ll follow up in a few days if I don’t hear back.

Best,  
[Your Name] | [Phone] | [LinkedIn]

---

### 2️⃣ First Follow‑up (2–3 days later, ≈ 70 words)

**Subject:** Re: Quick note about a role that matches your talent  

Hi **[First Name]**,

Just checking if you saw my previous message. I respect your schedule, so a short 10‑minute chat is all I’m asking. Let me know a time that works, or feel free to reply with any questions.

Thanks again,  
[Your Name]

---

### 3️⃣ Second Follow‑up (1 week later, ≈ 60 words)

**Subject:** Last touch – still interested in a role that fits your talent?  

Hi **[First Name]**,

I don’t want to fill up your inbox, so this will be my final outreach. If you’re open to learning more, please drop a quick reply; otherwise I’ll consider you not interested and won’t follow up again.

Appreciate your time,  
[Your Name]

---

#### Why this works
- **Immediate relevance:** You reference the candidate’s *talent* (natural skill) right away, showing you’ve done the homework [1].
- **Time‑boxed ask:** Each message limits the request to a 10‑minute call, explicitly acknowledging their busy schedule.
- **Clear cadence & polite exit:** A three‑step sequence (initial, gentle reminder, final “no‑pressure” note) gives the candidate control and avoids over‑messaging.

Feel free to tweak the placeholders for industry‑specific language, but keep the core structure—short, talent‑focused, and time‑respectful.
