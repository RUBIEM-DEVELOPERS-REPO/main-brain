# Research: As a Data Analyst, write findings memos with the chart, the takeaway, and the recommended action

**Findings Memo – Data‑Analyst Template**  

| **Section** | **Content** |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Title** | *[Descriptive title of the analysis]* |
| **Background** | Brief context – why the data set was collected, source, time‑frame, and any relevant business question. |
| **Key Metrics / Variables** | List of the most important fields (e.g., sales $ , conversion rate, churn %, etc.) and how they are measured. |
| **Chart** | Insert a single, high‑impact visual that tells the story at a glance (e.g., line chart for trend, bar chart for comparison, heat map for correlation). <br>*Caption:* “Figure 1 – [What the chart shows]”. |
| **Takeaway** | One‑sentence insight that directly answers the business question. It should be **action‑oriented** and **data‑driven** (e.g., “Revenue grew 12 % YoY, driven primarily by the North‑East region”). |
| **Supporting Evidence** | • Quantitative evidence (e.g., “North‑East sales increased from $2.3 M to $2.6 M (p < 0.01)”). <br>• Qualitative notes if relevant (e.g., “Seasonal promotion launched in Q2”). |
| **Recommended Action** | Concrete next step(s) for the stakeholder, tied to the takeaway (e.g., “Allocate an additional 10 % marketing budget to the North‑East for Q3 to sustain growth”). |
| **Assumptions & Limitations** | List any data quality issues, time‑lag, or external factors that could affect interpretation. |
| **Next Steps / Follow‑up** | Suggested further analysis (e.g., deeper segmentation, A/B test) and timeline. |
| **Prepared by** | *Your name, role, date* |

---

### How to Use This Memo

1. **Populate the template** with your specific data set and analysis results.  
2. **Keep the chart simple** – a single visual should capture the core pattern; avoid clutter.  
3. **Write the takeaway** in plain language, focusing on *what* the data tells the decision‑maker, not *how* you derived it.  
4. **Link the recommendation** directly to the takeaway; the action must be feasible and measurable.  

> *Data are “a collection of discrete or continuous values that conveys information” and become actionable insights once they are organized, visualized, and interpreted [1].*
