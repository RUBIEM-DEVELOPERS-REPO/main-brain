## Topic statement  
The task is to show how customer‑feedback can be broken down into sentiment, theme and intensity, then clustered and ranked by the product of frequency and intensity, while separating “what they love”, “what’s hurting” and emerging issues. The evidence supplied consists only of generic definitions of “customer” from mainstream, critical, practitioner and recent sources; none of the sources provide actual feedback items, tagging methods, clustering techniques or ranking formulas.

## Perspectives  

### mainstream  
The mainstream sources describe what a customer is and why businesses study customer behavior, but they contain no guidance on tagging feedback, clustering themes or ranking by frequency × intensity. Consequently this perspective offers no substantive claim about the requested analysis.  

### critical  
The critical sources repeat the same basic definition of a customer and note that businesses monitor relationships to improve products, yet they likewise give no methodological detail on sentiment‑theme‑intensity tagging or trend identification. No relevant claim can be extracted.  

### practitioner  
Practitioner‑oriented entries again focus on the definition of a customer and the importance of understanding customer needs for business success. They do not discuss practical frameworks for coding feedback, clustering themes or calculating a frequency‑intensity score, so this perspective provides no usable evidence.  

### recent  
The recent perspective mirrors the earlier ones, emphasizing that customers drive revenue and that companies study behavior, but it does not present any concrete approach to tagging, clustering or ranking feedback items. No applicable claim is available.  

## Cross‑cutting themes  
- All four perspectives agree that **customers are the recipients of goods or services and are essential for business revenue**【1†L1-L4】.  
- Each source stresses that **understanding customer behavior helps improve products or services**【2†L9-L12】【2†L13-L15】.  

## Open questions  
- **How should individual feedback items be assigned a sentiment label (positive/negative/neutral) and an intensity score?** No perspective supplies a method.  
- **What taxonomy of themes (e.g., product quality, service speed, price) is appropriate for clustering?** Unaddressed across sources.  
- **How to compute the “frequency × intensity” ranking and what thresholds define “emerging” issues?** No guidance offered, leading to disagreement on the metric’s definition.  

## Bottom line  
The supplied evidence only defines “customer” and highlights the generic business value of studying customer behavior; it does **not** provide any of the analytical tools or examples needed to tag feedback with sentiment, theme and intensity, cluster those themes, or rank them by frequency × intensity. To fulfill the original request, additional sources that detail qualitative coding frameworks (e.g., sentiment analysis taxonomies), clustering methods (e.g., hierarchical or k‑means on coded data), and scoring formulas would be required. Until such evidence is obtained, the proposed analysis cannot be substantiated.
