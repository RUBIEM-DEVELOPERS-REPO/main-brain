# Research: contract review best practices for contracts reviewer role

**Contract‑Reviewer Best Practices**

| Phase | Key Actions | Why It Matters |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1. Preparation** | • Confirm the parties’ identities and signing authority (e.g., board resolutions, corporate sign‑off limits).<br>• Gather the relevant background (business purpose, prior negotiations, regulatory constraints). | Ensures the agreement is enforceable and avoids later challenges to authority or capacity [1]. |
| **2. Structural Review** | • Check that the contract contains the three essential elements: **offer**, **acceptance**, and **consideration** (or a valid promissory‑estoppel claim).<br>• Verify that the agreement is **legally permissible** – no illegal subject matter or public‑policy violations. | A contract lacking any of these fundamentals is void or unenforceable [1]. |
| **3. Clause‑by‑Clause Analysis** | • **Definitions** – make sure key terms are clear and unambiguous.<br>• **Scope of Work / Deliverables** – confirm precise description, timelines, and acceptance criteria.<br>• **Payment & Consideration** – ensure amounts, schedule, invoicing, and any contingencies are spelled out.<br>• **Termination & Remedies** – look for clear triggers, notice periods, and liquidated damages or indemnities.<br>• **Confidentiality / IP** – verify ownership, licensing, and protection provisions.<br>• **Dispute Resolution** – note governing law, jurisdiction, and arbitration/mediation clauses.<br>• **Force Majeure & Change‑of‑Law** – confirm appropriate risk‑allocation for unforeseeable events. | Detailed clause review catches hidden risks and aligns the contract with the parties’ commercial intent. |
| **4. Compliance Checks** | • Confirm any **Statute of Fractions**‑type requirements (e.g., contracts for land, goods > $500, etc.) are satisfied with a written instrument.<br>• Ensure required signatures, dates, and, where applicable, electronic‑signature compliance. | Missing statutory formalities can render a contract unenforceable [1]. |
| **5. Risk Assessment** | • Identify high‑impact clauses (liability caps, warranties, indemnities).<br>• Rate each risk (low/medium/high) and recommend mitigations (e.g., additional insurance, carve‑outs). | Provides the decision‑maker with a clear picture of exposure. |
| **6. Red‑lining & Recommendations** | • Use track‑changes to propose language that clarifies ambiguities, balances obligations, or adds missing protections.<br>• Accompany changes with concise rationales (e.g., “adds definition to avoid divergent interpretations”). | Keeps the negotiation focused and defensible. |
| **7. Final Sign‑off** | • Prepare a **review checklist** confirming all items above are addressed.<br>• Obtain sign‑off from the appropriate internal stakeholder (legal, finance, business owner). | Guarantees a consistent, repeatable process and audit trail. |

### Quick Reference Checklist (for each contract)

1. **Parties & Authority** – correct legal names, signing power.  
2. **Essential Elements** – offer, acceptance, consideration (or promissory estoppel).  
3. **Legality** – no illegal subject matter.  
4. **Statutory Formalities** – writing required? (Statute of Frauds).  
5. **Key Clauses** – scope, payment, term, termination, IP, confidentiality, dispute resolution.  
6. **Risk Flags** – liability, indemnity, force majeure.  
7. **Compliance** – signatures, dates, electronic‑signature rules.  
8. **Documentation** – maintain version history and reviewer sign‑off.

Following this structured approach helps a contract reviewer systematically verify enforceability, protect the organization’s interests, and streamline negotiations.
