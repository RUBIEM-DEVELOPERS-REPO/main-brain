# Research: insurance products suitable for clients based on identified needs

**Matching insurance products to common client needs**

| Client need | Most relevant product(s) | Why it fits |
|-------------|--------------------------|-------------|
| **Vehicle protection (personal or commercial)** | Motor / Car Insurance, eBike & eScooter Insurance | Covers accidental damage, theft, third‑party liability and offers 24 h emergency assistance — exactly what drivers and fleet owners require. [1] |
| **Travel abroad or business trips** | Travel Insurance (including “iCycle” for bike‑rental trips) | Provides medical cover, trip cancellation, lost‑baggage and personal accident benefits while overseas. [1] |
| **Home & property security** | Home Insurance, HomeLet (rental‑property) Insurance | Protects the building, contents and liability for owners or landlords; HomeLet adds landlord‑specific coverage. [1] |
| **Pet health & liability** | Pet Insurance | Pays for veterinary treatment, accidental injury and third‑party liability if the pet causes damage. [1] |
| **Health & dental expenses** | Health & Dental Insurance | Covers routine check‑ups, specialist treatment, dental work and, in some plans, prescription drugs. [1] |
| **Life‑stage financial planning** | Life & Pensions | Provides death benefit, income protection for families and retirement‑pension options. [1] |
| **Business continuity** | Business Insurance (including Protected Cells) | Offers property, liability, professional indemnity and specialised “Protected Cell” structures for risk segregation. [1] |
| **Marine & leisure vessels** | Boat Insurance, Yacht Secure | Covers hull damage, liability, crew injury and environmental liability for private or charter vessels. [1] |
| **Special‑use mobility** | Bicycle & eScooter Insurance | Tailored for cyclists and e‑scooter owners, covering theft, accidental damage and third‑party liability. [1] |

### How to use this list

1. **Identify the client’s primary risk exposure** (e.g., they own a car, travel frequently, run a small business, etc.).  
2. **Select the corresponding product column** above.  
3. **Confirm any optional add‑ons** (e.g., roadside assistance for motor policies, personal accident riders for travel, or landlord indemnity for HomeLet).  
4. **Quote the client** through Atlas’s “I’d like a quote” portal, choosing the exact product line that matches the need.  

> *Note:* The Atlas platform bundles all of these lines under a single “Select Insurance” menu, making it easy to generate multi‑product quotes for clients with several overlapping needs. [1]
