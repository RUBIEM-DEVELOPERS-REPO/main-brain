**Job Summary – Insurance Underwriter (Custom Role)**  

**Core Purpose**  
Act as the liaison between the underwriting team, insurance agents, and brokers to collect, verify, and synthesize any additional information needed to evaluate risk and finalize policy decisions.

**Key Responsibilities**  

| Responsibility | What It Involves |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Information Gathering** | Reach out to agents and brokers to request missing or supplemental data (e.g., loss history, property appraisals, financial statements, underwriting questionnaires). |
| **Clarification & Follow‑up** | Ask targeted follow‑up questions to resolve ambiguities, confirm details, and ensure all documentation meets internal standards. |
| **Documentation Management** | Log all communications and received documents in the underwriting system, tagging them for easy retrieval and audit trails. |
| **Risk Assessment Support** | Summarize the collected information and present it to senior underwriters or the underwriting committee for final risk evaluation. |
| **Compliance & Quality Control** | Verify that all received data complies with regulatory requirements and internal underwriting guidelines before it is used in decision‑making. |
| **Stakeholder Communication** | Keep agents and brokers informed of request status, expected timelines, and any additional data needed, maintaining professional and timely correspondence. |
| **Continuous Improvement** | Identify recurring information gaps and suggest process enhancements (e.g., standardized data request templates) to reduce turnaround time. |

**Typical Workflow**  

1. **Initial Review** – Scan the submitted application for missing items.  
2. **Request Dispatch** – Send a concise, itemized request to the relevant agent/broker.  
3. **Receive & Verify** – Log incoming documents, check completeness, and flag any inconsistencies.  
4. **Clarify** – Conduct brief calls or emails to resolve outstanding questions.  
5. **Summarize** – Compile a briefing note for the underwriting decision‑makers.  
6. **Close Loop** – Notify the agent/broker of the final outcome and any next steps.

**Key Skills & Attributes**  

- Strong written and verbal communication; ability to ask precise, purposeful questions.  
- Detail‑oriented with solid organizational habits for tracking multiple requests.  
- Familiarity with insurance terminology, underwriting guidelines, and compliance standards.  
- Proficiency with underwriting software and document‑management systems.  
- Customer‑service mindset to maintain positive relationships with external partners.

**Performance Metrics**  

- **Turnaround Time** – Average days from request to receipt of complete information.  
- **Data Completeness Rate** – Percentage of submissions that meet all documentation requirements after the first request.  
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction** – Feedback scores from agents/brokers on responsiveness and clarity.
