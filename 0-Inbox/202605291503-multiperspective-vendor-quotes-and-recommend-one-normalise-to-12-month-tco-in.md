## Topic statement  
The task is to choose a single vendor by normalising each vendor’s quoted price to a 12‑month total cost of ownership (TCO) that includes onboarding, training and implementation, and then ranking the options in a comparison matrix covering cost, risk, functional fit, implementation effort, support quality and exit‑costs.

## Perspectives  

### mainstream  
The sources labelled “mainstream” provide only generic definitions of what a vendor is and the types of vendors that exist (manufacturers, wholesalers, retailers, service providers) [2][3][4].  They do not contain any guidance on how to normalise quoted prices, what cost items to include, or how to construct a multi‑criteria comparison matrix.  

### critical  
The “critical” perspective likewise consists solely of generic vendor‑definition content identical to the mainstream sources and offers no critique, limitation or methodological advice about normalising TCO or building a comparison matrix [2][3][4].  

### practitioner  
The practitioner sources repeat the same vendor‑definition material and do not supply practical case studies, templates or step‑by‑step instructions for normalising quotes, estimating onboarding/training/implementation costs, or scoring risk, fit, support or exit‑costs [2][3][4].  

### recent  
The recent perspective also mirrors the earlier definitions and contains no up‑to‑date (2026) discussion of vendor‑selection frameworks, TCO normalisation techniques, or matrix design [2][3][4].

## Cross‑cutting themes  
- **All perspectives treat “vendor” as a supply‑chain term** – manufacturers, wholesalers, retailers or service providers [2][3][4].  
- **None provide concrete methodology** for normalising quotes to a 12‑month TCO or for building the requested comparison matrix.  

## Open questions  
- **How to define the scope of onboarding, training and implementation costs?** No source specifies which activities belong in each bucket.  
- **What risk metrics should be used?** Perspectives give no guidance on risk categories (financial, operational, security, etc.).  
- **How to weight the matrix dimensions (cost vs. risk vs. fit, etc.)?** No consensus or recommended weighting scheme appears.  
- **What constitutes an exit‑cost and how to estimate it?** The literature does not address contract termination penalties or data migration costs.  

## Bottom line  
The available evidence consists only of generic vendor definitions and does not address the core practical problem of normalising vendor quotes to a 12‑month TCO or constructing a multi‑criteria comparison matrix.  Consequently, no informed recommendation can be derived from the supplied sources.  To move forward, external guidance—such as procurement best‑practice frameworks, TCO calculation worksheets, or case‑study templates from industry analysts—will be needed to fill the methodological gaps identified above.
