# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, generate new business through leads, referrals, and networking

**How an Insurance Sales Agent Can Generate New Business**

Below is a concise, evidence‑based framework that combines the three core channels you asked about—**leads, referrals, and networking**. The only direct evidence we have from the vault are three research notes that all carry the same title, so the recommendations below are derived from standard best‑practice concepts that align with those notes.

| Channel | Key Actions | Why It Works |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **1. Leads (Inbound & Outbound)** | • Create a simple, mobile‑friendly landing page with a clear quote‑request form.<br>• Run geo‑targeted paid ads (Google Ads, Facebook) for the most common local policies (motor, home, health).<br>• Offer a free “Insurance Needs Checklist” PDF in exchange for contact info.<br>• Use a CRM to track every prospect and set automated follow‑up reminders. | Leads give you a predictable pipeline; a landing page + lead magnet converts web traffic into qualified contacts. |
| **2. Referrals** | • Ask satisfied clients for introductions right after a claim is settled successfully.<br>• Implement a “Refer‑a‑Friend” program that rewards both the referrer and the new client (e.g., a discount on the next premium).<br>• Send quarterly “Thank‑You” emails that include a short referral request and a one‑click referral link. | People trust personal recommendations; a structured incentive turns happy customers into a low‑cost acquisition source. |
| **3. Networking** | • Join local business groups (Chambers of Commerce, industry meet‑ups, community clubs).<br>• Attend or sponsor community events (sports teams, school fundraisers) to increase brand visibility.<br>• Host short “Insurance 101” workshops for small‑business owners and families; collect attendee contact details for follow‑up.<br>• Leverage LinkedIn: post regular tips, comment on prospects’ updates, and send personalized connection requests. | Face‑to‑face and online networking builds credibility and opens doors to both individual and corporate accounts. |

### A Simple Weekly Routine

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| **Monday** | Review new inbound leads; send first‑touch emails. |
| **Tuesday** | Call 5 recent clients and ask for referrals; log any introductions. |
| **Wednesday** | Attend a local networking event or host a 30‑min webinar. |
| **Thursday** | Run a paid‑ad performance check; adjust targeting/creative. |
| **Friday** | Update CRM with notes; plan next week’s outreach calendar. |

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target (example) |
|--------|------------------|
| New qualified leads per week | 10‑15 |
| Referral conversions (referrals → policy) | 20 % of referrals |
| Networking contacts added per month | 30‑40 |
| Quote‑to‑policy conversion rate | 25 % |
