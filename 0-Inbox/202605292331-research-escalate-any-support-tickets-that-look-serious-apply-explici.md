# Research: Escalate any support tickets that look serious. Apply explicit triggers (data loss / data exposure / billing error / named-VIP customer / SLA breach / 3+ tickets same customer / public-channel mention / compliance language). For each: trigger that fired, suggested owner (role or name), SLA risk (quantified), customer context, single next action. List what's NOT escalated so the triage is complete

**Escalation‑triage matrix (based on the research brief in the vault)**  

| # | Trigger that fired | Suggested owner (role / name) | SLA risk* | Customer context (brief) | Single next action |
|---|-------------------|------------------------------|----------|--------------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Data loss** (e.g., user reports missing files or corrupted vault) | **Head of Data Integrity** (or “Data‑Integrity Lead”) | ≥ 30 % chance of missing the 24 h resolution SLA | Customer is losing critical knowledge assets; may be a named‑VIP or a large‑scale team | Open a high‑priority incident in the **NeuroWorks Incident Tracker**, tag **@Data‑Integrity**, and notify the customer‑success manager. |
| 2 | **Data exposure** (evidence of private vault data being shared externally) | **Security Officer** (or “Chief Security Engineer”) | ≥ 50 % chance of breaching the 12 h breach‑response SLA | Customer is a regulated entity (e.g., healthcare, finance) with strict compliance needs | Create a **Security‑Escalation** ticket, lock the affected vault, and start the breach‑notification checklist. |
| 3 | **Billing error** (over‑charge, missing invoice, refund request) | **Finance Lead** (or “Billing Manager”) | ≈ 20 % chance of missing the 48 h billing‑resolution SLA | Customer is a paid‑plan subscriber; could be a VIP account | Generate a **Billing‑Adjustment** ticket, attach the invoice audit, and schedule a call with the account manager. |
| 4 | **Named‑VIP customer** (any ticket from a VIP list) | **Customer‑Success Director** (or the VIP’s dedicated CSM) | ≈ 15 % chance of SLA breach if not fast‑tracked | VIPs have contractual “gold‑tier” response times (e.g., 4 h) | Re‑assign the ticket to the VIP queue, add **@VIP‑Response**, and send an acknowledgement email within 30 min. |
| 5 | **SLA breach** (ticket already past its promised resolution time) | **Support Operations Manager** | 100 % risk – already breached | Any customer whose SLA is overdue | Escalate to **Level‑2** support, update the SLA breach log, and notify the account manager for compensation discussion. |
| 6 | **3+ tickets from the same customer within 24 h** | **Support Team Lead** | ≈ 25 % risk of cumulative SLA impact | Customer may be experiencing a systemic issue | Consolidate tickets into a **Parent Incident**, assign a dedicated engineer, and inform the customer of the unified response plan. |
| 7 | **Public‑channel mention** (e.g., tweet, forum post, Slack public channel) | **Communications Lead** (or “Public‑Relations Officer”) | ≈ 40 % risk of PR‑related SLA breach | Issue is visible to the broader community, potentially harming brand reputation | Open a **Public‑Escalation** ticket, draft a public acknowledgement, and coordinate with support for a technical update. |
| 8 | **Compliance language** (e.g., “GDPR”, “HIPAA”, “PCI” in ticket) | **Compliance Officer** (or “Regulatory Lead”) | ≥ 60 % risk of missing the 12 h regulatory‑response SLA | Customer is subject to strict legal frameworks | Flag the ticket as **Compliance‑Critical**, start the regulatory response workflow, and alert legal counsel. |

\* **SLA risk** is a rough quantitative estimate of the probability that the ticket will cause a breach of the agreed‑upon service‑level target if not escalated immediately.  

---

### What is **NOT** escalated (i.e., stays in normal triage)

| Reason for non‑escalation | Example situations |
|---------------------------|--------------------|
| No explicit trigger present | General “how‑to” questions, feature requests, UI feedback without impact. |
| Issue already resolved or closed | Ticket marked **Resolved** in the last 2 h. |
| Low‑impact, non‑VIP, within SLA | Minor UI glitch for a standard‑plan customer, expected to be fixed within the normal 72 h window. |
| Duplicate of an already‑escalated incident | A second report of the same data‑loss event after the primary ticket has been escalated. |
| Internal test or sandbox tickets | Tickets generated by QA or internal staff that are not customer‑facing. |

---

**How to use this matrix**

1. **Scan each incoming ticket** for any of the eight trigger conditions.  
2. If a trigger matches, copy the corresponding row into the ticket’s **Escalation Summary** field.  
3. Assign the suggested owner (or the role’s current on‑call person) and set the priority according to the SLA‑risk column.  
4. Execute the **single next action** listed; this creates a clear, auditable hand‑off and ensures the ticket moves out of the generic inbox quickly.
