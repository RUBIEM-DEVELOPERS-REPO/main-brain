# Research: As a Executive Assistant, write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer

**Pre‑Meeting Brief – Executive Assistant**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Who** | *Name & Role* – Identify the meeting participant(s) (e.g., **Jane Doe, VP of Marketing**; **John Smith, CFO**). |
| **What they want** | Summarise the key objective(s) the person is bringing to the meeting (e.g., “Approve Q3 budget re‑allocation,” “Review new product launch timeline,” “Discuss partnership contract terms”). |
| **The ask** | The specific decision or action the participant expects from you or the meeting host (e.g., “Provide a signed NDA by Friday,” “Confirm availability for a follow‑up call,” “Allocate $150 k for the pilot”). |
| **Recommended answer** | Your suggested response or next step, based on the information you have and the organization’s priorities (e.g., “Approve the re‑allocation, pending final numbers from Finance,” “Schedule the follow‑up for Tue 10 am,” “Escalate the contract to Legal for review”). |

### How to Build the Brief (per the research plan)

1. **Gather the participant’s background** – Pull their title, recent projects, and any prior meeting notes from the vault.  
2. **Clarify their goal** – Look for language in the meeting request or email that states what they hope to achieve.  
3. **Identify the concrete ask** – Distinguish between “what they want” (the broader goal) and the specific request they need answered now.  
4. **Formulate the recommendation** – Align the ask with current priorities, constraints, and any pending approvals; note any follow‑up required.
