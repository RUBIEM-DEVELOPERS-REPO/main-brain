### Senior Customer Success Manager – Typical Daily / Weekly Workflow  

| Frequency | Core Activity | Goal / Output | Typical Tools / Artefacts |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Daily** | **Account health check** – review health scores, usage metrics, support tickets for all 30 accounts (especially those flagged as “at‑risk”). | Early detection of churn risk; update NRR forecast. | Dashboard (e.g., Gainsight, Totango), CRM view, ticketing system. |
|           | **Customer outreach** – send quick check‑in emails or Slack messages to high‑touch accounts; respond to inbound queries. | Maintain relationship, surface upsell/cross‑sell opportunities. | Email, internal chat, CRM notes. |
|           | **Data hygiene** – verify contact information, renewal dates, and contract terms in the CRM. | Accurate forecasting and renewal planning. | CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot). |
|           | **Collaboration with Product** – log any feature requests or bugs reported by customers; prioritize by impact on NRR. | Feed product roadmap with revenue‑impacting feedback. | Product‑feedback portal, JIRA/Asana. |
| **Weekly** | **Weekly QBR preparation** – pull usage, health, and NRR data for the accounts you’ll present. Draft agenda and key talking points. | Structured, data‑driven QBRs that reinforce value. | PowerPoint/Google Slides, analytics reports. |
|           | **Quarterly Business Review (QBR)** – host a 30‑45 min call with each account (or grouped by segment) to review outcomes, roadmap alignment, and next steps. | Strengthen partnership, surface expansion opportunities, reduce churn. | Video‑conference, shared screen, QBR deck. |
|           | **Save‑play execution** – for any account flagged as “at‑risk,” run a predefined “save play” (e.g., executive sponsor outreach, tailored success plan, discount/contract renegotiation). | Convert at‑risk accounts back to healthy status. | Playbook, internal escalation matrix. |
|           | **Pipeline & NRR update** – update the Net Revenue Retention (NRR) model with new renewals, expansions, and churn; share with Finance and Sales leadership. | Transparent, up‑to‑date revenue forecasting. | Spreadsheet or BI tool, leadership sync. |
|           | **Team sync** – attend the Customer Success stand‑up and the Product‑Customer liaison meeting. Share top‑level insights and coordinate on cross‑functional initiatives. | Alignment across org, quick issue resolution. | Meeting notes, shared Kanban board. |
| **Monthly / Quarterly** (beyond the scope of daily‑weekly but often tied to the role) | **Strategic account planning** – develop 90‑day success plans for high‑value accounts; identify expansion targets. | Long‑term growth and retention. | Account plan template, CRM. |
|           | **Renewal negotiations** – lead contract renewal discussions for accounts approaching renewal (typically 30‑60 days out). | Secure renewals, upsell where possible. | Contract management system. |

#### Typical Day‑to‑Day Rhythm (example)

1. **08:30 – 09:00** – Review health dashboard; flag any accounts dropping below threshold.  
2. **09:00 – 10:00** – Send proactive outreach to flagged accounts; schedule “save‑play” calls.  
3. **10:00 – 11:00** – Update CRM with latest ticket statuses; log new feature requests.  
4. **11:00 – 12:00** – Prepare QBR deck for Thursday’s client meeting; pull latest usage data.  
5. **12:00 – 13:00** – Lunch / buffer.  
6. **13:00 – 14:00** – Attend CS stand‑up; share at‑risk list, coordinate with Product liaison.  
7. **14:00 – 15:30** – Conduct a “save‑play” call with an at‑risk account; document outcome.  
8. **15:30 – 16:30** – Update NRR forecast; send summary to Finance.  
9. **16:30 – 17:00** – Quick email check‑ins; schedule next week’s QBRs.

**Key Metrics Tracked Daily/Weekly**

- **NRR trend** (overall and per‑segment)  
- **Health score** (usage, support tickets, NPS)  
- **Number of active save‑plays** and their success rate  
- **Feature‑request volume** passed to Product  
- **QBR completion rate** and post‑QBR satisfaction scores
