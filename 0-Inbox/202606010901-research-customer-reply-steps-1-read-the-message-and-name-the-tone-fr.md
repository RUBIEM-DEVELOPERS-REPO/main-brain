# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak

**Tone:** Neutral (the request is procedural, no emotion shown).  

**Underlying need:** A quick, ready‑to‑use framework for replying to a customer that (1) names the tone, (2) surfaces the real need, (3) opens with the right acknowledgment, (4) resolves that need with a concrete commitment date, and (5) flags any churn‑risk or expansion signal.  

---  

### Draft reply  

Hi [Customer Name],

Thanks for reaching out – I hear you’re looking for [brief restatement of the literal ask, e.g., “the latest usage report”]. What will help you most right now is [the underlying need, e.g., “seeing the data organized by project so you can spot any gaps before the next sprint”].

I’ve pulled the report and added the project‑level breakdown you asked for. You’ll find it attached, and I’ll also upload it to the shared folder by **12 May 2026** so the whole team can access it.

If anything looks off or you need additional filters, just let me know and I’ll adjust it right away.

Best,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Success, NeuroWorks  

---  

**Flag:** No churn‑risk language detected; the customer is simply requesting information. No explicit expansion signal (e.g., request for more seats or new features) appears in the message.
