# Research: onboarding new mid‑market customer NeuroWorks best practices

**Onboarding New Mid‑Market Customers – NeuroWorks Playbook**

| Phase | What to Do | Why it Matters | How to Execute (NeuroWorks‑specific tips) |
|-------|------------|----------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **1. Pre‑Kickoff Prep** | • Gather contract data, stakeholder map, and success metrics.<br>• Assign a dedicated onboarding manager (or “Customer Success Lead”). | Sets expectations and shows the customer you’re organized before the first meeting. | Use a short questionnaire that captures the neuro‑research goals (e.g., data‑pipeline speed, compliance needs) and the primary users (research scientists, data engineers). |
| **2. Welcome & Kickoff Call** | • Conduct a 60‑minute kickoff with all key stakeholders.<br>• Review the agreed‑upon success criteria and timeline. | Builds trust and aligns everyone on the “time‑to‑value” milestones. | Follow Gainsight’s recommendation to **personalize** the agenda: role‑based agenda items (e.g., technical integration for IT, ROI discussion for executives). |
| **3. Role‑Based Training** | • Deliver separate training tracks for data scientists, analysts, and admin users.<br>• Provide hands‑on labs using NeuroWorks’ sandbox environment. | Early wins are critical to reduce churn (Gainsight). | Create a “Neuro‑Lab” sandbox that mirrors the client’s data schema; schedule 2‑hour labs within the first two weeks. |
| **4. Implementation Sprint** | • Map out a 2‑4‑week sprint to configure pipelines, security settings, and integrations (e.g., EMR, cloud storage).<br>• Use a shared Kanban board for visibility. | Demonstrates rapid delivery and keeps momentum. | Assign a technical lead who checks in daily with the client’s IT point‑of‑contact; log all decisions in the board. |
| **5. Early‑Value Milestones** | • Identify 1‑3 quick‑win outcomes (e.g., a baseline neural‑signal report, a compliance audit).<br>• Celebrate completion with a short demo. | Early wins prove value and increase advocacy (Gainsight). | Schedule a “First Insight Review” meeting at day‑15; capture screenshots and metrics for the client’s internal report. |
| **6. Regular Check‑Ins** | • Set a cadence of weekly 15‑minute status calls during the first month, then bi‑weekly. | Keeps momentum and surfaces issues before they become blockers. | Use a simple agenda template: status, blockers, next steps, success metric update. |
| **7. Documentation & Knowledge Base** | • Upload all training recordings, configuration docs, and FAQs to a client‑specific portal. | Reduces support tickets and empowers self‑service. | Leverage Gainsight’s Skilljar integration to host role‑based modules; tag each with the client’s project code. |
| **8. Formal Handoff to Ongoing Success** | • Conduct a “Go‑Live Review” that signs off on all success criteria.<br>• Transition the onboarding manager to the long‑term Customer Success Manager. | Marks the end of the intensive phase and starts the growth phase. | Provide a one‑page “Health Dashboard” that tracks adoption, usage, and ROI metrics. |
| **9. Feedback Loop** | • Send a short Net‑Promoter Survey (NPS) and an open‑ended onboarding feedback form. | Captures improvement ideas and reinforces the partnership. | Use the feedback to refine the NeuroWorks onboarding checklist for future mid‑market accounts. |
| **10. Ongoing Enablement** | • Quarterly business reviews (QBRs) focused on expansion opportunities (e.g., additional modules, custom analytics). | Turns satisfied customers into advocates and upsell candidates. | Align QBR agenda with the original success criteria and highlight any new features relevant to the client’s research roadmap. |

### Key Takeaways from the Evidence
* **Structured process accelerates time‑to‑value** – Gainsight stresses that a well‑designed onboarding program builds trust and reduces churn [1].
* **Personalization & role‑based training are critical** – Tailoring onboarding to each user group aligns success criteria and maintains momentum [1].
* **Checklists and step‑by‑step guides keep the team on track** – Nearshore’s 10‑step checklist provides a practical framework that maps directly onto the phases above [2].

### Quick‑Start Checklist (NeuroWorks)

1. ☐ Pre‑kickoff questionnaire completed  
2. ☐ Kickoff agenda shared & meeting held  
3. ☐ Role‑based training schedule booked  
4. ☐ Sprint backlog created in shared board  
5. ☐ First quick‑win defined & delivered by day 15  
6. ☐ Weekly status calls logged  
7. ☐ Documentation uploaded to client portal  
8. ☐ Go‑Live Review signed off  
9. ☐ NPS & onboarding survey sent  
10. ☐ QBR calendar set for next 12 months  

Following this playbook will help NeuroWorks turn a mid‑market sale into a long‑term, high‑advocacy partnership.
