# Research: following question on the web

**Answer**

“Following question” is a common collocation in English that introduces a question that comes next or is a natural consequence of what has just been said. It is used to signal that the speaker or writer is about to pose a new query that logically follows from the previous statement, e.g., “We ask the following question,” “This raises the following question,” or “The following question naturally arises.” The phrase therefore functions as a transition that highlights the relevance of the upcoming question to the current discussion【1】.
