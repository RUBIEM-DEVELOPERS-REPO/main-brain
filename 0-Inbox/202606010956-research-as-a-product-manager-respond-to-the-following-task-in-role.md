# Research: As a Product Manager, respond to the following task in role:

**Product‑Manager Response**

Below is a concise, evidence‑based outline of how I would approach the (unspecified) product task, using the definitions of “product” and related concepts from the supplied sources.

---

### 1. Clarify the Problem & Success Metrics
| Step | What I’ll Do | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Stakeholder interview** | Capture the business goal, target users, and constraints. | Aligns the effort with the *market need* that defines a product [1]. |
| **Define success metrics** | Choose quantitative KPIs (e.g., activation rate, NPS, revenue) and qualitative goals (customer delight). | Provides a measurable “product‑fit” yardstick [1]. |

### 2. Market & User Research
| Activity | Source of Insight |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Market fit analysis** – size, trends, competitor landscape | Confirms the product addresses a real need [1]. |
| **User personas & journey mapping** | Helps surface pain points and opportunities for a unique value proposition [1]. |
| **Customer feedback loops** (surveys, interviews, usage analytics) | Ensures continuous improvement and alignment with user expectations [1]. |

### 3. Define the Product Vision & Scope
* **Vision statement** – a one‑sentence description of the future state we aim to enable.  
* **Value proposition** – what makes this product different and why customers should choose it [1].  
* **MVP scope** – core features that deliver the vision while minimizing time‑to‑market.  

### 4. Prioritisation Framework
I would use a simple, evidence‑driven matrix (e.g., **Impact × Effort**) and cross‑check against:
* **Market fit** – does it solve a high‑priority need? [1]  
* **Unique value** – does it differentiate us? [1]  
* **Technical feasibility** – can we deliver with existing resources?  

### 5. Roadmap & Delivery Plan
| Timeline | Milestones |
|----------|------------|
| **Month 0‑1** | Research, define vision, MVP backlog. |
| **Month 2‑3** | Design & prototype; early user testing. |
| **Month 4‑5** | Build MVP; internal QA. |
| **Month 6** | Launch beta; collect feedback. |
| **Month 7‑9** | Iterate, add high‑impact features, scale. |

### 6. Go‑to‑Market (GTM) Strategy
* **Positioning & messaging** – highlight the unique value proposition.  
* **Channel selection** – choose distribution channels that reach the target segment efficiently (e.g., direct SaaS, marketplace, partner ecosystem).  
* **Pricing model** – align price with perceived value and market standards [1].  

### 7. Measurement & Iteration
* **Launch metrics** – adoption, activation, churn.  
* **Post‑launch surveys** – capture qualitative feedback.  
* **Continuous backlog grooming** – feed insights back into the roadmap.
