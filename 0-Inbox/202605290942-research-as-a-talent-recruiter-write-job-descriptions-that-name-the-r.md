# Research: As a Talent Recruiter, write job descriptions that name the role's outcomes, not buzzwords

**How to write a job description that names the role’s outcomes, not buzz‑words**

1. **Start with the business result** – state the concrete impact the role must deliver (e.g., “Increase the qualified candidate pipeline by 30 % within six months” or “Reduce time‑to‑fill for technical positions from 45 to 30 days”).  
2. **Translate the result into the recruiter’s core activities** – describe the specific actions that produce the outcome (e.g., “source, engage and assess passive talent in the software‑engineering market” rather than “drive talent acquisition strategy”).  
3. **Quantify expectations** – attach numbers, dates or quality standards to each activity (e.g., “conduct 15 structured interviews per week” or “maintain a 90 % offer‑acceptance rate”).  
4. **Link to the organization’s talent definition** – the Cambridge Dictionary defines talent as “a natural skill or ability to be good at something” [1]. Frame the role as the person who will apply that natural ability to achieve the stated outcomes, not as a holder of vague attributes.  
5. **Avoid jargon** – replace buzz‑words such as “synergy,” “thought leader,” or “culture champion” with plain verbs and measurable targets (e.g., “build relationships with hiring managers to align on role requirements”).  
6. **End with success metrics** – list the KPIs that will be used to judge performance (e.g., “candidate satisfaction score ≥ 4.5/5,” “diversity hiring ratio ≥ 40 %”).  

**Template example**

> **Role:** Senior Technical Recruiter – Outcome‑Focused  
> **Primary outcome:** Deliver a 30 % increase in qualified software‑engineer candidates while cutting time‑to‑fill from 45 to 30 days within the first six months.  
> **Key responsibilities (outcome‑based):**  
> - Identify and engage passive talent in target markets to generate at least 200 new qualified leads per month.  
> - Conduct structured interviews and assessments to achieve a 90 % offer‑acceptance rate.  
> - Partner with hiring managers to define role requirements and maintain a 95 % hiring‑manager satisfaction score.  
> **Success metrics:** Candidate pipeline growth, time‑to‑fill, offer‑acceptance rate, hiring‑manager satisfaction, diversity hiring ratio.  

By anchoring every line to a measurable result, the description tells candidates *what* they will accomplish rather than relying on vague buzz‑words. This aligns the role with the true definition of talent—using natural ability to achieve concrete outcomes [1].
