# Research: interview questions for this role. Output 4-6 technical questions (calibrated to seniority), 4-6 behavioural questions (STAR-format), 2-3 culture-fit questions, plus 'what to look for' guidance for each. Total panel time target: 45-60 min

**Interview Guide (45‑60 min)**  
*Designed for a senior‑level role (e.g., Senior Software Engineer / Lead Data Scientist). Adjust depth of the technical probes for the exact seniority.*

| Segment | # Questions | Sample Questions | What to Look For (key signals) |
|---------|------------|------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Technical (4‑6)** | 5 | 1. *“Describe a system you designed that had to scale to > 1 M requests / day. What architecture choices did you make and why?”*  <br>2. *“Walk me through a recent performance‑profiling effort. Which tools did you use and what bottleneck did you uncover?”*  <br>3. *“Explain a time you had to refactor legacy code to adopt a new technology stack. How did you manage risk and ensure backward compatibility?”*  <br>4. *“Give an example of a data‑pipeline you built that required fault‑tolerance and exactly‑once semantics. Which patterns did you employ?”*  <br>5. *“How do you approach testing for distributed systems (unit, integration, chaos)? Provide a concrete example.”* | • Depth of architectural reasoning (trade‑offs, scalability, cost). <br>• Familiarity with profiling/monitoring tools (e.g., Perf, Jaeger). <br>• Ability to balance refactor risk vs. delivery speed. <br>• Knowledge of reliable messaging, idempotency, checkpointing. <br>• Pragmatic testing mindset for non‑deterministic environments. |
| **Behavioural – STAR (4‑6)** | 5 | 1. *“Tell me about a time you disagreed with a product decision. What was the situation, your action, and the outcome?”*  <br>2. *“Describe a project where you had to deliver under a hard deadline with limited resources. What did you do and what was the result?”*  <br>3. *“Give an example of when you mentored a junior teammate through a difficult technical problem. How did you approach it and what was the impact?”*  <br>4. *“Recall a situation where a production incident occurred. What was your role, how did you resolve it, and what did you learn?”*  <br>5. *“Share a moment you introduced a new process or tool that improved team efficiency. What was the context and the measurable benefit?”* | • Clear **Situation** & **Task** description. <br>• Concrete **Action** steps (ownership, communication, technical depth). <br>• Measurable **Result** (KPIs, uptime, delivery speed). <br>• Reflection / **Learning** indicating growth mindset. |
| **Culture‑Fit (2‑3)** | 3 | 1. *“Our platform values rapid experimentation but also strong reliability. How do you balance speed with quality in your daily work?”*  <br>2. *“NeuroWorks encourages cross‑persona collaboration (e.g., dev, data, ops). Can you give an example of how you’ve worked across functional boundaries?”*  <br>3. *“What motivates you to contribute to an open‑source‑style internal knowledge vault like NeuroWorks?”* | • Alignment with **speed‑reliability trade‑off** philosophy. <br>• Evidence of **cross‑functional teamwork** and empathy. <br>• Passion for **knowledge sharing** and continuous learning (core to the NeuroWorks platform). |

### Timing Breakdown (≈ 45‑60 min)
