# Research: As a Talent Recruiter, draft outreach + follow-up that respects candidate time

**Talent‑Recruiter Outreach (Initial Message)**  

*Subject:* Quick chat about a role that fits your schedule  

Hi **[Candidate First Name]**,  

I’m **[Your Name]**, a recruiter for **[Company/Team]**. I came across your profile and was impressed by your experience with **[specific skill or project]**.  

I know you’re busy, so I’ll keep this brief:

- **Role:** *[Title]* – a **[X‑month]** contract/remote position that lets you shape **[key impact area]**.  
- **Why it might interest you:** *[One line about growth, tech stack, or flexibility]*.  
- **Time‑commitment:** The interview process is designed to take **no more than 30 minutes** of your time (initial call) and **another 30 minutes** for a technical discussion, if you’re interested.

If this sounds worth a quick look, could you spare **15 minutes** for a call this week? I’m flexible and can work around your calendar—just let me know a couple of windows that suit you.

Thanks for considering, and I’ll be mindful of your time.

Best,  
**[Your Name]**  
Talent Acquisition – **[Company]**  
[Phone] • [Email] • [LinkedIn]

---

**Follow‑up #1 (48 h later – if no reply)**  

*Subject:* Re: Quick chat about a role that fits your schedule  

Hi **[First Name]**,  

Just wanted to make sure my previous note didn’t get lost in your inbox. I respect that your time is valuable, so if now isn’t a good moment, feel free to let me know a better window or simply reply “no thanks.”  

If you’re open, a **15‑minute** call this week would let us see if the role aligns with your goals—no pressure beyond that.  

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best,  
**[Your Name]**

---

**Follow‑up #2 (7 days after initial outreach)**  

*Subject:* One‑click way to schedule if you’re interested  

Hi **[First Name]**,  

I understand you have a packed schedule. To make it as easy as possible, here’s a link to my calendar where you can pick a time that works for you: **[Calendly/Booking Link]** (the call is limited to 15 minutes).  

If you’d rather not explore this opportunity, just hit “reply” with a quick “no thanks” and I won’t follow up again.  

Thank you for your time.

Best,  
**[Your Name]**

---

**Key Principles (from internal research)**  

- Keep each message **under 150 words** and **focus on one clear ask**.  
- Explicitly state the **total time commitment** for the interview process.  
- Offer **flexible scheduling** and a **one‑click calendar link**.  
- Provide an easy “no thanks” opt‑out to avoid perceived pressure.
