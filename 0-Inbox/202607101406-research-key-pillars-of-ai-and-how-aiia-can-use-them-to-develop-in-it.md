## Key pillars of AI (supported by the evidence)

Drawing on [1], the durable pillars of AI R&D strategy include:

1. **Sustained, long-term research investment** — AI history shows boom-bust "AI Winter" cycles driven by overpromising and underdelivering; careful, forward-looking research policy is essential to avoid setbacks [1].
2. **Accountability and trust** — accountability of AI systems "in the context of the interplay between AI and the broader society is essential for adopting AI systems via the trust placed in them" [1].
3. **Foundational technical approaches** — the field's roots span symbolic logic/reasoning, neural networks, self-learning systems, expert systems, and knowledge representation [1].
4. **Alignment with societal benefit** — continual AI progress should be directed at humanity's significant challenges to improve social good [1].
