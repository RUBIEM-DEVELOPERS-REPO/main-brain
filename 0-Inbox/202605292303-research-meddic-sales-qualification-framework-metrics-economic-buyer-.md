# Research: MEDDIC sales qualification framework metrics economic buyer decision criteria decision process pain champion

**MEDDIC** is a sales‑qualification methodology that helps reps focus on the most predictive signals of a successful deal.  
The acronym stands for **Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, and Champion**. Below is a concise description of each element and how they fit together in a qualification conversation.

| Component | What it means | Typical qualifying questions |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Metrics** | Quantifiable business outcomes the prospect cares about (e.g., revenue increase, cost reduction, productivity gains). | “What KPIs would you use to measure the success of a solution like ours?” |
| **Economic buyer** | The person who ultimately controls the budget and can sign off on the purchase. | “Who is responsible for approving the spend for this project?” |
| **Decision criteria** | The explicit factors the buyer will use to compare alternatives (features, ROI, compliance, support, etc.). | “What are the top three criteria you’ll use to evaluate vendors?” |
| **Decision process** | The steps, timeline, and stakeholders involved from initial interest to final sign‑off. | “Can you walk me through the steps your team follows to make a purchasing decision?” |
| **Identify pain** | The specific business problem or opportunity that creates urgency for a solution. | “What challenges are you currently facing that you’d like to solve?” |
| **Champion** | An internal advocate who believes in your solution and will help navigate the organization. | “Is there someone on your team who is especially enthusiastic about improving this area?” |

### How the pieces interlock
1. **Pain** creates the *need* for change.  
2. **Metrics** translate that need into measurable goals, giving you a concrete ROI story.  
3. **Decision criteria** reveal what the buyer will weigh when comparing options, allowing you to align your value proposition.  
4. **Decision process** tells you *when* and *how* to deliver information, ensuring you stay on the buyer’s timeline.  
5. **Economic buyer** is the gatekeeper of the budget; securing their support is essential for closing.  
6. **Champion** helps you navigate internal politics, keeps momentum, and can influence the economic buyer and other stakeholders.

### Practical use
- **During discovery**, focus first on uncovering **pain** and **metrics**.  
- **Mid‑pipeline**, map the **decision process** and identify the **economic buyer** and **champion**.  
- **Towards closing**, align your proposal with the **decision criteria** and quantify the impact using the earlier‑identified **metrics**.
