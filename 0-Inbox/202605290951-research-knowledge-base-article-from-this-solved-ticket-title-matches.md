**How do I contact Citizens Advice for help with benefits, debt or consumer problems?**  
*Updated 29 May 2026*  

---

### What you’ll see  

- A **phone number** you can call for free from a mobile or landline (e.g., 0800 144 8848 for the national Adviceline).  
- An **online chat** option that opens a conversation with a trained adviser.  
- A **“Find your nearest Citizens Advice”** form where you enter a postcode or town to get local office details.  
- Information about **Relay UK** (type 18001 then the number) for callers who can’t hear or speak.  

---

### Why this happens (plain English)  

Citizens Advice offers several ways to reach an adviser because people need help at different times and with different issues (Universal Credit, debt, consumer complaints, etc.). The multiple channels make sure you can get advice even if you can’t call during office hours, have a hearing impairment, or prefer face‑to‑face help at a local centre.  

---

### 3‑step fix to get the help you need  

1. **Choose the fastest channel**  
   - **Phone:** Dial the national Adviceline 0800 144 8848 (free). Have your postcode ready so the system can route you to the right local office.  
   - **Chat:** Click “Talk to us online” on the Contact page and select the topic that matches your issue (e.g., “debt problems” or “claiming Universal Credit”).  
2. **If you need a local office**  
   - Go to the “Find your local Citizens Advice” page and type your postcode or town. The site will display the nearest office’s address, phone number and opening hours.  
3. **If you can’t hear or speak**  
   - Use **Relay UK**: dial 18001 then the relevant number (e.g., 18001 0800 144 8848). No extra charge applies.  

---

### Still stuck?  

If you’ve tried the steps above and still can’t get the advice you need, contact our support team with the following details:  

- Your **full name** and **preferred contact method** (phone or email).  
- The **postcode** you used (or the one you live/work at).  
- The **topic** you need help with (Universal Credit, debt, consumer issue, etc.).  
- A brief description of **what went wrong** (e.g., “chat closed before I could ask a question”, “call dropped”, “local office not listed”).  

Send this information to **support@citizensadvice.org.uk** or call **0800 144 8848** and ask to be transferred to the support desk.
