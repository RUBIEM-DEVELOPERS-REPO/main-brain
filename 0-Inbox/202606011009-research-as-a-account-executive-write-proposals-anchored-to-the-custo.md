# Research: As a Account Executive, write proposals anchored to the customer's measurable outcome, not feature lists

**How to Write Proposals That Speak to Measurable Customer Outcomes (Not Feature Lists)**  

| Step | What to Do | Why It Works (Outcome‑focused) |
|------|------------|--------------------------------|
| **1. Start with the Business Goal** | Identify the specific metric the prospect wants to move – e.g., “reduce time‑to‑market by 30 %,” “increase qualified‑lead conversion by 15 pp,” or “cut support tickets by 20 %.” | Anchors the conversation in the result the buyer cares about, not the technology. |
| **2. Quantify the Current Baseline** | Ask for or estimate the client’s present numbers (e.g., current cycle time, churn rate, revenue per rep). | Gives a clear “before” picture so the proposed impact can be measured. |
| **3. Translate Platform Capabilities into Outcome Drivers** | Map each NeuroWorks capability (knowledge‑vault, multi‑persona task runner, email bridge, scheduled tasks) to a driver of the goal. Example: *“Our automated task‑dispatch reduces hand‑off latency, which directly shrinks cycle time.”* | Shows the logical link between what the platform does and the metric you’ll move. |
| **4. Build a “Value Equation”** | Outcome = Baseline × (1 + % improvement). Populate it with realistic improvement percentages based on similar deployments (e.g., 20 % faster decision cycles from the knowledge‑vault). | Provides a concrete, numeric forecast that the buyer can validate. |
| **5. Include Success‑Criteria & KPIs** | Define how success will be measured (e.g., weekly sprint‑completion rate, monthly support‑ticket volume). State who will track them and how often. | Turns the proposal into a testable plan rather than a list of features. |
| **6. Offer a Pilot with Clear Milestones** | Propose a short‑term pilot (30‑60 days) that targets a single KPI. Outline the expected lift and the data you’ll collect. | Lowers risk and gives the prospect a tangible way to see the outcome before a full rollout. |
| **7. Summarize the Business Impact** | Close with a concise ROI statement: *“By delivering a 30 % reduction in time‑to‑market, you can launch 3 extra products per year, translating to an estimated $X M incremental revenue.”* | Reinforces the financial or strategic benefit in the buyer’s language. |
| **8. Keep the Feature List in an Appendix** | If the prospect asks for details, place a brief feature matrix in an appendix, referenced only after the outcome story. | Prevents the proposal from drifting back into a feature dump. |

### Quick Template

```
[Client Name] – Proposal for [Goal]

1. Business Objective
   • Desired outcome: __________ (e.g., 25 % reduction in onboarding time)

2. Current Baseline
   • Current metric: __________ (e.g., 12 days average onboarding)

3. NeuroWorks Solution – Outcome Drivers
   • Knowledge vault → instant access to SOPs → 15 % faster onboarding
   • Multi‑persona task runner → automated hand‑offs → 10 % reduction in delays
   • Email bridge → single‑source of truth → 5 % fewer clarification emails

4. Projected Impact (Value Equation)
   • New onboarding time = 12 days × (1 – 0.25) ≈ 9 days
   • Annual cost saving = $____

5. Success Metrics & Measurement Plan
   • KPI 1: onboarding cycle time (tracked weekly)
   • KPI 2: support tickets per month (tracked via ticketing system)

6. Pilot Plan (30‑day)
   • Scope: onboarding of Team A
   • Milestones: Day 0 baseline, Day 15 interim review, Day 30 final results

7. ROI Summary
   • Expected $____ revenue uplift in Year 1
   • Payback period: __ months

8. Appendix – Feature Matrix (optional)
```

**Key Takeaway:** Every sentence in the proposal should tie back to a measurable result the customer cares about. Features are only useful when they are the *means* to achieve that result, and they belong in an appendix, not the main narrative.
