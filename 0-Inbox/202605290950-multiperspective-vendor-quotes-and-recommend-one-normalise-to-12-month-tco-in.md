# Multi-perspective research: vendor quotes and recommend one. Normalise to 12-month TCO including onboarding + training + implementation. Build a comparison matrix on cost / risk / fit / implementation / support / exit-cost (with

## Topic statement
The task is to evaluate multiple vendor proposals, normalising each to a 12‑month total cost of ownership (TCO) that includes onboarding, training, and implementation, and then to recommend a single vendor. The evaluation should be presented in a comparison matrix covering cost, risk, fit, implementation, support, and exit‑cost dimensions.

## Perspectives  

### mainstream  
The mainstream sources retrieved provide only generic definitions of “vendor” and describe types of vendors (manufacturers, retailers, wholesalers, service providers) without any guidance on how to normalise quotes, build a TCO model, or construct a comparison matrix [2][4]. No specific methodology or recommendation for vendor selection is offered.

### critical  
The critical perspective also returns only generic vendor definitions and blocked web‑page notices; it contains no analysis of cost normalisation, risk assessment, or matrix construction [1][2]. Consequently, it offers no critique of existing vendor‑selection practices.

### practitioner  
The practitioner sources repeat the same Investopedia definition of a vendor and outline basic vendor categories, but they do not discuss practical steps for normalising quotes, calculating 12‑month TCO, or building a decision matrix [2][3]. No case‑study or hands‑on guidance is present.

### recent  
The recent perspective mirrors the earlier ones, supplying only vendor definitions and no up‑to‑date methodology, tools, or recommendations for the requested comparison matrix [2][4].

## Cross‑cutting themes
- **All perspectives reduce the term “vendor” to a basic definition (seller of goods or services) [2][4].**  
- **None of the sources provide a framework for normalising vendor quotes to a 12‑month TCO.**  
- **No source mentions the specific evaluation criteria (cost, risk, fit, implementation, support, exit‑cost).**  

## Open questions
- **Methodology for TCO normalisation:** Which financial model should be used to incorporate onboarding, training, and implementation costs? (No perspective offers an answer.)  
- **Risk assessment criteria:** How should contractual, operational, and security risks be quantified across vendors? (Absent from all sources.)  
- **Fit and implementation scoring:** What weighting scheme best reflects organisational priorities? (No guidance provided.)  
- **Exit‑cost estimation:** How to capture migration, data‑portability, and termination fees in the matrix? (Unaddressed.)  

## Bottom line
The available evidence consists solely of generic vendor definitions and offers no substantive insight into normalising quotes, calculating a 12‑month TCO, or constructing a multi‑criteria comparison matrix. Consequently, we cannot recommend a specific vendor or present a robust matrix based on the supplied sources. To move forward, the organisation will need to consult specialised procurement or financial‑analysis literature that details TCO modelling, risk scoring, and exit‑cost estimation. The current evidence base is insufficient for an evidence‑based recommendation.
