# Research: procurement request from this need. Restate the need precisely. Evaluate 2-3 options. Show TCO (year-1 + 3-year) including onboarding + training + implementation. Recommend one with reasons. Tie to a business goal. Risks + mitigations. Suggest the approver based on amount. End with 1-3 negotiation pushbacks

**Restated Need**  
The organization must acquire a procurement‑management solution that will (1) automate the end‑to‑end sourcing workflow (specification, bid solicitation, evaluation, contract award, payment), (2) provide a secure, web‑based collaborative environment for internal stakeholders and external suppliers, and (3) include onboarding, training and implementation services so the system can be used productively within the first fiscal year.

---

## Option 1 – Commercial SaaS Procurement Platform (generic)

| Cost Item | Year 1 | Years 2‑3 (annual) |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| License / subscription | $45 k | $30 k |
| Implementation & integration | $20 k | – |
| On‑boarding & user training (20 hrs) | $10 k | – |
| Ongoing support / upgrades | $8 k | $8 k |
| **Total 3‑yr TCO** | **$83 k** | **$46 k** (years 2‑3) → **$129 k** total |

*Rationale*: SaaS platforms typically charge a base subscription plus a one‑time implementation fee and optional training. The numbers are industry‑average estimates derived from public pricing tables for comparable procurement‑software vendors.

---

## Option 2 – Ghana Public Procurement Authority (PPA) / GHANEPS solution  

The Public Procurement Authority (PPA) runs the Ghana Electronic Procurement System (GHANEPS), a web‑based, government‑mandated platform that supports all procurement categories, supplier registration, tender publication, evaluation and contract management【3】. Access to the system itself is free for public entities; costs are limited to training, customization and change‑management support.

| Cost Item | Year 1 | Years 2‑3 (annual) |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| System licence / usage fee | $0 | $0 |
| Customization / integration (e.g., API to ERP) | $12 k | – |
| On‑boarding & training (incl. external facilitator) | $6 k* | – |
| Ongoing admin support (internal staff) | $5 k | $5 k |
| **Total 3‑yr TCO** | **$23 k** | **$5 k** (years 2‑4) → **$33 k** total |

\*The PPA recently issued a “Request for Expression of Interest for External Training Facilitators” (May 2026) indicating that paid training is available but modest in cost【2】.

---

| **Total 3‑yr TCO** | **$42 k** | **$8 k** (years 2‑3) → **$58 k** total |

---

### Recommendation  

**Choose Option 2 – PPA / GHANEPS**  

*Why it aligns with the business goal*  
- **Cost efficiency** – The 3‑year TCO is roughly **75 % lower** than the commercial SaaS alternative and 40 % lower than a custom‑built portal, freeing budget for strategic initiatives.  
- **Regulatory compliance** – GHANEPS is mandated for all government procurement in Ghana, ensuring that the solution meets statutory transparency and fairness requirements【3】.  
- **Speed to value** – No licence fee and a short, paid training window (already advertised by the PPA) allow the organization to go live within the first quarter, satisfying the “operational within year‑1” requirement.  

*Risks & Mitigations*  

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Limited customization compared with commercial SaaS | Conduct a scoped requirements workshop early; use PPA’s “external training facilitator” program to tailor workflows. |
| Dependence on public‑sector support timelines | Secure a service‑level agreement (SLA) with the PPA for response times; maintain an internal admin champion. |
| Internal staff capacity for ongoing admin | Allocate a part‑time procurement analyst (≈ 5 % FTE) and cross‑train existing finance staff. |

### Approver Recommendation  

- **Amount**: $33 k total (under $50 k).  
- **Typical authority**: Department‑level budget holder (e.g., Procurement Manager) with sign‑off from the CFO for any external training contracts. If the organization follows a tiered‑approval matrix, a **Level 2** approver (≤ $50 k) is sufficient.

### Negotiation Push‑backs (1‑3)

1. **Training cost ceiling** – Request that the PPA cap external facilitator fees at $5 k; argue that the public‑sector mandate already includes basic user training.  
2. **Customization scope** – Ask for a fixed‑price quote for any required API integration, with a “no‑surprise” clause limiting change‑order fees to 10 % of the original estimate.  
3. **Support SLA** – Insist on a 24‑hour response time for critical system outages, with a service credit (e.g., 5 % of monthly hosting cost) if breached.
