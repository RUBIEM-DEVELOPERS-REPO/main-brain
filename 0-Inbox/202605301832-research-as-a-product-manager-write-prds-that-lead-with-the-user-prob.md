# Research: As a Product Manager, write prds that lead with the user problem and the measurable outcome

**Product Requirement Document (PRD) – “Problem‑First, Outcome‑Driven”**  
*Based on the research note *“As a Product Manager, write PRDs that lead with the user problem and the measurable outcome”* [Vault: 0‑Inbox/202605291953‑research‑as‑a‑product‑manager‑write‑prds‑that‑lead‑with‑the‑user‑prob.md]*  

---

### 1. Title & Brief
**Feature/Improvement:** *[Insert concise feature name]*  
**Owner:** *Product Manager*  
**Target Release:** *[Quarter / Version]*  

---

### 2. User Problem (the “Why”)

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Who** | Specific user persona (e.g., “Data‑driven marketer”) |
| **What they’re trying to do** | The core task or goal they need to accomplish |
| **Pain point** | Concrete evidence of friction, loss, or inefficiency (e.g., “spends 30 minutes daily stitching reports from three tools”) |
| **Impact** | Quantified cost or risk (e.g., “reduces campaign launch speed by 15 %”) |

*Start the PRD with this narrative – it frames the entire effort.*  

---

### 3. Desired Outcome (the “What Success Looks Like”)

| Metric | Current Baseline | Target Goal | Measurement Method |
|--------|------------------|------------|--------------------|
| **Primary KPI** (e.g., time‑to‑complete, conversion lift) | 30 min/report | ≤ 10 min/report (‑ 66 % reduction) | Automated usage logs |
| **Secondary KPI** (e.g., NPS, error rate) | NPS 45 | NPS ≥ 60 | Quarterly survey |
| **Business Impact** (e.g., revenue, cost saving) | $0 | $50 k/quarter saved | Finance model |

*Every requirement must tie back to at least one of these measurable outcomes.*  

---

### 4. Solution Overview (the “How”)

- **High‑level description** of the proposed solution (e.g., “single‑pane dashboard that aggregates data via API”)  
- **Key user flows** that directly address the problem statement.  
- **Assumptions** that are validated before development (e.g., data source availability).  

---

### 5. Requirements (linked to outcomes)

| Requirement | Linked Outcome | Acceptance Criteria |
|-------------|----------------|---------------------|
| R1: Unified data import wizard | Primary KPI – time reduction | User can import from all three tools in ≤ 2 clicks; test shows ≤ 5 min total time |
| R2: Real‑time preview of merged report | Secondary KPI – error rate | < 1 % data mismatch reported in QA |
| R3: Export to PDF/CSV with one click | Business Impact – cost saving | Export completes in < 2 seconds for > 10 k rows |

*Each row must reference a measurable outcome from Section 3.*  

---

### 6. Success Measurement Plan

1. **Instrument** product telemetry to capture the primary KPI.  
2. **Run** a 4‑week A/B test (control = current workflow, variant = new feature).  
3. **Analyze** post‑launch data against the target goals.  
4. **Report** findings to stakeholders and decide on rollout scope.  

---

### 7. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Data source API limits | Medium | Delay in achieving time goal | Cache frequent calls; negotiate higher limits |
| User adoption resistance | Low | Lower NPS | Conduct early‑stage beta with power users; incorporate feedback |

---

### 8. Timeline & Milestones

| Milestone | Date | Owner |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Problem validation (user interviews) | Week 1 | PM |
| Prototype demo | Week 3 | Design |
| Development start | Week 4 | Engineering |
| Internal QA | Week 7 | QA |
| Beta launch | Week 9 | PM |
| Full release | Week 12 | PM |

---

### 9. Appendices

- **User interview excerpts** (supporting the problem statement)  
- **Detailed flow diagrams**  
- **Glossary of terms**  

---

**Bottom line:** By opening the PRD with a clear, quantified user problem and tying every requirement to a concrete, measurable outcome, the team stays focused on delivering real value rather than building features in a vacuum. This structure aligns product, engineering, and business goals from day 1.
