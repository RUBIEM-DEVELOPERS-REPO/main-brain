# Research: job summary work with agents and brokers gather additional information

**Job Summary**

The position is an **Insurance Broker/Agent support role** that ​focuses on acting as the liaison between the insurance company (or brokerage) and the network of agents and brokers. The primary responsibility is to **collect, verify, and consolidate additional information** needed to complete client applications, process policy changes, or resolve underwriting questions.  

Typical duties include:  

| Core Activity | What it entails |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Coordinate with agents and brokers** | Respond to inquiries, request missing documents, and obtain clarifications on risk details or coverage needs. |
| **Gather supplemental data** | Collect loss histories, financial statements, risk assessments, or any other underwriting information that agents/brokers may not have initially provided. |
| **Validate and enter information** | Ensure data accuracy in the underwriting system, flag inconsistencies, and follow up for corrections. |
| **Facilitate communication** | Serve as the point‑of‑contact between underwriting, claims, and the external sales force, keeping all parties informed of status and next steps. |
| **Support policy issuance** | Assist in preparing quotes, binding coverage, and delivering final policy documents once all required information is received. |

**Why this matters:**  
Insurance brokers rely on timely, complete information to price risk correctly and to meet regulatory requirements. By gathering the needed details, the role helps accelerate the underwriting cycle, improves customer service, and reduces the likelihood of policy cancellations or claims disputes.

**Key Skills & Requirements** (drawn from typical broker job descriptions):  

* Strong communication and relationship‑building abilities with agents, brokers, and internal teams.  
* Attention to detail for verifying documents and data entry.  
* Knowledge of insurance terminology, underwriting processes, and compliance standards.  
* Ability to prioritize multiple requests and meet tight deadlines.  

**Typical Work Environment**  

* Office‑based or remote, often using CRM/underwriting platforms.  
* Frequent phone, email, and sometimes face‑to‑face interactions with external agents and brokers.  

**Sources**

In short, the role is a critical support function that ensures agents and brokers supply all necessary data so that policies can be underwritten, issued, and serviced efficiently.
