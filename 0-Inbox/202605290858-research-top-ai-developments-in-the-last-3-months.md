# Research: top AI developments in the last 3 months

**Top AI developments (March – May 2026)**  

| Date (2026) | Development | Why it matters |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| **May 22** | **AMD’s 2 nm “Venice” EPYC processor goes into production** – the first high‑performance‑computing chip built on TSMC’s 2 nm node, aimed at AI workloads. | Provides a new, ultra‑dense compute platform that challenges NVIDIA’s dominance and will accelerate training‑inference for large models. |
| **May 22** | **Japan’s government and major banks obtain access to Anthropic’s Claude Mythos**; a 36‑entity public‑private working group is created to manage cybersecurity risks. | Marks the first large‑scale, sovereign‑level deployment of a leading foundation model for finance, highlighting policy‑driven AI adoption and risk‑management frameworks. |
| **May 21** | **U.S. Senate introduces the FARM AI Act** (Fostering Agricultural Research and Modernization through AI). | Directs USDA grants to AI research, creates a senior USDA AI coordinator, and aligns agricultural AI work with NIST standards – a major federal push to embed AI in food production. |
| **May 21** | **U.S. Department of Health & Human Services begins AI‑driven audit of all state health programs** using ChatGPT and other tools. | First nationwide, AI‑automated oversight of federal health spending, aiming to curb fraud, waste, and abuse across 50 states. |
| **May 21** | **Google re‑launches Search powered entirely by Gemini 3.5 Flash** – AI‑generated answer pages replace traditional link lists; supports follow‑up, image/video queries, and “information agents”. | The most significant Search overhaul in 25 years, reshaping how users discover information and how businesses capture search traffic. |
| **May 21** | **Meta’s AI‑driven restructuring cuts ~8 000 jobs and reallocates ~7 000 staff to AI teams**; 6 000 planned hires are cancelled. | Demonstrates how AI efficiency is reshaping large tech workforces, with direct cost savings and a shift toward AI‑centric product development. |
| **May 21** | **Shield AI integrates its Hivemind autonomous‑pilot software into the Pentagon’s LUCAS loitering‑munitions drone**. | Shows rapid militarization of advanced multi‑agent autonomy, extending AI from simulation to real‑world combat systems. |
| **May 21** | **Bipartisan “FARM AI” legislation** (see above) and **Trump’s cancellation of an AI executive order** over competitive‑edge concerns. | Highlights the political volatility surrounding AI policy in the U.S., with competing pressures to both accelerate and restrain regulation. |

### What the picture tells us
- **Hardware breakthroughs** (AMD’s 2 nm EPYC) are now a key driver of AI capability, not just software advances.  
- **Government‑level deployments** (Japan’s Claude Mythos, U.S. health‑audit AI, FARM AI Act) indicate that AI is moving from experimental labs into core public‑service functions.  
- **Search and consumer interfaces** are being re‑imagined (Google Gemini 3.5 Flash), which will ripple through digital marketing and SEO.  
- **Corporate restructuring** (Meta layoffs, Shield AI defense integration) shows AI’s impact on labor and defense sectors, while political actions (Trump’s order cancellation) reveal strategic concerns about maintaining a global AI lead.

These eight items capture the most consequential AI news across hardware, policy, enterprise, consumer products, and defense in the last three months.
