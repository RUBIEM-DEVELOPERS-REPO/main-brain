# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, sell auto, home, life, health, or commercial insurance policies

## Quick‑Start Pitch for an Insurance Sales Agent  

### 1. Open with a Hook  
> “Hi [Prospect], I’m [Your Name] with [Your Agency]. I help families and businesses protect what matters most—your car, home, health, future earnings, and commercial assets—so you can focus on living and growing without worry.”  

### 2. Identify the Need (Ask‑Based)  
| Prospect Type | Key Questions to Surface Pain Points |
|---------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Auto owner** | “How often do you drive, and have you ever faced a costly accident or a stolen‑vehicle claim?” |
| **Homeowner** | “Do you know whether your policy covers both the structure and personal belongings after a fire or flood?” |
| **Life/Health seeker** | “What would happen to your family’s lifestyle if you were suddenly unable to work?” |
| **Commercial client** | “Which business risks keep you up at night—property loss, liability, employee injury, or interruption of operations?” |

### 3. Match the Product Suite (Evidence‑Based Benefits)  

| Product | What It Covers | Why It Matters (per Investopedia) |
|---------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Auto Insurance** | Liability, collision, comprehensive, medical payments | Provides financial protection against accidents and is mandatory in almost every U.S. state【2】 |
| **Homeowners Insurance** | Dwelling, personal property, liability, loss‑of‑use | Shields your biggest asset from fire, theft, natural disasters, and lawsuits【2】 |
| **Life Insurance** | Term or whole life, death benefit, optional cash value | Guarantees a payout to beneficiaries, preserving family income and paying off debts【2】 |
| **Health Insurance** | Hospital, physician, prescription, preventive care | Covers routine and emergency medical costs, preventing catastrophic out‑of‑pocket bills【2】 |
| **Commercial Insurance** | Property, general liability, workers’ comp, business interruption | Protects a company’s assets, employees, and continuity—essential for any operating business【2】 |

### 4. Highlight a Trusted Local Provider (Jubilee Insurance – Uganda)  
If the prospect is in Uganda or values an established regional insurer, mention Jubilee’s broad portfolio: auto, home, health, life, and corporate health cover, serving over 1.9 million customers across five countries【1】. This demonstrates you can source reputable carriers that fit the client’s geography.

### 5. Show the Value Equation  
1. **Premium** – Affordable, customized to risk factors (age, driving record, property value, etc.)【2】.  
2. **Deductible** – Choose a level that balances monthly cost vs. out‑of‑pocket risk.  
3. **Policy Limits** – Ensure coverage is high enough to protect against worst‑case losses.  

Explain that a modest increase in premium can dramatically raise limits and lower deductibles, giving peace of mind.

### 6. Call‑to‑Action  
> “Let’s schedule a 15‑minute review of your current coverage. I’ll run a free quote comparison and show you exactly where you could save or gain better protection.”  

### 7. Follow‑Up Materials  
- One‑page “Insurance Essentials” handout (auto, home, life, health, commercial).  
- Sample quote sheets from Jubilee (or a U.S. carrier) highlighting premium vs. coverage tiers.  

---

**Bottom line:** Position yourself as the *trusted advisor* who simplifies complex insurance choices, tailors coverage to each client’s life stage or business need, and delivers measurable protection and savings. Use the questions above to uncover gaps, then map those gaps to the appropriate product from the suite.
