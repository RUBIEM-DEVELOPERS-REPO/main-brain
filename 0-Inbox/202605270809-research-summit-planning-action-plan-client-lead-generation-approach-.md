# Research: summit planning action plan client lead generation approach financial projection template for a summit on June 16th

**Summit Action‑Plan (June 16) – Lead‑Gen & Financial‑Projection Toolkit**  

| Phase | What you’ll do | Who owns it | Key deliverable | Timing |
|-------|----------------|-------------|----------------|--------|
| **1. Define summit goals & target audience** | • Clarify the primary objective (e.g., “generate 150 qualified leads for our advisory services”). <br>• Profile the ideal attendee (industry, seniority, revenue range). | Summit Lead | Goal sheet & audience persona | by May 5 |
| **2. Build the lead‑generation funnel** | • Create a **registration landing page** with a strong value proposition and a short form (name, email, firm size). <br>• Offer a pre‑summit “preview kit” (e.g., a one‑page financial‑plan template – see Sophia Bera’s **Meeting Recap Template/Action Plan**). <br>• Set up automated email nurture series (confirmation, reminder, post‑event “next‑steps” email). | Marketing Manager | Funnel map, landing‑page URL, email flow diagram | by May 15 |
| **3. Content & speaker schedule** | • Use the **Financial Plan Template** as a workshop backbone: a 30‑min breakout where attendees fill out the one‑page plan. <br>• Invite 2‑3 speakers who can illustrate how the template drives client action. | Program Director | Detailed agenda with time blocks for the “Action‑Plan Workshop”. | by May 20 |
| **4. Financial projection template** | • Adapt Bera’s **Financial Plan Template** to the summit context: create a one‑page projection sheet that estimates revenue from leads (e.g., conversion rate × average client fee). <br>• Populate with realistic assumptions (e.g., 10 % conversion, $5,000 annual fee). | Finance Analyst | “Summit Revenue Projection” (one‑page Excel/PDF). | by May 25 |
| **5. Execution checklist** | • Confirm venue/virtual platform, test registration integration, rehearse the workshop. <br>• Assign day‑of roles (host, tech support, note‑taker). | Operations Lead | Day‑of run‑book. | by June 10 |
| **6. Post‑summit follow‑up** | • Send the **Meeting Recap Template/Action Plan** to every attendee with their completed one‑page plan and next‑step recommendations. <br>• Track leads in CRM, apply the projection model to forecast pipeline value. | Sales Lead | Post‑event email + CRM report. | June 17‑30 |

### How to use the templates

1. **Meeting Recap Template/Action Plan** (download from Sophia Bera’s page) – serves as the “take‑away” for each participant, turning summit insights into concrete actions.  
2. **Financial Plan Template** – repurpose the sections on income, expenses, and cash‑flow to build a **Summit Revenue Projection** sheet. Plug in:  

   * Expected registrations (target = 200)  
   * Lead‑to‑client conversion (10 % conservative)  
   * Average advisory fee (e.g., $5 k/yr)  

   Result: projected $100 k of new revenue from the summit.

### Quick‑Start Checklist (downloadable)

- [ ] Register attendees → export list → import to CRM.  
- [ ] Attach the one‑page financial‑plan template to the pre‑summit kit.  
- [ ] Run a test of the email nurture flow.  
- [ ] Populate the projection model with actual registration numbers after the event.  

**Result:** A clear, action‑oriented summit plan that not only fills seats but also converts them into qualified leads and quantifiable revenue using proven financial‑planning templates.
