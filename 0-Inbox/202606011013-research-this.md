# Research: this

**“This” – a brief overview**

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Word class** | Demonstrative **pronoun** *and* **determiner**. |
| **Core function** | Points to a specific person, thing, idea, or situation that is **nearby in space, time, or discourse** (i.e., something just mentioned or about to be introduced). |
| **As a determiner** | Modifies a noun directly: *This book is overdue.* It tells the listener **which** noun is meant. |
| **As a pronoun** | Stands alone, replacing a noun: *I love this.* Here it **refers back** to an already‑mentioned entity. |
| **Discourse role** | • Signals a transition or connection between ideas.<br>• Highlights immediacy or relevance, making the content feel current and engaging.<br>• Helps the reader track reference points, improving clarity. |
| **Typical contexts** | - Physical objects within reach: *Pass me this cup.*<br>- Recently introduced concepts: *This argument shows…*<br>- Emphasizing urgency: *This problem requires immediate attention.* |

**Key take‑aways**

- **“This”** is a demonstrative that anchors communication to a particular referent, either by directly qualifying a noun or by substituting for one already known.  
- Its use creates **clarity**, **cohesion**, and a sense of **immediacy** in both spoken and written language.
