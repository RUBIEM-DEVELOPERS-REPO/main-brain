# Research: best practices for selling auto, home, life, health, and commercial insurance policies

**Best‑practice checklist for selling auto, home, life, health & commercial insurance**

| Step | What to do | Why it works | How to apply it across product lines |
|------|------------|--------------|--------------------------------------|
| **1. Build a fact‑finding habit** | Have every client (in‑person or on the phone) fill out a short “risk profile” form that captures assets, dependents, existing coverages, debt and future goals. | Up‑to‑date data lets you spot gaps and tailor quotes quickly. | Use the same form for auto/home (vehicle / property details) and for life/health (income, family, health status) and for commercial (business revenue, liability exposure). |
| **2. Leverage non‑licensed staff** | Train CSRs to collect the fact‑finding form and flag prospects who may need life, health or commercial cover. Reward the staff member with the most completed forms. | CSRs see every client first, so they become the first line of cross‑sell detection without violating licensing rules. | CSR script example: “Do you have a plan for unexpected financial challenges?” – then pass the lead to a licensed agent. |
| **3. Insert the “life‑insurance cue” in every conversation** | Use a set of conversation starters (e.g., “When was the last time you reviewed your life insurance?”) whenever you discuss any P&C policy. | Repetition creates a habit and reminds clients that you’re a full‑service agency, boosting retention and revenue. | While quoting an auto policy, say: “I can also run a quick life‑insurance quote to see what a monthly cost would look like.” The same line works for home, health or commercial quotes. |
| **4. Transition smoothly during a P&C sale** | After the client accepts an auto or home quote, ask: “Would you like to add life‑insurance protection today as well?” | The client is already in a buying mindset, making the extra ask feel natural. | For commercial clients, follow the auto/home quote with: “Many businesses also protect key‑person risk with life/health policies—can I show you a quick estimate?” |
| **5. Follow‑up promptly after the first policy is bound** | Within 7‑10 days call the new customer, thank them, and offer a “no‑obligation review” of any other coverage they might need. | The fresh‑out‑of‑the‑door experience lowers resistance and uncovers unmet needs. | Script: “We’ve set up your auto coverage; now let’s make sure your family and business are fully protected. When’s a good time for a quick review?” |
| **6. Use quick‑quote tools** | Have a one‑click life/health or commercial quote generator ready; tell the client it only takes a few minutes. | Demonstrates value, reduces perceived effort, and often yields an immediate sale. | Integrate the tool into your CRM so that after an auto/home quote is generated, the life/health quote button appears automatically. |
| **7. Keep licensing current** | Ensure you hold the appropriate **General Lines – Property & Casualty** license for auto, home and commercial, and the **General Lines – Life, Accident & Health** license for life/health products. Pass the state exams (InsTX‑PC06 for P&C; InsTX‑LAH05 for life/health) and maintain continuing‑education credits. | You can legally discuss and sell each line; non‑licensed staff can only gather information. | If you’re expanding product lines, add the needed license before marketing the new line. |
| **8. Track cross‑sell metrics** | Record the number of fact‑finding forms completed, leads passed to licensed agents, and conversion rates for each product line. | Data shows what’s working, lets you reward staff, and drives continuous improvement. | Use a simple dashboard: % of auto/home clients who receive a life‑insurance quote, % of commercial quotes that generate a health‑benefit add‑on, etc. |

**Key take‑aways**

- **Every interaction is a cross‑sell opportunity.** Use scripted prompts and fact‑finding to keep the conversation focused on the client’s whole risk picture.  
- **Non‑licensed staff are your eyes and ears.** They can collect data and flag needs, but only licensed agents should present and close the sale.  
- **Timing matters.** The moment a client says “yes” to an auto or home policy is the optimal moment to introduce life, health or commercial coverage.  
- **Stay compliant.** Hold the correct licenses (P&C for auto/home/commercial; Life/Health for life and health) and keep them current.  

Following this structured, data‑driven approach helps you sell all five lines more efficiently, improves client retention, and maximizes agency revenue.
