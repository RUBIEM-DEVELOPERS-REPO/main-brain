# Research: As a Executive Assistant, write pre-meeting briefs: who, what they want, the ask, the recommended answer

**Pre‑Meeting Brief Template (Executive Assistant)**  

| Section | What to Include | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | • Name, title, and department of each participant  <br>• Relationship to the meeting purpose (e.g., decision‑maker, stakeholder, subject‑matter expert) | Gives the organizer a quick “who’s who” so they can tailor language and anticipate dynamics. |
| **What They Want** | • Primary objectives or concerns of each attendee (e.g., “needs budget approval for Q3 marketing spend,” “seeks clarification on timeline”) <br>• Any pre‑meeting intel (recent emails, prior decisions) | Understanding individual goals helps steer the agenda toward outcomes that satisfy all parties. |
| **The Ask** | • Specific decision, information, or commitment the organizer needs from the group (e.g., “approve $150 k for the pilot,” “confirm launch date”) <br>• Desired format of the response (vote, written sign‑off, verbal agreement) | Clarifies the meeting’s purpose and keeps the conversation focused on delivering a concrete result. |
| **Recommended Answer / Suggested Response** | • Draft talking points or a concise response the organizer can use (e.g., “We recommend proceeding with the pilot because …”) <br>• Supporting data or talking‑point bullets (sales forecast, risk mitigation) <br>• Anticipated objections and brief rebuttals | Provides the organizer with a ready‑made, evidence‑backed reply, reducing on‑the‑spot thinking and increasing confidence. |

### How to Populate the Template  

1. **Gather participant info** from calendar invites, org charts, or recent correspondence.  
2. **Review recent communications** (emails, Slack threads, prior meeting notes) to infer each person’s interests.  
3. **Define the core ask** with the meeting’s sponsor—what must come out of the session?  
4. **Draft the recommended answer** using any available data; keep it to 2‑3 bullet points plus a one‑sentence rationale.
