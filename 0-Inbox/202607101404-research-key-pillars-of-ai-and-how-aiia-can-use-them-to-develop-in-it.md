### What the evidence supports

From the assessment of the US National AI R&D Strategic Plan [1], several foundational pillars of AI development can be drawn:

1. **Sustained, long-term research investment.** AI has historically cycled through booms and "AI Winters" caused by overpromising and underdelivering (1970s, late 1980s–90s). Continuous, realistic funding — rather than hype-driven cycles — is essential to durable progress [1].
2. **Accountability and trust.** The paper stresses that "accountability of AI systems in the context of the interplay between AI and the broader society is essential for adopting AI systems via the trust placed in them" [1].
3. **Forward-looking research strategy/policy.** Careful design of research strategy is described as crucial to avoiding future setbacks
