# Research: As a Executive Assistant, triage inbox into act-now / read-later / ignore — with explicit reasoning.

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Triage inbox into act-now / read-later / ignore — with explicit reasoning
Description: Persona-derived starter task for "Evie"

**Inbox‑Triage Framework for an Executive Assistant (Evie)**  

| Category | When to place an email here | Explicit Reasoning (tied to the “executive” role) |
|----------|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Act‑Now** | • Requires a decision, approval, or action that **directly impacts the executive’s schedule, commitments, or high‑value deliverables** (e.g., meeting requests, deadline changes, contract sign‑offs). <br>• Contains time‑sensitive information that, if delayed, could cause missed opportunities or operational risk. | An *executive* is “someone in a high position … who makes decisions and puts them into action”【1】. The assistant’s primary duty is to enable that decision‑making by surfacing items that need immediate executive input or execution. |
| **Read‑Later** | • Informational or background material that the executive may need for strategic thinking, but **does not require an immediate response** (e.g., industry reports, non‑urgent updates, newsletters). <br>• Items that can be batched for a daily/weekly review slot. | The executive’s time is scarce; non‑urgent content can be deferred while still being retained for later strategic consumption. This respects the executive’s focus on high‑impact tasks while preserving useful knowledge. |
| **Ignore** | • Spam, obvious junk, or messages **irrelevant to the executive’s role, organization, or current priorities** (e.g., unrelated sales pitches, personal ads). <br>• Duplicate notifications that have already been acted on. | The executive’s “group of people who run a business or an organization” needs a clean information flow; eliminating noise prevents decision fatigue and protects the executive’s bandwidth【1】. |

### Quick Decision Flow

1. **Is the email time‑critical?**  
   - Yes → **Act‑Now**. Flag, add to today’s priority list, and forward to the executive with a concise action request.  

2. **Is the content strategic but not urgent?**  
   - Yes → **Read‑Later**. Tag for “Review‑Weekly” and place in a dedicated folder or task list.  

3. **Is the email irrelevant, duplicate, or spam?**  
   - Yes → **Ignore**. Delete or archive; optionally add a rule to auto‑filter similar future messages.  

### Reasoning Summary  

- **Act‑Now** aligns with the executive’s need to *make decisions and put them into action*【1】.  
- **Read‑Later** respects the executive’s focus on high‑impact work while preserving useful information for later strategic use.  
- **Ignore** maintains a clean inbox, preventing the executive’s “group of people who run a business” from being distracted by noise【1】.  

Implement this triage as a repeatable workflow in your email client (rules, tags, and quick‑reply templates) so that every incoming message is automatically evaluated against the three criteria above. This ensures the executive’s time is protected and that critical actions surface promptly.
