# Research: key pillars of AI and how Aiia can use them to develop in its R&D

Based on the provided research evidence, integrating Artificial Intelligence into Research and Development (R&D) is a strategic shift that strengthens innovation capabilities and shortens development cycles [1]. 

### Key Pillars of AI in R&D
The core pillars and mature use cases of AI in the R&D and product lifecycle include:
*   **Accelerating Requirements Engineering:** Generative AI uses semantics and Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to instantly extract and consolidate requirements. This cuts down requirement definition times, reduces documentation effort and rework, and keeps development aligned with customer needs [1].
*   **Optimizing Variant and Complexity Management:** Advanced algorithms and AI streamline unnecessary complexity, improve product costs, and reduce manufacturing costs for individual products to optimize overall operations [1].
*   **Embedding Compliance into R&D:** AI-powered compliance assistants track global regulatory changes, assess product portfolios, and connect compliance departments directly with R&D to implement updates rapidly and reduce risk [1].
*   **Boosting Data Migration:** AI streamlines the migration of critical development data across systems [1].

### How Aiia Can Apply These to Develop its R&D
To successfully adopt and scale these pillars, Aiia should align its AI integration across five key organizational dimensions: **strategy, products, processes, IT-technology, and organization** [1]. 

By focusing on these dimensions, Aiia can leverage the pillars of AI to:
1.  **Accelerate innovation cycles** by improving experimentation, data analysis, and product development processes [1].
2.  **Reduce costs and streamline complexity** to lower individual product manufacturing costs and unlock competitive advantages [1].
3.  **Shorten speed to market** while proactively mitigating risks through automated regulatory tracking and compliance integration [1].

To ensure successful adoption, Aiia must address the primary challenges of data quality, security, data availability, and software costs by establishing a strong, business-led governance framework [1].
