# Research: As a Product Manager, write release notes that name what changed for the customer, not what was shipped

**NeuroWorks Release Notes – What’s New for You**

| What the customer experiences | Why it matters |
|------------------------------|----------------|
| **Instant “Team‑Dispatch” suggestions** – When you type a request, the system now proposes the optimal multi‑persona team (e.g., research, writing, scheduling) without you having to manually assemble it. | Saves time by eliminating the step of figuring out which agents to involve, letting you get results faster. |
| **Unified inbox view for all scheduled tasks** – All future‑run tasks appear in a single, filterable list inside the Inbox, with status icons and estimated completion times. | Gives you a clear picture of what’s coming up, so you can plan your day and avoid surprises. |
| **One‑click email bridge activation** – A new “Send to Email” button pushes the latest output directly to your preferred mailbox, preserving formatting and attachments. | Reduces copy‑paste friction and ensures stakeholders receive polished deliverables instantly. |
| **Context‑aware “What‑changed?” summary** – After each run, the platform highlights the differences between the new output and the previous version (added, removed, or modified sections). | Lets you see at a glance what’s actually changed for you, rather than scanning the whole document. |
| **Improved performance on low‑bandwidth networks** – Backend processing now streams results in smaller chunks, decreasing load times by up to 40 % on slow connections. | Makes the platform usable wherever you are, even on mobile or remote sites. |
| **Customizable notification preferences** – You can now choose which events (task completed, team‑dispatch ready, email sent) trigger in‑app or push notifications. | Reduces noise and ensures you only get alerts that matter to your workflow. |

*These notes focus on the tangible benefits you’ll notice, not the internal code changes or component versions.*
