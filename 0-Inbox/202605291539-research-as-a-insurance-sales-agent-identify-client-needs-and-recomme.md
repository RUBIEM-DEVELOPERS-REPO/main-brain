# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, identify client needs and recommend suitable insurance products

**How an Insurance Sales Agent can Identify Client Needs and Recommend the Right Products**  

Below is a concise, evidence‑based workflow that blends the research plan from the internal vault with the concrete product categories and selling points shown on the COSMOTE Insurance website [1].

| Step | What to Do | Why it Matters | Typical Questions / Tools |
|------|------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **1. Gather Personal & Financial Profile** | • Age, marital status, dependents<br>• Income, assets, debts, existing policies<br>• Employment stability & future plans | Determines risk exposure and affordability. | “Can you tell me about your current household income and any loans you have?” |
| **2. Conduct a Risk‑Needs Assessment** | • Identify life‑stage risks (e.g., new parent, retirement, business start‑up).<br>• Map lifestyle risks (commuting, travel, hobbies).<br>• Evaluate property‑related risks (home location, vehicle usage). | Aligns coverage to the client’s most likely loss scenarios. | “Do you own a vehicle or a boat? How often do you travel abroad?” |
| **3. Prioritise Needs** | Rank needs into **mandatory** (legal, mortgage protection), **protective** (health, life, disability), and **enhancement** (travel, cyber, leisure). | Helps the client see where gaps are most critical. | Use a simple 1‑5 importance scale with the client. |
| **4. Match Needs to Product Families** | • **Auto / Motorcycle** – liability, collision, glass‑break, roadside assistance [1].<br>• **Home** – fire, flood, earthquake, pipe‑burst, theft [1].<br>• **Health / Card** – check‑up, dental, physiotherapy, mental‑health [1].<br>• **Boat / Truck / Agricultural** – civil liability, cargo, breakdown [1].<br>• **Life / Disability** – term, whole, income protection (not listed on COSMOTE but standard in the market). | Directly links each identified risk to a concrete coverage. | Show the client a side‑by‑side comparison chart (COSMOTE’s online tool provides this). |
| **5. Use a Comparison Engine** | Enter the client’s data into the COSMOTE “Σύγκριση Ασφαλειών” portal to retrieve real‑time quotes and coverage matrices [1]. | Demonstrates transparency, price competitiveness, and “smart proposals” that COSMOTE advertises. | “Let’s run a quick comparison so you can see the price difference between the basic and premium packages.” |
| **6. Present Tailored Proposals** | • Highlight the **mandatory** cover first (e.g., auto liability, home fire).<br>• Add **protective** layers (health card, life).<br>• Offer **optional** enhancements (glass‑break, cyber, travel).<br>• Explain discounts (e.g., bundle auto + home, coupon MAGENTA MOMENTS) [1]. | Shows value‑added benefits and cost‑saving opportunities. | Use visual aids (tables, infographics) that show coverage vs. price. |
| **7. Address Objections & Close** | • Clarify policy terms, claim process, and 24/7 support [1].<br>• Re‑emphasise the “economy” and “speed” benefits (policy issued in < 5 min). | Builds trust and reduces friction. | “Do you have any concerns about the deductible or the claim timeline?” |
| **8. Follow‑Up & Review** | Schedule a 6‑month check‑in and an annual review to adjust coverage as life changes. | Retains the client and uncovers upsell opportunities. | “Let’s set a reminder to revisit your policy before your next birthday.” |

### Quick Reference of Core Product Types (COSMOTE)

| Category | Core Coverages (per COSMOTE) | Typical Client Fit |
|----------|-----------------------------|--------------------|
| **Auto** | Liability, glass‑break, roadside assistance, uninsured‑motorist damage [1] | Daily commuters, families with multiple cars |
| **Motorcycle** | Similar to auto, plus theft & damage [1] | Young adults, hobby riders |
| **Home** | Fire, earthquake, pipe‑burst, theft, flood [1] | Homeowners, renters (via contents add‑on) |
| **Health Card** | Free check‑up, 50 % dental, physiotherapy, psychology [1] | All ages, especially families |
| **Boat** | Civil liability for recreational vessels [1] | Owners of leisure boats |
| **Truck / Agricultural** | Mixed (civil liability, breakdown, glass‑break) [1] | Professionals using vehicles for work |
| **Life / Disability** *(standard market)* | Term, whole, income protection | Parents, primary earners |

### Bottom Line
1. **Diagnose** the client’s risk profile with a structured questionnaire.  
2. **Prioritise** mandatory, protective, and optional needs.  
3. **Map** each need to a specific COSMOTE product line, leveraging the online comparison tool for transparent pricing.  
4. **Present** a concise, benefit‑focused proposal, highlight discounts, and schedule regular reviews.

Following this evidence‑based process will help you uncover genuine client needs and recommend the most suitable insurance solutions efficiently.
