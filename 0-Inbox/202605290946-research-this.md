# Research: this

**“This”** is a demonstrative word in English that can function as either a determiner (adjective) or a pronoun.  

- **As a determiner** it modifies a noun to point to a single person, object, idea, or situation that is **near** the speaker in space, time, or context:  
  *“This book is interesting.”*  

- **As a pronoun** it stands alone, replacing a noun that is already understood from the context:  
  *“This is my favorite.”*  

Key properties:

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Number** | Singular (used for one item) |
| **Distance** | Near (physically, temporally, or emotionally) |
| **Typical uses** | Introducing or referring to something currently being discussed, held, or about to be shown; e.g., “Can you sign this form?” or “What’s this?” |

The Cambridge Dictionary notes that *this* is used “to show which one is referred to” and gives examples such as “Can you sign this form here for me?” and “This is the one I want”【1】. It also functions when introducing a person (“Harry, this is Joan”) and to refer to recent or ongoing situations (“by this time”, “I will not tolerate this behaviour”).  

In summary, **“this” points to a single, nearby referent and can serve either as a modifier before a noun or as a standalone pronoun**.
