# Research: customer reply. Steps: 1) Read the message and name the tone (frustrated / confused / excited / neutral). 2) Identify the underlying need (often different from the literal ask). 3) Draft a reply that opens with the right acknowledgment, resolves the real need, attaches a date to any commitment. 4) Flag any churn-risk or expansion-signal language separately. No macro-speak.

Context: Role:

**Tone:** Neutral  

**Underlying need:** The customer wants a clear, concise reply that acknowledges their message, solves the real issue they’re facing, and gives a firm date for any follow‑up—without corporate‑sounding filler.

**Draft reply**

> Hi [Customer Name],
> 
> Thanks for letting me know about [briefly restate the specific concern, e.g., “the delay in receiving the latest NeuroWorks report”]. I understand how important it is for you to have that information on time.
> 
> I’ve looked into the root cause and [explain the solution, e.g., “the report generation job was stuck in the queue; I’ve cleared it and triggered a fresh run”]. You should have the completed report in your inbox by **Friday, June 7 (2026)**.
> 
> If anything else comes up or you need further assistance, just reply to this email or reach out on Slack. I’m here to help.
> 
> Best,  
> [Your Name]  
> Customer Success, NeuroWorks  

**Churn‑risk / expansion signal:** None detected in the message.
