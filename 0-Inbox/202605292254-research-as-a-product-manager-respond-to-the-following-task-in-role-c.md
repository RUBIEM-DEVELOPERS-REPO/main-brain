# Research: As a Product Manager, respond to the following task in role:

Context: Role: Custom
Title: Ask Priya anything
Description: Free-form task framed through the Product Manager role. Empty plan — re-plans against the persona system prompt each run

**Product‑Manager Response – “Ask Priya Anything”**

---

### 1. What the “product” is  
A product is “something that can be seen or experienced, created to meet the needs or desires of consumers” and can be a physical item, service, experience or concept [1]. In our case **Priya** is the conversational interface (a service‑as‑product) that lets users ask any question and receive a tailored answer.

### 2. Core Product Attributes to Define  

| Attribute | Why it matters | Target outcome |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Market fit** – Does Priya solve a real need (instant, trustworthy answers) for a defined user segment? | Guarantees adoption and revenue. | ≥ 80 % of beta users report “need met”. |
| **Unique Value Proposition (UVP)** – Fast, context‑aware answers powered by the local NeuroWorks AI‑workforce, with zero data leaving the user’s machine. | Differentiates from generic chat‑bots. | Clear UVP statement on landing page. |
| **Quality / Accuracy** – Answer relevance, factual correctness, tone consistency. | Builds trust and reduces churn. | ≥ 90 % “useful” rating in post‑interaction surveys. |
| **Pricing** – Free tier (limited queries) + paid tier (unlimited, priority processing). | Aligns price with perceived value and covers infrastructure cost. | Conversion rate ≥ 5 % from free to paid. |
| **Distribution** – Bundled with NeuroWorks desktop client, optional web widget. | Low friction onboarding. | 70 % of new installs activate Priya within 24 h. |
| **Feedback Loop** – In‑app rating + optional “Explain why” button. | Drives continuous improvement. | Weekly backlog of top‑rated improvement ideas. |

### 3. Suggested Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Plan  

| Sprint | Deliverable | Success Metric |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Sprint 0 – Discovery** | Validate market need via 10‑person user interview set; capture use‑cases. | ≥ 6 interviewees express “would use daily”. |
| **Sprint 1 – Core Engine** | Integrate Priya with NeuroWorks local LLM; enable free‑form text input. | < 2 s latency for 95 % of queries. |
| **Sprint 2 – UI/UX** | Simple chat window (single‑page), “Ask Priya” button, rating stars. | ≥ 80 % users find UI “intuitive”. |
| **Sprint 3 – UVP & Branding** | Create landing page, tagline, and FAQ that highlight local‑only processing. | Click‑through rate ≥ 15 % from landing page to app. |
| **Sprint 4 – Paid Tier** | Add usage quota tracking, payment gateway, and priority queue. | First‑month paid‑user count ≥ 50. |
| **Sprint 5 – Analytics & Feedback** | Dashboard for query topics, satisfaction scores; “Explain why” feature. | Identify top‑3 improvement themes each week. |

### 4. Risks & Mitigations  

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Incorrect answers** – could erode trust. | High | Continuous evaluation against a curated knowledge base; human‑in‑the‑loop review for low‑confidence responses. |
| **Performance on low‑spec machines** – latency spikes. | Medium | Optimize model size for local execution; fallback to lightweight retrieval mode. |
| **User privacy concerns** – misunderstanding of “local only”. | Medium | Clear messaging on data never leaves the device; open‑source audit of the client. |

### 5. Next Steps for You (Product Manager)  

1. **Create a concise product brief** using the table above and circulate to engineering and design leads.  
2. **Set up a discovery interview schedule** (target 10 participants) and draft a questionnaire focused on current pain points with existing Q&A tools.  
3. **Define success‑criteria dashboards** (latency, satisfaction, conversion) in the analytics platform.
