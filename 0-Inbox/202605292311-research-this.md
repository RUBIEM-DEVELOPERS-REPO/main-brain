# Research: this

**“This” – a brief definition**

- **Part of speech:** Demonstrative determiner and pronoun.  
- **Core meaning:** Used to point to a specific person, object, idea, or situation that is near in space, time, or thought to the speaker. It signals that the referent is the one being highlighted or introduced.  
- **Typical uses:**  
  1. **Identifying a specific item** – “Can you sign **this** form?”  
  2. **Introducing someone** – “Harry, **this** is Joan.”  
  3. **Referring to a previously mentioned concept** – “I’ll explain **this** later.”  
  4. **Emphasizing a current situation** – “I will not tolerate **this** behaviour any longer.”  

The word functions both as a **determiner** (preceding a noun) and as a **pronoun** (standing alone). It contrasts with “that,” which points to something more distant, and with the plural forms “these” and “those.”
