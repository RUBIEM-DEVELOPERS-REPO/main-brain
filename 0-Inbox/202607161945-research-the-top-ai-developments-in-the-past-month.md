# Research: the top AI developments in the past month

Based on the provided evidence, the top AI developments and trends observed as of mid-2025 include the following key areas:

### 1. Cost and Efficiency Improvements
* **Dramatic Decrease in Inference Costs:** The per-token pricing required to achieve equivalent results on standard benchmarks (such as the MMLU) has decreased dozens of times over in under two years, making models vastly cheaper to run [2].
* **The Return of Mixture of Experts (MoE) Models:** There is a renewed industry focus on MoE architectures to optimize performance and efficiency [2].

### 2. Model Evolution and Capabilities
* **More Reasonable Reasoning Models:** The industry has seen the rise of more practical and balanced reasoning models [2].
* **Resource Demands:** AI developments are causing an increasing drain on digital resources [2].
* **Adoption Lag:** While technical capabilities have advanced rapidly, actual real-world AI action and implementation continue to trail behind the industry's rhetoric [2].

### 3. Emerging and Upcoming Trends
Looking ahead through the remainder of 2025, experts anticipate several shifting dynamics:
* **Benchmark Saturation and Diversification:** Existing benchmarks are becoming saturated, prompting a need for new ways to measure model performance [2].
* **Transcending Transformer Models:** Research is moving toward architectures that go beyond traditional transformer models [2].
* **Embodied AI and Robotics:** Increased development in robotics, embodied AI, and world models [2].
* **Agentic and Collaborative AI:** A shift toward "AI coworkers" (with associated emotional consequences) alongside navigating the balance between user privacy and personalization [2].
