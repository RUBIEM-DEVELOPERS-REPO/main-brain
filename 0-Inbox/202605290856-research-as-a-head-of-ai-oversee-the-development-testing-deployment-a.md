# Research: As a Head of AI, oversee the development, testing, deployment, and monitoring of ai models and ai-enabled tools

**Head of AI – Core Responsibilities**

| Phase | What you must deliver | Why it matters (evidence) |
|-------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **1. Strategy & Scope** | • Define the business problems AI will solve and the portfolio of models/tools to be built.<br>• Establish an AI‑governance framework that maps to standards such as ISO 42001 (the emerging “AI Management System” standard). | The Elevate guide notes that a “master bundle” for ISO 42001 gives everything needed to *define scope, govern AI responsibly, map evidence, and operationalise an audit‑ready AI Management System*【1】. |
| **2. Development** | • Lead cross‑functional data‑science, engineering and product teams to create models that meet performance, fairness, privacy and security criteria.<br>• Embed governance checkpoints (data provenance, bias testing, explainability) directly into the CI/CD pipeline. | Governance platforms (e.g., Elevate’s “AI Guardian”) are marketed as “Vulnerability Management as a Service,” implying that continuous security testing must be baked into development【1】. |
| **3. Testing & Validation** | • Run systematic validation: functional accuracy, robustness to adversarial inputs, compliance with regulatory policies (CMMC, GDPR, etc.).<br>• Produce audit‑ready artefacts (model cards, data sheets, risk assessments). | The ISO 42001 bundle includes tools to *map evidence* and produce an audit‑ready system, underscoring the need for documented test results【1】. |
| **4. Deployment** | • Choose deployment patterns (cloud, edge, on‑prem) that satisfy latency, cost and security requirements.<br>• Automate rollout with model‑ops pipelines that enforce the governance policies defined earlier. | “AI Guardian” provides *Vulnerability Management as a Service* for deployed models, indicating that post‑deployment risk monitoring is a core deployment responsibility【1】. |
| **5. Monitoring & Continuous Improvement** | • Implement real‑time monitoring for drift, performance decay, bias emergence, and security incidents.<br>• Feed monitoring data back into the development loop for retraining or de‑commissioning.<br>• Keep governance documentation up‑to‑date for audits. | Ongoing “vulnerability management” and the need for an *audit‑ready AI Management System* imply continuous oversight after launch【1】. |
| **6. Stakeholder & Compliance Management** | • Communicate model capabilities, limitations and risk posture to executives, legal, and external regulators.<br>• Align AI initiatives with enterprise policies (e.g., CMMC Level‑2 playbooks) and emerging standards (ISO 42001). | Elevate’s “CMMC Master Policy Compendium” is highlighted as a library of compliance playbooks, showing that the Head of AI must bridge technical work and formal policy compliance【1】. |

### Practical Checklist for a Head of AI
1. **Governance charter** – adopt ISO 42001‑aligned policies.  
2. **Tool stack** – select an AI‑governance platform (e.g., AI Guardian) for vulnerability scanning, evidence mapping, and audit readiness.  
3. **CI/CD integration** – embed bias, privacy and security tests; generate model‑cards automatically.  
4. **Deployment guardrails** – enforce runtime security (vulnerability‑as‑a‑service) and performance SLAs.  
5. **Observability dashboard** – track drift, incident alerts, and compliance status in real time.  
6. **Audit trail** – maintain versioned documentation for every model lifecycle stage.  

**Bottom line:** As Head of AI you are accountable for the end‑to‑end lifecycle—strategy, responsible development, rigorous testing, secure deployment, and continuous monitoring—while ensuring every step is documented and aligned with emerging standards such as ISO 42001 and CMMC. This creates an *audit‑ready* AI Management System that protects the organization and delivers trustworthy AI outcomes.
