# Research: As a Head of AI, identify opportunities to use ai to improve products, operations, customer experience, and decision-making

**AI‑driven levers for a product‑focused organization**  

| Business area | AI opportunity | Typical AI techniques | Expected impact (why it matters) |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Products** | • Feature recommendation & personalization (e.g., adaptive UI, content ranking)  <br>• Automated quality‑assurance (code‑review bots, test‑case generation) <br>• Predictive maintenance of SaaS services (anomaly detection on telemetry) | Recommender systems, reinforcement learning, large‑language‑model (LLM) code assistants, time‑series forecasting | Faster time‑to‑market, higher user engagement, lower defect‑escape rate |
| **Operations** | • Intelligent workflow orchestration (dynamic task routing, load‑balancing) <br>• Process mining & automation (RPA‑augmented with NLP) <br>• Resource‑usage optimisation (cloud‑cost forecasting) | Graph‑based scheduling, process‑mining + LLM summarisation, demand‑forecasting models | Reduced cycle time, lower operating expense, higher throughput |
| **Customer Experience** | • AI‑powered support (chat‑bots, ticket‑triage, sentiment‑aware routing) <br>• Personalised onboarding & education (adaptive tutorials) <br>• Proactive churn prediction & outreach | Retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG) chat, sentiment analysis, churn‑risk classifiers | Higher CSAT/NPS, lower support cost, increased retention |
| **Decision‑making** | • Data‑driven strategy dashboards (automated KPI insights, anomaly alerts) <br>• Scenario simulation & “what‑if” planning (generative models for demand, pricing) <br>• Risk & compliance monitoring (regulatory‑text extraction) | Auto‑ML pipelines, generative AI for synthetic data, LLM‑based document parsing | Faster, evidence‑based decisions; better risk posture; ability to test strategies before rollout |

### How to start (practical roadmap)

1. **Audit existing data & workflows** – map where signals (logs, user actions, support tickets) exist and where decisions are currently manual.  
2. **Prioritise quick‑win pilots** – pick a high‑volume, low‑risk use case (e.g., ticket‑triage with an LLM) and run a 4‑week proof‑of‑concept.  
3. **Build a reusable AI platform layer** – leverage NeuroWorks’ “second‑brain” knowledge vault to store model artefacts, prompts, and evaluation metrics so later projects can reuse them.  
4. **Establish governance & monitoring** – define model‑performance SLAs, bias checks, and a feedback loop from product/ops teams.  
5. **Scale & integrate** – once the pilot meets its KPI (e.g., 30 % reduction in average handle time), embed the model into the production stack via NeuroWorks’ task‑dispatch or email‑bridge capabilities.
