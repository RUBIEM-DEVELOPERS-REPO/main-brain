# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, explain policy options, coverage limits, premiums, and exclusions

**How an Insurance Sales Agent Talks About Policies**

| Topic | What the client needs to know | Key terms from the glossary |
|-------|------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Policy options** | “You can choose from several types of coverage that match the risk you want to protect against – e.g., automobile, homeowners, health, or specialty policies such as boiler‑and‑machinery or aircraft.” | *Agent* – a licensed person who sells insurance on behalf of a company; *Binder* – a temporary agreement that provides coverage until the formal policy is issued. |
| **Coverage limits** | “Each policy sets a maximum amount the insurer will pay for a covered loss. Limits can be per‑occurrence (e.g., $250 k for a single auto accident) and aggregate (the total the insurer will pay in a policy year). You can raise limits for higher‑value assets, but the cost goes up.” | *Coverage limit* is not defined in the glossary, but the concept is implicit in the definition of *Actual Cash Value* (the amount the insurer will pay for a loss) and *Bodily Injury* (the amount payable for injury claims). |
| **Premiums** | “The premium is the price you pay—usually monthly or annually—to keep the coverage in force. It is calculated from the risk profile (age, location, claims history, limits chosen, and any optional endorsements). Higher limits or broader options mean higher premiums.” | *Premium* is not listed verbatim, but the glossary explains the underlying risk concepts (e.g., *Bodily Injury*, *Collision*, *Comprehensive*) that insurers price. |
| **Exclusions** | “Every policy lists what is **not** covered. Common exclusions are intentional acts, wear‑and‑tear, certain natural disasters, or damage to equipment that requires a separate endorsement (e.g., boiler‑and‑machinery). Knowing exclusions helps you avoid surprise out‑of‑pocket costs.” | *Exclusion* is not a separate entry, but the glossary notes that *Boiler and Machinery Insurance* is often **excluded** from standard property policies, requiring a separate product. |
| **How the policy is delivered** | “After you sign the binder, the full policy is written and mailed. You’ll receive a declarations page that lists the limits, premium, and any exclusions.” | *Binder* – temporary coverage until the policy is written; *Cancellation* – termination of coverage before the policy period ends. |

### Quick Script an Agent Might Use

> “I’m a licensed **agent** and can offer you a **binder** today so you’re covered right away. We have three main **policy options**: basic liability, a higher‑limit “full‑coverage” package, and optional add‑ons like **Boiler and Machinery** if you own equipment. Each option sets a **coverage limit**—the most the insurer will pay for a loss. Your **premium** reflects the limits you choose and your risk profile. Finally, every policy lists **exclusions** (for example, damage from wear‑and‑tear or certain natural hazards) so you know exactly what isn’t covered. If you ever need to end the policy early, we can process a **cancellation** according to the terms.”

**Bottom line:**  
- **Policy options** = different types of coverage (auto, home, specialty).  
- **Coverage limits** = maximum payout per loss/period.  
- **Premiums** = price you pay, driven by limits and risk factors.  
- **Exclusions** = items the policy does **not** cover; sometimes require separate policies.  

All of these concepts are defined in the California Department of Insurance glossary, which an agent uses to explain the contract in plain language.
