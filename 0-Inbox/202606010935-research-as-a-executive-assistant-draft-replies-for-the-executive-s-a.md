# Research: As a Executive Assistant, draft replies for the executive's approval — never pretend to be them

**Executive‑Assistant Draft‑Reply Guide**  
*(All drafts are for the executive’s review and approval – the assistant never signs as the executive.)*  

---  

### 1. General Structure  

| Section | Purpose | Sample Phrase |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Subject line** | Clear, action‑oriented | “Re: Approval Needed – Q3 Marketing Budget” |
| **Greeting** | Polite, professional | “Dear [Name],” |
| **Opening sentence** | Acknowledge receipt or context | “Thank you for your email regarding …” |
| **Body** | Summarise request, provide status, propose next steps | “I have reviewed the attached proposal and …” |
| **Call‑to‑action** | State what the executive needs to decide or sign off on | “Please let me know if you would like me to forward this to the finance team for final sign‑off.” |
| **Closing** | Offer assistance, thank them | “Happy to provide any further details.” |
| **Signature** | Assistant’s name, title, contact | “Best regards, [Your Name] – Executive Assistant” |

*(The above layout follows best‑practice for executive correspondence as reflected in the research notes [0‑Inbox/202605290932‑research‑as‑a‑Executive‑Assistant‑draft‑replies‑for‑the‑executive‑s‑a.md].)*  

---  

### 2. Sample Drafts  

#### a) Meeting Confirmation  

```
Subject: Confirmation – Strategy Review Meeting (May 15, 10 am)

Dear Jane,

Thank you for proposing the Strategy Review meeting. I have checked the executive’s calendar and confirmed availability on May 15 at 10 am in Conference Room B.  

Please let me know if you need any supporting documents prepared in advance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant
```

#### b) Request for Decision / Approval  

```
Subject: Approval Required – Vendor Contract Extension

Dear Mark,

I hope you’re well. The contract with Acme Solutions is set to expire on June 30. The legal team recommends a one‑year extension under the same terms (see attached).  

Could you please confirm whether we should proceed with the extension? If approved, I will have procurement issue the amendment today.

Thank you,
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant
```

#### c) Declining an Invitation (Polite)  

```
Subject: Re: Invitation to the Industry Roundtable

Dear Dr. Lee,

Thank you for inviting the executive to the Industry Roundtable on June 12. Unfortunately, the executive’s schedule is fully booked that day.  

We appreciate the opportunity and would be interested in any materials or a summary you can share after the event.

Kind regards,
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant
```

#### d) Follow‑up on Pending Item  

```
Subject: Follow‑up – Q2 Sales Report Submission

Dear Tom,

Just a quick reminder that the Q2 sales report is due to the executive by Friday, May 19. Please let me know if there are any obstacles so we can address them promptly.

Best,
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant
```

---  

### 3. Tips for the Assistant  

1. **Never sign as the executive** – always close with your own name and title.  
2. **Keep the tone neutral and professional**; avoid personal opinions.  
3. **Attach or reference any supporting documents** the executive may need to review.  
4. **Highlight the decision point** (e.g., “Please approve”, “Do you need more info?”).  
5. **Proofread for clarity and brevity** before sending to the executive for final approval.  

These templates can be quickly adapted to most routine executive communications while ensuring the executive’s voice is only added after their explicit approval.
