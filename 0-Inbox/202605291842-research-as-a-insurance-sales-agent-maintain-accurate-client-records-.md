# Research: As a Insurance Sales Agent, maintain accurate client records and follow up on policy renewals

**How an Insurance Sales Agent Can Keep Accurate Client Records and Stay on Top of Policy Renewals**

| Step | What to Do | Why It Matters | Practical Tips |
|------|------------|----------------|----------------|
| **1. Create a Structured Client Profile** | Use a single, searchable record for each client that includes contact info, policy numbers, coverage details, premium amounts, payment history, and renewal dates. | A consistent format makes it easy to locate information quickly and reduces the risk of duplicate or missing data. | • In NeuroWorks, store the profile in a dedicated “Clients” vault folder (e.g., `_knowledge/clients/`). <br>• Tag each entry with `#policy‑type`, `#renewal‑date`, and `#status`. |
| **2. Log Every Interaction** | Record calls, emails, meetings, and notes immediately after they occur. | Documentation provides a clear audit trail and helps you recall client preferences or objections. | • Use the built‑in note‑taking tool in NeuroWorks and link the note to the client’s profile via a back‑link (`[[client‑name]]`). <br>• Include date, channel, summary, and any follow‑up actions. |
| **3. Set Automated Renewal Reminders** | Create a recurring task that triggers 30‑45 days before each policy’s expiration. | Early outreach gives clients time to review options and prevents lapses that could lead to lost business. | • In NeuroWorks, add a “Renewal” task with a due‑date equal to the policy’s renewal date minus 30 days. <br>• Assign the task to yourself and enable email or desktop notifications. |
| **4. Conduct a Pre‑Renewal Review** | Before contacting the client, pull up their policy details, claim history, and any recent life‑event updates (e.g., new vehicle, marriage). | Tailoring the conversation shows you understand their evolving needs and can propose appropriate adjustments. | • Use a saved query that pulls all policies expiring within the next 60 days. <br>• Review the client’s claim log (if any) to discuss potential coverage changes. |
| **5. Follow‑Up with a Formal Renewal Package** | Send a renewal quote, coverage summary, and any needed documentation (e.g., updated disclosures). | A clear, professional package speeds the decision‑making process and reinforces trust. | • Generate the package from a template stored in NeuroWorks (`_templates/renewal‑package.md`). <br>• Attach the PDF to the client’s record and log the outbound email. |
| **6. Confirm Receipt & Update Status** | Ask the client to confirm receipt and indicate whether they accept, modify, or decline the renewal. | Closing the loop ensures you know the outcome and can act on any changes promptly. | • Mark the task as “Completed” once you receive a response. <br>• Update the client’s record with the new policy term, premium, and any endorsements. |
| **7. Archive Expired Policies** | Move policies that have lapsed or been cancelled to an “Archive” folder, preserving them for compliance audits. | Retaining historical data satisfies regulatory requirements and provides a reference for future cross‑selling. | • Tag archived items with `#expired‑YYYY`. <br>• Keep a summary index of all archived policies for quick lookup. |

### Quick Checklist (Copy‑Paste into NeuroWorks)

```
- [ ] Create / update client profile
- [ ] Log latest interaction
- [ ] Add renewal reminder (30‑45 days before expiry)
- [ ] Review claim history & life‑event updates
- [ ] Send renewal package (quote + docs)
- [ ] Confirm receipt & record client decision
- [ ] Archive old policy (if applicable)
```

### Why This Works
- **Consistency**: A single vault location and standardized tags keep data uniform.  
- **Proactivity**: Automated reminders give you a head‑start on renewals, reducing missed opportunities.  
- **Compliance**: Detailed logs and archived records meet typical industry audit standards (e.g., NAIC guidelines) [1].
