### 1. Scope of Microsoft 365 Support (Consumer vs. Business)

| Customer type | Product examples | Account type | How support is delivered |
|---------------|------------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Home / Consumer** | Microsoft 365 Personal, Family, Premium, Basic; one‑time Office purchases (Home & Student, Home & Business, Professional) | Personal Microsoft account | Self‑service help center; limited live‑chat/phone options; no admin portal. |
| **Business / Enterprise** | Microsoft 365 Apps for Business, Business Standard, Enterprise E3/E4/E5, A5, nonprofit plans | Work or school account (admin‑managed) | Admin can sign in to the Microsoft 365 admin center and open tickets on behalf of users; broader live‑chat, phone, and on‑site options. |

*Source: Microsoft 365 Customer Service and Support page*【1】.

---

### 2. Key Contractual Clauses to Review

| Clause | What to Look For | Why It Matters |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Support Level / SLA** | Definition of “business product” vs. “home product”; response time guarantees; availability of phone vs. chat. | Determines the service level you can promise to internal users or external clients. |
| **Admin‑Only Access** | Language that only an admin can open support tickets for business customers. | Impacts who in your organization can trigger a support request; may require internal process documentation. |
| **Subscription vs. Perpetual License** | Distinguish rights for Microsoft 365 subscriptions (ongoing support) vs. one‑time Office purchases (limited support). | Affects renewal/termination clauses and cost modeling. |
| **Third‑Party Discount Programs** | Mention of “Microsoft Workplace Discount Program” for office purchases through a company benefit. | May trigger additional compliance or reporting obligations. |
| **Data Privacy / Security** | Any references to data handling during support interactions (especially for enterprise plans). | Must align with your organization’s privacy policy and any GDPR/CCPA commitments. |

---

### 3. Action Checklist for Logan

1. **Identify the product tier** (home vs. business) for the user(s) in question.  
2. **Confirm the account type** (personal vs. work/school) to know who can open a support ticket.  
3. **Map the required support level** to the appropriate SLA clause in the Microsoft 365 agreement.  
4. **Verify any discount‑program language** if the license was obtained via a corporate benefit.  
5. **Document any data‑privacy considerations** that arise from the support process.
