<a id="executive-summary"></a>## Executive summary

Zimbabwe’s five most visible “market presence” banks for mass retail—__CBZ, FBC, POSB, Stanbic Zimbabwe, and ZB__—compete for Gen Z \(≈13–29\) primarily through __\(a\) youth/student account packaging__, __\(b\) low\-friction onboarding \(KYC\-lite \+ digital onboarding\)__, and __\(c\) high\-frequency payments ecosystems__ \(USSD/app payments, merchant payments, school fees, airtime and bill pay\)\. [\[1\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

From an AFC standpoint, the biggest competitive threat is __distribution \+ onboarding speed__\. ZB and POSB have extremely large agency footprints \(RBZ: __ZB 1,282 agencies; POSB 588 agencies__\) and can win Gen Z by proximity and low cost\-to\-serve\. [\[2\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf) Meanwhile, FBC and Stanbic win disproportionate Gen Z share by making onboarding simpler through __USSD \+ WhatsApp \+ app__ \(FBC\) and __KYC\-lite low\-cost accounts__ \(Stanbic BluEase\)\. [\[3\]](https://www.fbc.co.zw/banking/about-us/faqs/fbc-digital-boarding-faqs) CBZ competes through a strong __life\-stage youth funnel__ \(Junior → Teen → Student Visa\) and a broad “lifestyle” digital stack \(CBZ Touch \+ USSD \*230\# \+ QR payments via CBZ Pay\)\. [\[4\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-junior-account/)

AFC’s advantage is that it already has a meaningful physical footprint and an agriculture\-linked strategy identity: RBZ describes AFC’s transformation \(Agribank → AFC Commercial Bank\) and mandate as a “one stop shop” for agriculture\-sector services; RBZ also lists AFC with __46 branches and 150 agencies__\. [\[5\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf) AFC can compete for Gen Z by \(1\) matching low\-friction onboarding and daily payments experiences and \(2\) differentiating with a Gen Z proposition tied to agriculture\-linked livelihoods and rural/peri\-urban youth enterprises, delivered via app \+ USSD\. [\[6\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

<a id="assumptions-and-methodology"></a>## Assumptions and methodology

Gen Z is assumed as __≈13–29__ \(teens, high school/college/university students, early\-career employees, and young entrepreneurs\)\. The analysis focuses only on Gen Z\-relevant capabilities: __youth/student products, digital wallets, cards, onboarding/KYC, and access channels__, plus partnerships that expand youth utility \(telcos, card schemes, remittance rails\)\. [\[7\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-junior-account/)

“Market presence” is treated as the user\-provided bank set and is supported with RBZ’s consolidated licensed\-institution data including __ownership and branch/agency footprint as at 31 Dec 2025__\. [\[8\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

Where “size” is requested, the report uses the latest publicly available __audited financial results/annual reports__ from official sources \(ZSE PDFs, bank annual reports\) for total assets\. [\[9\]](https://www.zse.co.zw/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/CBZ-Holdings-Audited-Financial-Results-FY.2024-3-files-merged.pdf)

<a id="bank-by-bank-gen-z-competitive-findings"></a>## Bank\-by\-bank Gen Z competitive findings

__CBZ Bank__

Institutional profile: RBZ lists CBZ Bank as __100% owned by CBZ Holdings__, with __37 branches__ and __20 agencies \+ 78 agency banking__\. [\[10\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf) CBZ Holdings’ audited consolidated results for FY2024 report __total assets of ZWG 34\.42bn__ and __total deposits of ZWG 21\.59bn__, indicating sector\-leading scale\. [\[11\]](https://www.zse.co.zw/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/CBZ-Holdings-Audited-Financial-Results-FY.2024-3-files-merged.pdf)

Gen Z products and features: CBZ’s youth lifecycle packaging is explicit: __CashPlus Junior__ for children under 12 \(parent/guardian\-operated\) and __CashPlus Teen__ for teenagers \(with SMS alerts, ATM/POS debit card, and CBZ Touch access\)\. [\[12\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-junior-account/) For older Gen Z and students, CBZ offers a __Student Visa Gold Card__ \(USD\-denominated Visa card for students studying locally and abroad\), and it positions balances as visible via CBZ Touch for customers with CBZ accounts\. [\[13\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/cbz-student-visa-gold-card/) CBZ also markets “youth lifestyle payments” through __CBZ Pay__, which links Visa card holders via CBZ Touch app and __USSD \*230\#__, enabling payments via QR and USSD\. [\[14\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/cbz-pay-2/)

Onboarding/KYC experience: CBZ’s inclusion pathway is strongly “branch/agent\-first but fast”: its __SmartCash Account__ can be opened by walking into a CBZ branch or agent with an ID and opening deposit, and the bank claims the account can be opened “within 5 minutes\.” [\[15\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/smartcash-account/) CBZ Touch is also available via __USSD \*230\#__, lowering digital barriers for non\-smartphone and low\-data customers\. [\[16\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/notice-to-our-valued-clients/)

Channels: CBZ Touch is positioned as the integrated mobile platform for balance checks and transfers, including transfers to wallet accounts such as Telecash\. [\[17\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/cbz-touch-mobile-banking/) CBZ supports merchant acquiring through POS terminals that accept payments from all banks on the __ZimSwitch__ platform and on __Visa and MasterCard/Maestro__ rails\. [\[18\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/pos-point-sale/)
