<a id="executive-summary-and-assumptions"></a>## Executive summary and assumptions

This report provides a __Gen Z–only competitor analysis__ of five Zimbabwe banks with major market presence—__CBZ, FBC, POSB, Stanbic Zimbabwe, and ZB__—from __AFC Bank \(formerly Agribank\)__’s perspective \(current as of __March 2026__\)\. Gen Z is assumed as __≈ ages 13–29__ \(including teens, students, first\-jobbers, and young entrepreneurs\)\.

Across the five banks, the __winning Gen Z playbook__ is consistent: \(1\) __named youth/student accounts__ with clear benefits and low fees; \(2\) __low\-friction onboarding__ \(KYC\-lite products, app/USSD onboarding, branch/agent onboarding\) and “always\-on” channels; \(3\) __transaction\-centric ecosystems__—merchant payments \(POS/ZIPIT Smart/QR\), bill payments \(especially __school fees__\), and mobile money/wallet linkages; and \(4\) __big\-distribution economics__ through __agencies and agent banking__ to lower cost\-to\-serve while expanding reach\. [\[1\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

Key competitive takeaways for AFC Gen Z growth: \- __CBZ__ is strongest on __youth lifecycle packaging \+ integrated digital app__ \(CBZ Touch \+ USSD\) including school/tertiary fees and in\-app wallet capabilities\. [\[2\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-junior-account/)  
\- __FBC__ is strongest on __digital onboarding \+ USSD \+ WhatsApp__ journeys and student banking positioning \(Scholar account \+ Mobile Moola\)\. [\[3\]](https://www.fbc.co.zw/banking/retail-sme-banking/transactional-accounts)  
\- __POSB__ is strongest on __KYC\-lite youth acquisition \+ deep inclusion behaviors__ \(youth account \+ student \+ informal traders\), and is increasingly “platform\-led” \(group savings \+ outbound remittance partnerships\)\. [\[4\]](https://www.posb.co.zw/youth-account/)  
\- __Stanbic__ is strongest on __remote/assisted KYC\-lite onboarding__ \(BluEase with “ID copy \+ selfie video” workflow\) and a clean digital “ways\-to\-bank” stack \(USSD \+ app\)\. [\[5\]](https://digital.stanbicbank.co.zw/open-an-account/bluease-kyc-lite-account)  
\- __ZB__ is strongest on __youth segmentation \(Junior Stash \+ Stash 16–26\)__ plus “wallet \+ agent reach” and broad merchant rails \(POS \+ e\-wallet works across all major MNOs\)\. [\[6\]](https://www.zb.co.zw/banking/accounts/individual-accounts/junior-stash-account)

<a id="gen-z-market-lens-and-evidence-base"></a>## Gen Z market lens and evidence base

<a id="Xefd3a38c5d934dfa0e6df533cf1c1ae333809cc"></a>### Why Gen Z acquisition is shaped by informality and low\-cost rails

ZimStat’s Economic Census preliminary reporting emphasizes that __micro and SME establishments are predominantly informal__, with only a minority of micro establishments formal—reinforcing why Gen Z banking success in Zimbabwe favors __KYC\-lite/on\-the\-go onboarding__ and services that match irregular incomes and informal trade realities\. [\[7\]](https://zimstat.co.zw/economic-census-preliminary-report/)

<a id="trust-and-consumer-assurance"></a>### Trust and consumer assurance

Zimbabwe’s __Deposit Protection Corporation \(DPC\)__ notes that membership includes __all commercial banks and POSB \(among other deposit\-taking institutions\)__ regulated/licensed under relevant Acts, and that deposits with asset managers are not covered—useful for Gen Z trust messaging \(especially for first\-time depositors\)\. [\[8\]](https://dpcorp.co.zw/member-banks/)

<a id="X52c5c24bac6c3f88b0defccbeb701f20f7d41a9"></a>### Payments interoperability as a competitive enabler

RBZ designated __ZimSwitch__ as a national payment switch \(regulatory designation\), and ZimSwitch positions itself as the __sole national electronic funds switch and clearing house__—explaining why banks compete hard on ZIPIT/merchant acceptance and interoperable payments experiences that Gen Z uses daily\. [\[9\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/press/July/00100_-Press-Statement---Zimswitch-Designation-as-National-Switch---9-7-2020-2.pdf)

<a id="bank-by-bank-gen-z-competitive-profiles"></a>## Bank\-by\-bank Gen Z competitive profiles

<a id="cbz-bank"></a>### CBZ Bank

__Institutional profile \(ownership, size, footprint\)__  
RBZ lists CBZ as __100% owned by CBZ Holdings__, with __37 branches__, __20 agencies and 78 agency banking__, and __1,094 employees__ \(as at 31 Dec 2025\)\. [\[10\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf) CBZ’s Integrated Annual Report highlights __total assets of ZWG 34\.42 billion__ \(FY ended 31 Dec 2024\), indicating leading scale\. [\[11\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/cbzh-integrated-annual-report-for-the-year-ended-31-december-2024/)

__Gen Z–targeted products and features__  
CBZ runs a highly visible youth lifecycle suite:  
\- __CashPlus Junior__: savings account for __children under 12__ operated by parent/guardian\. [\[12\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-junior-account/)  
\- __CashPlus Teen__: teen savings account with __SMS alerts__, __ATM/POS debit card__, and __CBZ Touch mobile banking__ access\. [\[13\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/banking/cashplus-teen-account/)  
\- __CBZ Student Visa Gold Card__: USD\-denominated Visa card positioned for students studying locally/abroad\. [\[14\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/cbz-student-visa-gold-card/)  
CBZ also pushes “youth\-relevant utility features” through CBZ Touch, such as __school and tertiary fees payments__ and other everyday services\. [\[15\]](https://www.cbz.co.zw/ebanking-services/cbz-touch-mobile-banking/)
