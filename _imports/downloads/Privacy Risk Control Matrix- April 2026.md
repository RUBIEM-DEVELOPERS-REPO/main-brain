PRIVC01

Policy

The enablers of a strong Privacy programme are in place e\.g\. Clear rules \(Policy\), Accountability, Skilled adequate resource and a Specialist community\.   

__Policy__  
__POSB responsibilities:__  
1\) Privacy Management Policy exists, is approved by POSB Risk and Compliance Committee, is communicated to the POSB privacy community, and is made available to all employees through POSB Policy Portal\.  
2\) Policy is reviewed at least annually\.  
3\) Policy is approved by the ExCo owner when there are significant changes to it\.   
4\) Upon changes to the policy, the Privacy Programme is reviewed and updated accordingly\.

  
__POSB responsibilities:__  
1\) Policy is rolled out across the bank in line with the POSB policy governance process\.

  
2\) Upon changes to the policy, the POSB Privacy Programme is reviewed and updated accordingly\.

  
3\) Any exceptions or variations from POSB Policy are justified by documented, compelling reasons, have been approved in line with the process set out within the Policy compliance in POSB document and are documented within the POSB Policy Portal\.

PRIVC02

Privacy Roles and Responsibilities

The enablers of a strong Privacy programme are in place e\.g\. Clear rules \(Policy\), Accountability, Skilled adequate resource and a Specialist community\.   

__Privacy Roles and Responsibilities\.__ __Privacy Roles and Responsibilities__ The following roles must exist and be operational, supported by documented role descriptions and evidence of appointment: 

  
__POSB responsibilities__   
1a\) __POSB Policy Owner \-__ Responsible for defining Risk Tolerance and overseeing the implementation of the Policy across POSB\.

1b\) __POSB Board Sponsor __\- Member of the POSB Executive Committee, appointed by the POSB CEO with accountability for effective POSB implementation of the Privacy Policy\.

  
2\) __POSB Privacy Officer__ \- Responsible for independent day to day leadership, management, oversight, and monitoring of the implementation of the Policy across POSB\. Supported by sufficient resources, including budget for second line activities, privacy counsel and sufficient number of privacy managers relative to workload\. Suitable professional qualification \(e\.g\. IAPP CIPP/PM\) or work experience\.

  
3\) __Privacy Counsel__ \- Responsible for providing expert legal support to the POSB Privacy Programme\.

4\) __Privacy and Security SteerCo__ \- brings together Privacy, Security and relevant business functions \(at least Commercial, Technology, HR, Enterprise, Finance\)\. Meets at least quarterly\. E band or above representation\. Reviews Programme status, monitors progress against plans, ensures risks are managed and handles escalations\.   
5\) __Privacy Leadership Team __\- Chaired by POSB Privacy Officer, brings together POSB DPOs to ensure consistent implementation of the Policy across POSB divisions\. Meets regularly to agree on joint strategies and plans, share best practices, and to address topical issues\.

PRIVC03

Business Roles and Responsibilities

The enablers of a strong Privacy programme are in place e\.g\. Clear rules \(Policy\), Accountability, Skilled adequate resource and a Specialist community\.   

__Business Roles and Responsibilities \( POSB\)\. __

  
1\) __CEO __has ultimate accountability for compliance and provides an attestation of compliance status to the regulator and to POSB Board ARC at least annually\.

  
2\) __ExCo__ \- For each department with High Risk Processing activities, an ExCo level individual is appointed to be accountable for compliance within his/her department\. This individual participates in Privacy and Security SteerCo, or delegates to at least E\-band representative\. At least annual briefing is delivered to ExCo\. 

__  __  
3\) __High Risk Process/Product Owner__ \- For each High Risk Process/Product, the process/product owner is appointed to be accountable for compliance, including I\) ensuring implementation and effectiveness of SPDA; ii\) supporting the Security and Privacy teams in SPDA and other compliance processes; iii\) ensuring any control deficiencies are addressed appropriately; iv\) support with potentially needed escalations and risk approvals; and v\) support with provision of annual tailored training to high risk teams\. Each High Risk Team receives tailored training at least annually\.  

  
4\) __SPOC__ \- ExCo and/or Process/Product owners should identify Privacy SPOCs to support with these responsibilities\. SPOCs receive tailored training at least annually\.

PRIVC04

Risk Management

Risks are logged and managed at appropriate level of seniority\.  

Risk Management

__POSB Responsibilities:__

1\) Communication of the bank’s Risk Tolerance to POSB ExCo at least annually\. 

2\) Risk Register, consisting of Tier 2 and Tier 3 Risks, in particular: 

a\) Tier 2: Existing or potential non\-compliances, other control deficiencies or other legal, reputational or policy related privacy risks, typically arising from 

 significant control deficiencies against Policy and POSB Baseline Privacy Requirements; 

b\) Tier 3: Risks arising from i\) changes in legal and regulatory environment; ii\) changes in societal and customer expectations and attitudes; iii\) use of new or emerging technologies; iv\) new business initiatives; v\) changes in legal and regulatory environment; and/or vi\) other such significant risks\. 

c\) Tier 3: Operational issues which cannot be managed by the project, process or product team and therefore need to be elevated to more senior decision making and risk management; 

3\) Operational Issue Tracker \(management of various operational issues to prevent them from becoming risk\)
