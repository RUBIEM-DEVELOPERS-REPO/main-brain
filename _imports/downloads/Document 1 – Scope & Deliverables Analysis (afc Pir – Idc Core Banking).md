Document 1: Scope and Deliverables Analysis
1. Engagement Overview
This   engagement   relates   to   a  Post-Implementation Review (PIR)  of   the  IDC Core Banking System,
commissioned   by   AFC   Holdings.   The   assignment   requires   an   independent,   evidence-based   assurance
review covering technology, operations, risk, compliance, and benefits realisation following system go-live.
The review extends beyond a diagnostic assessment and explicitly includes implementation support and
independent validation of remediation actions prior to assignment closure,   making   this   both   an
assurance and limited oversight engagement.
2. Scope of Work Analysis
The scope defined in the TOR covers ten (10) integrated review domains:
2.1 Core System Performance and Stability
Assessment of effectiveness, reliability, availability, scalability, and resilience of the IDC Core Banking
System post go-live
Review of system performance against agreed SLAs and operational benchmarks
2.2 Requirements and Benefits Realisation
Validation of alignment between implemented functionality and approved business, functional, and
technical requirements
Assessment of achievement of intended business benefits and success criteria
2.3 Technical Architecture and Infrastructure
Review of system architecture, hosting environment, integration points, and infrastructure adequacy
Assessment of performance, capacity, failover, and disaster recovery readiness
2.4 Data Migration and Data Integrity
Validation of data migration accuracy, completeness, and reconciliation processes
Assessment of data integrity controls and auditability
2.5 Information Security and Access Controls
Review of cybersecurity controls, logical access management, segregation of duties, and security
configuration
Assessment of compliance with internal policies and regulatory requirements

2.6 Operational Readiness and Change Management
Evaluation of user adoption levels and training effectiveness
Assessment of change management practices and operational preparedness
2.7 Operating and Support Model
Review of incident, problem, change, and service management processes
Assessment of post-go-live support effectiveness and escalation procedures
2.8 Vendor Performance
Evaluation of vendor performance against contractual obligations and SLAs
Assessment of issue resolution effectiveness and delivery quality

Identification and documentation of key risks, control gaps, issues, and lessons learned
2.10 Remediation and Optimisation
Development of practical, prioritised remediation and optimisation recommendations
Independent validation of remediation actions prior to closure
3. Deliverables Analysis
The TOR specifies five mandatory deliverables:
3.1 Comprehensive PIR Report
Detailed findings across all scope areas
Root cause analysis and evidence-based conclusions
Actionable and prioritised recommendations
3.2 Executive Summary (Board and Senior Management)
High-level summary of key findings, material risks, and critical actions
Board-ready presentation format
3.3 Risk-Rated Issues and Recommendations Matrix
Risk classification by severity, impact, urgency, and ownership
Alignment to AFC risk management practices
3.4 High-Level Remediation and Optimisation Roadmap
Sequenced remediation actions
Timelines, dependencies, and accountable stakeholders
