AFC Bank Product Innovation Strategy for Gen Z
and Pensioners
Introduction
This document presents a detailed strategy for new banking products that AFC Bank (formerly Agribank)
can introduce for two key segments: Gen Z (18–35) and  Pensioners. The recommendations are based
on a comparative analysis of Zimbabwean banking institutions and identified gaps in the current market.
The research reviewed products offered by commercial banks, building societies, and microfinance
institutions. The analysis shows that most banks focus on traditional offerings such as student accounts,
personal loans, and pension accounts, while several innovative opportunities remain underserved. 
The objective of this strategy is to design high‑impact, easy‑to‑implement products that align with AFC
Bank's development mandate and agricultural heritage.
SECTION 1: GEN Z PRODUCT OPPORTUNITIES
1. AFC Hustler Account
Description
A low‑fee digital account designed for freelancers, informal traders, gig workers, and young entrepreneurs.
How the Product Works
Users open the account digitally with simplified KYC requirements. Transactions from mobile money, POS
payments, and bank transfers create a financial activity record. This activity is then used to build a credit
profile that qualifies the user for micro‑loans.
Customer Pain Points Solved
Lack of formal income proof
Limited access to credit
Difficulty opening traditional bank accounts
Improvements
AI‑based transaction credit scoring
Integration with mobile wallets
Built‑in bookkeeping dashboard

5. AFC RentFlex
Description
A financing product allowing young professionals to pay rent monthly instead of annually.
How the Product Works
The bank pays the landlord upfront and the tenant repays the bank monthly through payroll deductions or
mobile payments.
Customer Pain Points
Landlords demanding 6–12 months rent upfront
Limited access to rental financing
