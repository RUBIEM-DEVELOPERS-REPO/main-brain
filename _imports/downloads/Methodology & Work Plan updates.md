1. Introduction and Methodological Philosophy
Rubiem adopts a risk-based, evidence-driven Post-Implementation Review (PIR) methodology that has
been   successfully   applied   across   banking   and   financial   services   institutions   in   Africa.   Our   approach   is
designed   to   provide   AFC   Holdings   with  independent   assurance,    practical   insights,   and  actionable
recommendations, while ensuring full alignment with regulatory expectations, industry best practice, and
AFC’s internal governance frameworks.
The methodology deliberately goes beyond a compliance checklist. It focuses on: - Whether the IDC Core
Banking System is  fit for purpose  - Whether it is  stable, secure, and sustainable  in live operations -
Whether   the  intended   business   benefits  are   being   realised   -   Whether   residual   risks   are   understood,
controlled, and actively managed
Given   the   TOR   requirement   that   the   assignment   is   only   complete   once   remediation   actions   are
independently validated, our methodology integrates assessment, remediation planning, and validation
into a single coherent delivery framework.
2. Guiding Principles
The methodology is underpinned by the following principles:
Independence and Objectivity – All findings are evidence-based and independently validated.
Risk-Based Focus – Review effort is prioritised on high-risk, high-impact areas such as system
stability, data integrity, security, and regulatory compliance.
Business and Technology Alignment – Equal emphasis is placed on technology performance and
business outcomes.
Practicality – Recommendations are realistic, prioritised, and implementable within AFC’s operating
context.
Regulatory Awareness – Consideration of local and regional banking regulatory expectations.
3. Detailed Methodology by Phase
Phase 1: Initiation and Planning
Objective: Establish a clear, shared understanding of scope, expectations, and success criteria.
Key Activities:  - Formal project kick-off with AFC project sponsor and key stakeholders - Confirmation of
scope,   timelines,   reporting   lines,   and   governance   -   Review   of   key   documentation   including   approved
requirements, system design, data migration artefacts, vendor contracts, SLAs, and regulatory obligations -
Development of a detailed review plan and evidence request list

Phase 2: Current-State Assessment (Technology, Data, Security)
Objective: Assess the stability, performance, and control environment of the IDC Core Banking System post
go-live.
Key   Activities:  -   Review   of   system   performance,   availability,   scalability,   and   resilience   -   Assessment   of
infrastructure,   hosting,   and   integrations   -   Validation   of   data   migration   accuracy,   completeness,   and
reconciliation - Review of cybersecurity, access management, and segregation of duties
Key Outputs: - Technical and data assessment findings - Preliminary risk register
Phase 3: Business, Operations, and Vendor Assessment
Objective: Evaluate business adoption, operational readiness, and vendor delivery.
Key   Activities:  -   Functional   performance   review   -   User   adoption   and   training   assessment   -   Review   of
operational support processes - Vendor performance evaluation against SLAs
Key Outputs: - Consolidated business and operational findings
Phase 4: Analysis, Reporting, and Remediation Planning
Objective: Translate findings into actionable management and Board outputs.
Key Activities: - Root cause analysis - Risk rating and prioritisation - Development of PIR report, executive
summary, and remediation roadmap
Key Outputs: - Final PIR Report and Executive Summary
Phase 5: Implementation Support and Independent Validation
Objective: Independently validate remediation actions prior to closure.
Key Activities: - Tracking and validation of remediation actions - Evidence review and sign-off
Key Outputs: - Implementation Support and Validation Report
