 BANKING & FINANCE QUIZ
What does NPL stand for in banking? A. Net Profit Loss B. Non-Performing Loan C. National Payment
Ledger D. Net Portfolio Liability Answer: B. Non-Performing Loan
What is the primary function of a central bank? A. Issue passports B. Print money and regulate the
financial system C. Manage stock markets D. Provide retail loans Answer: B. Print money and
regulate the financial system
What is a suspense account used for? A. Long-term investments B. Temporary holding of unclear
transactions C. Paying salaries D. Recording profits only Answer: B. Temporary holding of unclear
transactions
Which of these is a liability for a bank? A. Customer deposits B. Bank building C. ATMs D. Furniture
Answer: A. Customer deposits
What is the meaning of liquidity in finance? A. Company profits B. How easily an asset can be
converted to cash C. Loan interest rate D. Employee salaries Answer: B. How easily an asset can be
converted to cash
In banking, KYC stands for: A. Know Your Customer B. Keep Your Credit C. Know Your Capital D. Key
Yearly Compliance Answer: A. Know Your Customer
Which of these is a short-term funding instrument? A. Treasury bill B. Corporate bond (10-year) C.
Mortgage D. Land purchase Answer: A. Treasury bill
What does ATM stand for? A. Automated Teller Machine B. Automatic Transaction Method C. Account
Transfer Machine D. Automated Tax Mechanism Answer: A. Automated Teller Machine
Which of the following is not a type of bank account? A. Savings account B. Current account C.
Investment account D. Liability account Answer: D. Liability account
What is compound interest? A. Interest only on the principal B. Interest on principal and previously
earned interest C. A flat monthly fee D. Tax on loans Answer: B. Interest on principal and previously
earned interest
Who regulates commercial banks in Zimbabwe? A. Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe B. Ministry of Finance
C. IMF D. Zimbabwe Stock Exchange Answer: A. Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe
What is a credit score used for? A. To measure loan eligibility B. To track ATM withdrawals C. To
record deposits only D. To measure stock market trends Answer: A. To measure loan eligibility
Which type of loan is typically backed by property? A. Personal loan B. Mortgage C. Payday loan D.
Overdraft Answer: B. Mortgage

Which of these is a digital payment method? A. Cash B. Cheque C. Mobile money D. Promissory note
Answer: C. Mobile money
What does FDIC (US context) provide? A. Health insurance B. Deposit insurance C. Loan guarantees
D. Tax collection Answer: B. Deposit insurance
Which is a long-term financing source for companies? A. Bank overdraft B. Share capital C. Trade
credit D. Short-term loan Answer: B. Share capital
Which of the following is a risk for banks? A. Interest rate risk B. Credit risk C. Operational risk D. All
of the above Answer: D. All of the above
What is a capital adequacy ratio? A. Ratio of bank profits to losses B. Bank’s capital compared to its
risk-weighted assets C. Shareholder equity divided by revenue D. Loan-to-value ratio Answer: B.
Bank’s capital compared to its risk-weighted assets
Which is an example of retail banking? A. Corporate loans B. Treasury management C. Savings
account for individuals D. Government bonds Answer: C. Savings account for individuals
What is the main role of investment banks? A. Accept deposits B. Facilitate mergers, acquisitions, and
capital raising C. Issue savings accounts D. Provide ATM services Answer: B. Facilitate mergers,
acquisitions, and capital raising
What does SWIFT stand for? A. Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication B.
Standard Worldwide International Fund Transfer C. Secure Wire International Fund Transfer D.
Standardized Wire Interbank Finance Transaction Answer: A. Society for Worldwide Interbank
Financial Telecommunication
What is the main feature of a demand deposit? A. Can be withdrawn anytime B. Fixed maturity C.
High interest rate D. Used only for corporate clients Answer: A. Can be withdrawn anytime
Which is a primary function of the stock market? A. Collect taxes B. Enable buying and selling of
shares C. Print money D. Manage loans Answer: B. Enable buying and selling of shares
What does inflation mean? A. Increase in the money supply B. Increase in general price levels over
time C. Decrease in bank deposits D. Government deficit Answer: B. Increase in general price levels
over time
Which type of bank account usually earns higher interest? A. Current account B. Savings account C.
Fixed deposit D. Checking account Answer: C. Fixed deposit
What is overdraft facility? A. Short-term loan linked to current account B. Investment in stocks C.
Bank charges D. Tax deduction scheme Answer: A. Short-term loan linked to current account
Which of the following is a non-banking financial institution (NBFI)? A. Commercial bank B. Insurance
company C. Central bank D. Reserve bank Answer: B. Insurance company
