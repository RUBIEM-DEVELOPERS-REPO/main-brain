Zimbabwe 2.0: AI for National Transformation 2026 
 “Leveraging AI for Innovation, Policy, and Inclusive National Development” 
1. Introduction 
Zimbabwe  is  emerging  as  a  digitally  capable  nation,  ready  to  harness  artificial  intelligence  for  sustainable 
economic  growth.  The  country’s telecommunications   penetration has   expanded   significantly,   creating 
connectivity  that  enables  digital  transformation across  urban  and  rural  areas. Innovation  hubs  at  universities 
and research institutions are  generating AI-ready talent and solutions, while national programs in healthcare, 
agriculture,  finance,  and  public  services increasingly  require  AI  integration  to  optimize  efficiency  and  impact. 
The informal economy, which forms a substantial part of Zimbabwe’s GDP, stands to benefit from AI-enabled 
solutions for market intelligence, logistics, and financial inclusion. 
 
With  these  developments,  Zimbabwe  is AI-ready, but  scaling  up  requires  strategic  investment  in national  AI 
infrastructure, high-performance compute, data readiness (public and private), skills development, and funding 
mechanisms.  Leveraging  AI  effectively  will  not  only  enhance  domestic  economic  growth but  also  strengthen 
Zimbabwe’s role  within  SADC  and  the  broader  African  AI  ecosystem,  enabling  regional  leadership  in  digital 
innovation, data governance, and AI-driven competitiveness. 
 
2. Preamble. 
Artificial  intelligence  represents  a  transformative  force  capable  of  accelerating  Zimbabwe’s  long-term 
development  aspirations.  Anchored  in Vision  2030,  the  country  has  defined  ambitious  targets  for  economic 
diversification,  innovation,  and  inclusivity. NDS2 operationalizes  these  ambitions,  identifying  priority  sectors 
where AI can enhance productivity and governance. 
 
The adoption of the National AI Policy provides a robust framework for ethical, accountable, and strategic AI 
deployment across public and private sectors. Complementing this framework, Zimbabwe has achieved progress 
in  data  protection  laws, ensuring  citizen  privacy  and  trust  in  digital  systems,  and  strengthened cybersecurity 
measures to  safeguard  national  digital  infrastructure.  These  foundational  elements  position  the  country  to 
adopt AI at scale while minimizing risks and ensuring alignment with national development objectives. 
 
3. Purpose of the Conference 
The Zimbabwe 2.0: AI for National Transformation 2026 conference aims to: 
1. Promote Zimbabwe’s readiness for AI adoption and identify strategies to scale deployment across sectors. 
2. Foster multi-sectoral collaboration to implement Vision 2030, NDS2 priorities, and the National AI Policy 
through AI-driven initiatives. 
 

                                                                                                    
3. Objectives 
3.1. Assess Zimbabwe’s AI readiness, including digital infrastructure, telecom coverage, innovation hubs, 
and sectoral preparedness. 
3.2. Explore strategies to build national AI infrastructure, including AI compute capacity and platforms for 
high-performance AI workloads. 
3.3. Identify methods for preparing public and private data for AI adoption, ensuring quality, accessibility, 
and ethical use. 
3.4. Discuss solutions for skills, capacity building, and knowledge transfer to support national AI initiatives. 
3.5. Examine funding mechanisms and investment strategies to accelerate AI deployment  
3.6. Showcase AI innovations and research outputs from universities, startups, and industry 
3.7. Promote AI inclusive growth, ensuring noone and no place is left behind   
3.8. Highlight Zimbabwe’s progress in data protection, cybersecurity, and digital infrastructure 
3.9. Explore sector-specific AI applications in health, agriculture, finance, education, and public services. 
 
4. Areas to be Covered  
4.1. AI in Agriculture, Food Security,  Healthcare and  Public Health 
4.2. Mining, Industry, Financial services and Inclusive Digital Economy 
4.3. Education, Skills Development and AI Capacity Building 
4.4. Digital Infrastructure and National AI Platforms 
4.5. Data Governance, Ethics and Policy 
4.6. Cybersecurity and AI-Enabled Risk Management 
4.7. Innovation, Entrepreneurship and AI Startups 
4.8. Sustainable Development and  Climate Action 
 
5. Who Should Attend 
The conference targets: 
 Government policymakers and regulators shaping technology, innovation, and development agendas. 
 Captains of Industry and professionals  
 Academia and researchers advancing AI knowledge, applications, and policy. 
 Technology entrepreneurs and startups driving AI innovation. 
 Civil society organizations advocating for ethical, inclusive AI. 
 International partners and investors supporting Zimbabwe’s AI ecosystem. 
 
6. Event Details 
6.1. Dates:  May 2026 
6.2. Venue: Nyanga  
7. Conference Organisers – Zimpapers and the Artificial Intelligence Institute Africa (Aiia)
