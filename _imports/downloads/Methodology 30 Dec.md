IDC   CBS Review –Methodology 
30 Dec 2025

1.Evaluation of the overall effectiveness, performance, and reliability of the IDC Core Banking System following go-live.
2.Assess project objectives, functional and technical requirements, success criteria, and expected business benefits achievement
3.Review stability, availability, scalability, performance, security and compliance with applicable regulatory requirements.
4.Assess system performance, validation of data migration, completeness, reconciliation processes, and overall data integrity.
5.Evaluate user adoption levels, and effectiveness of end-user training, change management, and AFC operational readiness 
6.Review operating and support model, including incident management, change management, and service management processes.
7.Assessment of security controls, user access management, duties segregation , and internal policies and regulatory requirements.
8.Evaluation of vendor performance in relation to contractual obligations, deliverables, and agreed service level agreements (SLAs).
9.Identification and documentation of key gaps, issues, risks, and lessons learned arising from the implementation.
10.Development of clear, practical, and prioritized recommendations aimed at remediation, system optimization, performance 
improvement, and future enhancements.
Scope andObjectives 

1.Executive Summary for Senior Management and the Board-Concise, high-level overview highlighting key 
findings, material risks, critical issues, and priority actions.
2.Comprehensive Post-Implementation Review (PIR) Report-Detailed findings, root cause analyses, 
conclusions, and actionable recommendations supported by evidence.
3.Risk-Rated Issues and Recommendations Matrix-Categorization of identified issues by severity, impact, 
urgency, and assigned ownership.
4.High-Level Remediation and Optimization Roadmap-Sequenced remediation and improvement actions, 
including timelines, dependencies, and responsible stakeholders.
5.Implementation Support and Validation Report (Critical Deliverable -Independent, evidence-based 
confirmation that all agreed recommendationsand corrective actions have been fully implemented.
6.Validation testing outcomes, management sign-off, and formal closure of remediated issues.
