AI Tech Forum Zimbabwe 2026 
 “Mastering AI Systems: Building Zimbabwe’s Intelligence Edge” 
 
1. Introduction 
Zimbabwe is entering a new era of technological advancement, with growing AI research, innovation hubs, and 
ICT  infrastructure supporting  education,  industry,  and  government.  Despite  these  gains,  there  are gaps  in  AI 
expertise, computational infrastructure, and advanced algorithm deployment, which limit the country’s ability 
to compete in high-tech industries and AI-driven economic sectors. 
The AI Tech Forum Zimbabwe 2026 is designed for AI experts, researchers, engineers, and technical innovators 
to discuss cutting-edge developments in algorithms, AI models, and infrastructure, and how these can be applied 
to Zimbabwe’s unique economic and industrial challenges. 
This summit will foster knowledge sharing, collaboration, and technical problem-solving, positioning Zimbabwe 
as a regional AI innovation leader while supporting the country’s Vision 2030, SADC integration, and AU digital 
agenda. 
 
2. Preamble. 
Artificial Intelligence is a transformational technology capable of reshaping industries, government services, and 
research  in  Zimbabwe.  The  AI  Tech  Forum  provides  a platform  for  experts to  exchange  technical  knowledge, 
explore emerging AI models, and address the challenges and opportunities specific to Zimbabwe. 
This  forum  aligns  with  national  priorities  including AI  infrastructure  development,  computational  capacity 
expansion,   data   readiness,   and   policy   frameworks, providing   a hands-on,   technical   environment for 
collaboration, experimentation, and building advanced AI solutions for local and regional needs. 
 
3. Purpose of the Conference 
The AI Tech Forum Zimbabwe 2026 conference aims to: 
1. To provide a technical knowledge-sharing environment for AI researchers, engineers, and practitioners 
focused on algorithms, models, and infrastructure. 
2. To explore how AI technologies can be applied to Zimbabwe’s national challenges, strengthen technical 
capacity, and support innovation-driven economic growth. 
 
1. Objectives 
1.1. Examine advanced AI algorithms, models, and architectures and their applications in Zimbabwe. 
1.2. Discuss  the design,  deployment,  and  optimization  of  AI  infrastructure (compute,  storage,  and 
networks). 

                                                                                                    
1.3. Share knowledge on machine learning, deep learning, neural networks, and AI frameworks relevant to 
local industry and research. 
1.4. Explore data  management,  data  readiness,  and  integration  of  public  and  private  datasets for  AI 
applications. 
1.5. Address AI system performance, scalability, and reliability challenges. 
1.6. Promote AI research collaboration between academia, industry, and government. 
1.7. Showcase emerging AI technologies, platforms, and tools 
1.8. Discuss ethics, governance, and AI policy considerations for high-level AI deployment. 
1.9. Build a technical roadmap for Zimbabwe’s AI development and regional competitiveness. 
 
2. Areas to be Covered  
2.1. AI Algorithms and Model Development 
2.2. Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Neural Networks 
2.3. AI Infrastructure: Compute, Storage, and Cloud System 
2.4. Data Readiness: Public and Private Data Integration 
2.5. AI Research Collaboration and Innovation Hubs 
2.6. AI Applications in Industry, Government, and Academia 
2.7. AI System Performance, Optimization, and Scalability 
2.8. Emerging AI Platforms, Tools, and Frameworks 
 
3. Who Should Attend 
 AI researchers, scientists, and data engineers 
 Machine learning and deep learning experts 
 University and research institute AI faculty 
 Industry AI specialists and solution architects 
 Government technology leaders and policy-makers in AI 
 Developers, engineers, and technical consultants 
 Startups and innovators in AI platforms and systems 
 Regional AI technical collaborators and SADC/AU partners 
 
4. Event Details 
4.1. Dates:  May 2026 
4.2. Venue: Nyanga  
 
5. Conference Organisers – Zimpapers and the Artificial Intelligence Institute Africa (Aiia)
