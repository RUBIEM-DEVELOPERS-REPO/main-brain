# 1\. Executive Summary

Artificial Intelligence Institute Africa proposes a practical, blended and implementation\-focused AI training programme for AFC\. The programme is designed to help AFC leadership, management, support functions, branches and technical teams build immediate AI productivity capability while establishing a structured pathway toward formal certification and long\-term organisational adoption\.

The proposed solution combines classroom training, online lessons, practical exercises and post\-implementation support\. This ensures that participants do not only understand AI concepts, but also apply AI tools to AFC\-relevant documents, reporting, communication, decision\-support and operational scenarios\.

- AFC\-specific methodology with examples aligned to productivity, governance, reporting and operational efficiency\.
- Four\-phase delivery model: classroom training, online lessons, practical exercise and post\-implementation support\.
- Cohort\-based work plan covering EXCO, management, support functions, branches and technical participants\.
- Learning pathway from short courses to national qualifications, professional competence certificates and advanced progression\.
- Certification coverage supported by University of Zimbabwe and HEXCO\-aligned programmes\.

# 2\. Understanding of AFC Requirements

AFC requires artificial intelligence training services that are practical, credible, measurable and aligned to organisational adoption\. The technical response therefore prioritises hands\-on learning, role\-specific application, responsible AI use and implementation support\.

The programme recognises that different AFC groups require different levels of exposure and depth\. Executives require strategic, governance and decision\-support use cases; management teams require productivity and reporting use cases; support functions require function\-specific tools; branches require practical customer and operational application; and technical teams require deeper workflow and implementation capability\.

# 3\. Appendix A: Methodology and Work Plan

## 3\.1 One\-Month Blended Methodology

The proposed methodology is structured as a one\-month learning and implementation cycle per cohort\. It moves participants from foundational classroom learning to online reinforcement, hands\-on practical application and post\-implementation support\.
