<a id="executive-summary"></a>## Executive summary

This report analyzes __five Zimbabwe banks with major market presence__—__CBZ, FBC, POSB, Stanbic Zimbabwe, and ZB__—from __AFC Bank \(formerly Agribank\)__’s perspective, focusing on their effectiveness in __Gen Z \(≈13–29\)__ and __pensioner \(retirees receiving pensions\)__ segments, and the digital/partnership strategies that make them competitive in Zimbabwe’s operating environment \(current as of __March 2026__\)\. The analysis uses __Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe \(RBZ\)__ consolidated institution data \(ownership and footprints\), plus __official product pages__ and __latest public financial disclosures__\.

Core findings:

CBZ combines the country’s largest “full\-suite” retail playbook—youth accounts, senior account fee relief, fast KYC\-lite onboarding and merchant acquiring—backed by very large scale \(FY2024 assets __ZWG 34\.42bn__\) and a broad delivery footprint \(__37 branches \+ agencies/agency banking__\)\. [\[1\]](https://www.zse.co.zw/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/CBZ-Holdings-Audited-Financial-Results-FY.2024-3-files-merged.pdf)

FBC relies on “digital onboarding \+ multi\-channel access” and targeted packaging \(especially its __Mobile Moola Scholar__ and __Senior Citizen__ offerings\), creating a strong youth\-to\-adult lifecycle funnel; it also runs merchant/bill payments via __ZIPIT Smart__ and USSD/WhatsApp\. [\[2\]](https://www.fbc.co.zw/banking/retail-sme-banking/transactional-accounts)

POSB differentiates via inclusion at scale—very wide distribution through __agencies__, segment\-specific accounts \(youth, students, informal traders, pensioners/seniors\), and recent platform moves like __Huruyadzo/Inkunzi__ \(digitized “Mukando” group savings\) and __POSB Remit Outbound__ with Mastercard \(a strategic leap into outbound remittance\)\. [\[3\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

Stanbic’s competitive edge is “global\-banked local execution”: it is wholly owned by __Standard Bank Group__, positioned as a universal bank with three business pillars \(CIB, Personal & Business, Wealth\), and it pushes a strong KYC\-lite and digital stack \(__BluEase \+ USSD \*247\# \+ Blue247 app__\)\. Its parent’s disclosures indicate meaningful scale in Zimbabwe \(FY24 __total assets USD 955\.6m__\) and a sizeable digital footprint\. [\[4\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

ZB competes through breadth \(large “service centre \+ agency” reach\), strong pensioner packaging \(__Heritage Account__\), youth/education savings \(Junior Stash \+ Stash Account\), mobile wallet capability \(ZB e‑Wallet\), diaspora channels \(MoneyGram \+ diaspora hub services\), and merchant acquiring\. [\[5\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

Implication for AFC:

AFC’s __physical footprint is materially competitive__ \(RBZ shows __46 branches \+ 150 agencies__, and a mandate as a one\-stop agriculture sector “financial \+ mechanisation \+ insurance” platform\), but the top five banks win by \(a\) __hyper\-targeted product packaging__ for youth and seniors, \(b\) __ultra\-low\-friction onboarding__ and multi\-channel access, and \(c\) __high\-leverage partnerships__ \(remittance networks, payment rails, card schemes\)\. [\[6\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf)

<a id="assumptions-and-methodology"></a>## Assumptions and methodology

Assumptions: Gen Z is defined as __≈13–29__, and “pensioners” refers to retirees receiving pensions \(often via bank accounts tied to pension administrators\)\. “Top 5 by market presence” is treated as the __user\-specified set__ \(CBZ, FBC, POSB, Stanbic Zimbabwe, ZB\), not a computed ranking\. “Market presence” is proxied through __distribution footprint__ \(branches/agencies\), product breadth, and public strategic moves\.

Methodology:  
Institutional profiles \(ownership, regulated status, footprints\) are taken from RBZ’s consolidated banking institutions information “as at 31 December 2025\.” [\[7\]](https://www.rbz.co.zw/documents/bank_sup/Consolidated_Website_Information_-_January_2026.pdf) Product and channel claims are sourced primarily from each bank’s official website pages \(accounts, “ways to bank,” remittances, POS/merchant services\)\. Where needed for “size,” the latest available audited/consolidated financial results or annual reports are used \(ZSE\-hosted or bank\-hosted\)\. [\[8\]](https://www.zse.co.zw/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/CBZ-Holdings-Audited-Financial-Results-FY.2024-3-files-merged.pdf) Depositor protection context is sourced from Deposit Protection Corporation \(DPC\), which states membership is mandatory for all RBZ\-licensed deposit takers and lists commercial banks \(including these five\) and POSB\. [\[9\]](https://dpcorp.co.zw/member-banks/)
