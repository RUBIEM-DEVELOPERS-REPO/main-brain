1. Objective 
The primary objectives of this engagement are to: 
• Conduct an independent and objective assessment of the effectiveness, efficiency, and 
overall stability of the Core Banking System following its implementation. 
• Evaluate the extent to which the implemented solution aligns with approved business 
requirements, regulatory expectations, and industry best practices. 
• Identify any gaps, risks, control deficiencies, performance issues, and opportunities 
for optimization or enhancement. 
• Provide ongoing support to monitor, track, and independently validate the 
implementation of agreed remediation actions and system improvements prior to 
formal closure of the assignment. 
 
2. Scope of Work 
The consultant shall conduct a comprehensive Post-Implementation Review (PIR) of the IDC 
Core Banking System. The scope of work shall include, but not be limited to, the following 
areas: 
1. Evaluation of the overall effectiveness, performance, and reliability of the IDC Core 
Banking System following go-live. 

2. Assessment of the degree to which the project objectives, functional and technical 
requirements, success criteria, and expected business benefits have been achieved. 
3. Review of system stability, availability, scalability, performance, resilience, security 
controls, and compliance with applicable regulatory and supervisory requirements. 
4. Assessment of functional system performance, including validation of data migration 
accuracy, completeness, reconciliation processes, and overall data integrity. 
5. Evaluation of user adoption levels, adequacy and effectiveness of end-user training, 
change management practices, and overall operational readiness of the organization. 
6. Review of the operating and support model, including incident management, problem 
management, change management, and service management processes. 
7. Assessment of information security controls, user access management, segregation of 
duties, and compliance with internal policies and regulatory requirements. 
8. Evaluation of vendor performance in relation to contractual obligations, deliverables, 
and agreed service level agreements (SLAs). 
9. Identification and documentation of key gaps, issues, risks, and lessons learned 
arising from the implementation. 
10. Development of clear, practical, and prioritized recommendations aimed at 
remediation, system optimization, performance improvement, and future 
enhancements. 
 
3. Deliverables 
The consultant shall provide the following deliverables as part of the engagement: 
1. Comprehensive Post-Implementation Review (PIR) Report 
o Detailed findings, root cause analyses, conclusions, and actionable 
recommendations supported by evidence. 
2. Executive Summary for Senior Management and the Board 
o Concise, high-level overview highlighting key findings, material risks, critical 
issues, and priority actions. 
3. Risk-Rated Issues and Recommendations Matrix 
o Categorization of identified issues by severity, impact, urgency, and assigned 
ownership. 
4. High-Level Remediation and Optimization Roadmap 
o Sequenced remediation and improvement actions, including timelines, 
dependencies, and responsible stakeholders. 
5. Implementation Support and Validation Report (Critical Deliverable) 
o Independent, evidence-based confirmation that all agreed recommendations 
and corrective actions have been fully implemented. 
o Validation testing outcomes, management sign-off, and formal closure of 
remediated issues. 
Note: The assignment shall not be deemed complete until all agreed remediation actions have 
been implemented and independently validated by the consultant. 
 
4. Consultant Qualifications and Requirements 
Bidders must demonstrate the following minimum qualifications and capabilities: 
• Availability of a multidisciplinary team with proven expertise in: 

o Core banking systems 
o IT architecture and infrastructure 
o Data migration, reconciliation, and data integrity 
o Cybersecurity and information security 
o Risk management and regulatory compliance 
• Evidence of having successfully completed at least two (2) Core Banking System 
Post-Implementation Reviews or equivalent independent assurance engagements 
within the last three (3) years. 
• Demonstrated hands-on experience with IDC Core Banking platforms (mandatory 
requirement). 
• Minimum of ten (10) years’ proven experience in banking IT environments and/or 
core banking system implementations or upgrades. 
• Submission of company financial statements, along with a high-level project 
methodology, approach, and proposed work plan. 
 
5. Reporting 
The consultant shall report to the designated Project Sponsor and provide regular progress 
updates throughout the engagement. Upon completion, the consultant will formally present 
the final findings, conclusions, and recommendations to management and other relevant 
stakeholders.
