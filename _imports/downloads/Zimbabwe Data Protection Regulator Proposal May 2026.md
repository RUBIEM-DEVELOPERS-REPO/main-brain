# Proposal for the Use of OneTrust and TrustArc by a Data Protection Regulator in Zimbabwe

__Prepared for: __Regulatory Review Purposes  
__Date: __  26 May 2026

## 1\. Executive Summary

This proposal outlines how the Data Protection Regulator, the Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe \(POTRAZ\) could leverage privacy governance and compliance platforms such as our home grown solution, __AI\-SUPTECH__ to strengthen enforcement of the Cyber and Data Protection Act \[Chapter 12:07\]\.  
  
The proposal focuses on:  
• Regulatory oversight and enforcement capabilities\.  
• Monitoring of the Records of Processing Activities \(ROPA\)\.  
• Data breach management and reporting\.  
• Data Subject Rights \(DSR\) request management\.  
• Assessment of data controllers’ compliance with the eight conditions for lawful processing of personal information\.  
• Identification of compliance gaps in existing data controller operations\.  
• Guidance for data controllers to become audit\-ready\.  
  
The proposal recognises that commercial privacy governance tools such as OneTrust or TrustArc were primarily developed for enterprise compliance management rather than regulator\-led enforcement\. As such, additional supervisory and audit functionality is required for a regulator\-grade compliance ecosystem\.

## 2\. Background and Context

Zimbabwe’s Cyber and Data Protection Act establishes obligations for data controllers and processors regarding the lawful processing of personal information\. The regulator requires mechanisms to:  
• Monitor compliance nationally\.  
• Receive and assess breach notifications\.  
• Review organisational privacy programmes\.  
• Investigate complaints\.  
• Enforce accountability\.  
• Promote transparency and trust in digital systems\.  
  
Traditional compliance assessments are manual, resource\-intensive and reactive\. Modern privacy governance platforms such as OneTrust and TrustArc can significantly improve regulatory visibility and compliance maturity\. We are proposing a home\-grown AI powered solution to automate privacy governance\.

## 3\. Overview of OneTrust and TrustArc

OneTrust and TrustArc are globally recognised privacy governance and compliance platforms\.   
  
OneTrust capabilities include:  
• Data mapping and discovery\.  
• Records of Processing Activities \(ROPA\)\.  
• Privacy Impact Assessments \(PIAs\)\.  
• Data Subject Rights workflows\.  
• Third\-party risk management\.  
• Consent management\.  
• Data breach management\.  
• Policy management\.  
• Vendor risk monitoring\.  
• Cross\-border transfer assessments\.  
  
TrustArc capabilities include:  
• Privacy programme management\.  
• Data inventory and mapping\.  
• Consent and cookie management\.  
• Privacy assessments\.  
• Compliance benchmarking\.  
• DSR fulfilment\.  
• Risk scoring and monitoring\.  
• Certification and accountability frameworks\.  
• Continuous compliance reporting\.  
  
Both platforms can support regulator\-supervised compliance ecosystems when integrated into a national compliance framework\. Our solution combines the capabilities provided by the cited international tools with a proposed POTRAZ specific National compliance Framework to be developed in collaboration with POTRAZ\.

## 4\. Regulatory Use Cases for POTRAZ

4\.1 National Privacy Compliance Registry  
The regulator could require all registered data controllers and processors to maintain validated ROPA submissions through a central supervisory portal integrated with our AI\-SUPTECH’s APIs\.  
  
Benefits:  
• Visibility into all processing activities\.  
• Classification of high\-risk sectors\.  
• Identification of cross\-border data transfers\.  
• Risk\-based supervision\.  
• Sectoral analytics and reporting\.  
  
4\.2 Records of Processing Activities \(ROPA\)  
The regulator could use automated ROPA templates aligned to Zimbabwean legal requirements\.  
  
Regulatory oversight functions:  
• Mandatory periodic submission\.  
• Automated completeness validation\.  
• Detection of missing lawful basis declarations\.  
• Monitoring of retention periods\.  
• Identification of excessive data collection\.  
  
Potential controls:  
• Mandatory annual ROPA attestation\.  
• Automated compliance scoring\.  
• Sector benchmarking\.  
• Audit trail verification\.
