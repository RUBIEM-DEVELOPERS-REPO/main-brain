‘Personal data’ means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (‘data subject’). An identifiable natural person is one who can be
identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier or to one or
more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person;
In other words, this is a very broad definition and includes, without limitation, information such as: 
Directly identifiable information such as name, email address; and/or 
Indirectly identifiable information such as mobile phone number (MSISDN), user or screen name (e.g. jsmith999), IP address (both dynamic and static in
most cases), unique identifiers such IMSI, IMEI (where these can be linked to other information in Vodafone’s possession or control that enables
Vodafone to link such identifiers to an individual) and any other form of unique identifier which enables Vodafone to separate out an individual from a
number of unique identifiers and to know something about and/or interact with the user of that identifier as an individual; and/or 
Information associated with that directly or indirectly identifiable information such as account related information such as address, payment details, CDRs
and information extracted from CDRs such as billing or usage information; and/or 
The private content generated by Vodafone customers and individuals such as cloud storage of contacts, photos, music, communications etc. 
Personal data may relate to Vodafone consumer customers and users, Enterprise users, employees, contractors, candidates, visitors of Vodafone premises etc.
