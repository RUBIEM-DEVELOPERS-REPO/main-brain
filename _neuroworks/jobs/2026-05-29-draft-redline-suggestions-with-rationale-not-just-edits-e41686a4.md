## Contract Red‑Line Suggestions – Sample Guidance  

Below is a structured set of typical red‑line edits a Contracts Reviewer would apply, together with the rationale for each change. These are generic best‑practice recommendations that can be adapted to the specific agreement once the full text is available.

| Clause / Section | Suggested Edit (red‑line) | Rationale |
|-------------------|---------------------------|-----------|
| **Definitions** | • Add ““Effective Date” means the date on which both parties have executed this Agreement.”  <br>• Clarify ambiguous terms (e.g., ““Confidential Information” includes all written, electronic, or oral information disclosed”). | Precise definitions reduce interpretive disputes and ensure all parties share a common understanding of key terms. |
| **Scope of Services** | • Insert “Services shall be performed in accordance with the attached Statement of Work (Exhibit A).”  <br>• Add a limitation: “Any work outside the Scope must be approved in writing and may be billed separately.” | Ties deliverables to a concrete, referenced document and prevents scope creep. |
| **Payment Terms** | • Change “Net 30 days” to “Net 30 days from receipt of a correct invoice.”  <br>• Add late‑payment interest: “Interest shall accrue at 1.5 % per month (or the maximum rate permitted by law) on overdue amounts.” | Clarifies invoicing requirements and provides a deterrent against delayed payments. |
| **Liability** | • Insert a cap: “Liability of either party shall not exceed the total fees paid under this Agreement, except for breaches of confidentiality or willful misconduct.”  <br>• Add indemnity: “Each party shall indemnify the other for third‑party claims arising from its negligence or breach.” | Caps exposure to a predictable amount while preserving protection for serious breaches. |
| **Termination** | • Add convenience termination: “Either party may terminate for convenience upon 30 days’ written notice.”  <br>• Specify effect: “Upon termination, all outstanding fees become immediately due and the receiving party shall return or destroy Confidential Information.” | Provides flexibility to end the relationship without cause and defines post‑termination obligations. |
| **Confidentiality** | • Strengthen: “Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely for performance of this Agreement and shall protect it with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential data, but no less than reasonable care.”  <br>• Add survival: “Confidentiality obligations survive for five (5) years after termination.” | Ensures robust protection and a clear duration for confidentiality duties. |
| **Force Majeure** | • Insert a clause enumerating events (e.g., natural disasters, acts of terrorism, pandemic) and require prompt notice and mitigation efforts. | Provides a balanced mechanism for unforeseeable events that could impede performance. |
| **Governing Law & Dispute Resolution** | • Change “laws of the State of X” to “laws of the State of X, without regard to conflict of law principles.”  <br>• Add arbitration: “Any dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association, seated in City, State.” | Clarifies legal framework and offers a faster, cost‑effective dispute resolution method. |
| **Assignment** | • Restrict: “Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other, except to an affiliate or in connection with a merger/acquisition.” | Prevents unwanted third‑party involvement while allowing reasonable corporate restructuring. |
| **Entire Agreement** | • Insert: “This Agreement, including all exhibits and schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, understandings, and agreements.” | Avoids reliance on prior oral or informal statements. |
| **Signature Block** | • Add title fields (e.g., “Chief Legal Officer”) and date lines for each party. | Ensures proper authority and record‑keeping. |

### How to Apply the Guidance  
1. **Insert the red‑line edits** directly into the contract using track changes or a comparable markup tool.  
2. **Document the rationale** (as shown) in a separate comment column or in the margin, so the negotiating counterpart can understand the purpose of each change.  
3. **Review the contract holistically** after applying the edits to confirm that no new inconsistencies or conflicts have been introduced.  
4. **Seek clarification** from the client on any business‑specific requirements (e.g., acceptable liability caps, preferred dispute‑resolution forum) before finalizing the red‑line version.  

*Assumed: The contract follows a typical commercial services format; the suggestions are therefore generic and should be tailored once the actual document is reviewed.*
