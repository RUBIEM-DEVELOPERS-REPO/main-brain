**Technical Writing — What “technical” Means**

When you, as a Technical Writer, describe something as *technical* you are referring to the specialized knowledge, methods, or language that belong to a particular field, industry, or job function. Authoritative dictionaries break the term down into three closely‑related senses that are useful for writing clear, audience‑appropriate documentation:

| Sense | Definition | Typical Context |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| **1. Relating to science or industry** | “relating to the knowledge, machines, or methods used in science and industry”【1】 | Describing engineering processes, laboratory equipment, or manufacturing workflows. |
| **2. Subject‑specific expertise** | “relating to the knowledge and methods of a particular subject or job”【1】 | Explaining how a software API works, or detailing a medical‑device protocol. |
| **3. Practical skills and methods** | “relating to practical skills and methods that are used in a particular activity”【1】 | Writing step‑by‑step instructions for assembling hardware or configuring a network. |

Merriam‑Webster reinforces these ideas, defining *technical* as “pertaining to a particular art, science, trade, or profession; involving specialized terminology or methods”【2】.

### How to Apply This in Your Writing

1. **Identify the audience’s expertise level** – If readers are specialists, you can retain the technical terminology; for a broader audience, translate or define those terms.
2. **Use precise, domain‑specific language** – This conveys authority and reduces ambiguity (e.g., “API endpoint” instead of “web address” when writing for developers).
3. **Balance detail with clarity** – Even when the content is inherently technical, break complex ideas into digestible steps, include diagrams, and provide glossaries for jargon.
4. **Maintain consistency** – Adopt a style guide that standardises how technical terms, units, and symbols are presented throughout the document set.

By grounding your documentation in these definitions, you ensure that “technical” content is both accurate to the field and accessible to the intended readers.
