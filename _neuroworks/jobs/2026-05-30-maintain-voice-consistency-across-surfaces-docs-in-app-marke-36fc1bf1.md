## Maintaining Voice Consistency Across Documentation, In‑App Text, and Marketing Hand‑off  

### 1. Define a Unified Voice Charter  
- **Core attributes** – List the tone (e.g., friendly, authoritative), style (concise, active‑voice), and personality traits that reflect the brand.  
- **Audience profiles** – Summarize primary user personas and the level of technical expertise each surface addresses.  
- **Key vocabulary** – Create a glossary of approved terms, preferred phrasing, and prohibited jargon.  

### 2. Centralize Guidelines in a Living Style Guide  
- Host a single, version‑controlled style guide (e.g., in a Markdown repository) that all teams can reference.  
- Include sections for **documentation**, **in‑app copy**, and **marketing** with examples that illustrate how the core voice adapts to each context while preserving the underlying tone.  

### 3. Implement Review Workflows  
- **Documentation** – Require peer review by a senior writer who checks for voice adherence before merge.  
- **In‑app** – Integrate a copy‑review step in the UI/UX design sprint; use a checklist that references the style guide.  
- **Marketing hand‑off** – Establish a formal sign‑off process where the technical writer validates that campaign copy aligns with the voice charter.  

### 4. Leverage Tooling for Consistency  
- Use linting plugins (e.g., Vale, Proselint) configured with the style guide to flag deviations automatically.  
- Apply terminology management tools (e.g., Phrase, Acrolinx) to enforce the approved glossary across all authoring platforms.  

### 5. Conduct Regular Audits and Updates  
- Schedule quarterly audits of each surface to identify drift and update the voice charter accordingly.  
- Gather feedback from product, design, and marketing stakeholders to refine the guidelines and ensure they remain practical.
