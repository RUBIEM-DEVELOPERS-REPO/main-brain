## Answer
**How to write a Talent‑Recruiter job description that focuses on outcomes, not buzzwords**

| Step | What to do | Why it matters (outcome‑focused) |
|------|------------|-----------------------------------|
| 1️⃣ Define the **core result** the role must deliver | e.g., “Increase the number of qualified candidates entering the interview pipeline by 20 % each quarter.” | Shows the business impact you expect, not the activities you think the recruiter will perform. |
| 2️⃣ Identify **key performance indicators (KPIs)** that measure that result | e.g., “Time‑to‑fill for senior‑engineer roles ≤ 45 days; candidate‑satisfaction score ≥ 4.5/5.” | Gives concrete, measurable targets that replace vague adjectives like “fast” or “efficient.” |
| 3️⃣ Translate each KPI into a **responsibility statement** that reads “Will … to achieve ….” | “Will design and run data‑driven sourcing campaigns to achieve a 20 % quarterly lift in qualified pipeline volume.” | Keeps the focus on *what* will be accomplished, not on the *how* (e.g., “leveraging cutting‑edge tools”). |
| 4️⃣ Add **impact statements** that tie the recruiter’s work to broader business goals | “Will partner with hiring managers to align talent strategy with product‑roadmap milestones, ensuring critical roles are staffed on schedule.” | Connects the role to company outcomes, making it clear why the work matters. |
| 5️⃣ Avoid **buzzword clusters** (e.g., “dynamic,” “synergistic,” “rock‑star”) and replace them with **action‑outcome language** | Instead of “dynamic talent‑acquisition guru,” write “Will own end‑to‑end talent‑acquisition for X‑function, delivering a 20 % increase in qualified candidates.” | Removes vague hype and makes expectations transparent. |
| 6️⃣ Include **minimum qualifications** that support the outcomes | “3+ years full‑cycle recruiting for technical roles; proven ability to improve pipeline conversion rates by ≥ 15 %.” | Ensures candidates have the proven capability to meet the stated results. |
| 7️⃣ Close with a **succinct purpose statement** | “Purpose: Deliver high‑quality talent that enables the organization to meet product‑delivery commitments on time and within budget.” | Reinforces the outcome‑first mindset for both recruiter and applicant. |

### Example Job Description (Outcome‑Centred)

> **Title:** Technical Talent Recruiter – Growth Teams  
> **Purpose:** Deliver high‑quality talent that enables the organization to meet product‑delivery commitments on time and within budget.  
> **Key Outcomes**  
> • Increase qualified candidate pipeline for senior‑engineer roles by **20 % each quarter**.  
> • Reduce **time‑to‑fill** for growth‑team positions to **≤ 45 days** while maintaining a **candidate‑satisfaction score ≥ 4.5/5**.  
> • Align talent‑acquisition strategy with quarterly product‑roadmap milestones, ensuring **critical roles are staffed on schedule**.  
> **Responsibilities**  
> • Design and run data‑driven sourcing campaigns **to achieve** the 20 % pipeline lift.  
> • Partner with hiring managers **to align** hiring plans with product milestones, **ensuring** on‑time staffing.  
> • Track, analyze, and report on KPI performance **to drive** continuous improvement.  
> **Qualifications**  
> • 3+ years full‑cycle recruiting for technical roles; **demonstrated** ≥ 15 % improvement in pipeline conversion.  
> • Proficiency with ATS and analytics tools **to support** data‑driven decision making.
