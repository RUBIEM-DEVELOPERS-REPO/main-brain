2. **Prioritise by impact and deadline** – Flag items that affect high‑stakes projects or have near‑term due dates, aligning follow‑up effort with strategic priorities.  

3. **Assign ownership** – Clearly note the responsible party (internal team member, external partner, or the executive) beside each task to ensure accountability.  

4. **Schedule regular “loop‑check” reviews** – Reserve a brief recurring slot (daily or every other day) to scan the open‑loop list, update statuses, and surface any stalls.  

5. **Send concise status nudges** – Use short, action‑oriented messages (e.g., “Hi [Owner], the deadline for [Commitment] is [Date]. Do you need anything from me to close this?”).  

6. **Escalate when needed** – After two reminders without progress, brief the executive with a summary and recommended next steps.  

7. **Close the loop formally** – Mark the task as completed, send a brief “Done” note to stakeholders, and archive the record for future reference.  

**Practical Tips**

- Use pre‑written reminder templates to reduce drafting time.  
- Attach relevant documents (meeting minutes, decision logs) so owners have context.  
- Visual dashboards (Kanban board, status bar) provide at‑a‑glance visibility.
