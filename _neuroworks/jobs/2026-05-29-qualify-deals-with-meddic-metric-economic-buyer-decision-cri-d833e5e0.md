## MEDDIC Qualification Guide for Account Executives

**Metric** – Identify the specific, measurable outcome the prospect expects (e.g., “reduce onboarding time by 40 %,” “save three full‑time equivalents,” “achieve SOC‑2 compliance within 30 days”). Quantify the target and the time horizon; this becomes the business case that justifies the purchase.

**Economic Buyer** – Pinpoint the individual who controls the budget and can sign the contract. Capture their name, title, and relationship to the buying process (e.g., CFO, VP of Operations). Verify whether you have direct access or need an introduction through a champion.

**Decision Criteria** – Document the formal rubric the prospect will use to evaluate solutions. Typical criteria include security compliance, integration depth, total cost of ownership, reference customers, and implementation timeline. Rank each criterion by importance as expressed by the buyer.

**Decision Process** – Map the exact workflow: who participates in each evaluation stage (technical review, legal, finance), required artifacts (RFP, PoC, pricing model), and expected timelines. Note any approval gates and the sequence in which stakeholders sign off.

**Identified Pain** – Articulate the concrete problem that drives urgency (e.g., “audit flagged gaps that must be closed within 60 days,” “current system causes a 15 % error rate in data entry”). Emphasize the impact on revenue, cost, or risk to demonstrate why the prospect will act now.

**Champion** – Secure an internal advocate who will actively promote your solution when you are not present. The champion should have a stake in the outcome (e.g., a department head who will benefit from efficiency gains) and the influence to rally other decision‑makers.

### How to Apply the Framework
1. **Gather data** during discovery calls, focusing on each MEDDIC element.  
2. **Populate a qualification matrix** (see template below) to visualize knowns, confidence levels, and gaps.  
3. **Prioritize next‑step questions** that close the highest‑impact gaps (usually Economic Buyer and Decision Process).  
4. **Validate the business case** by linking the Metric to the Identified Pain, then align your solution’s value proposition accordingly.
