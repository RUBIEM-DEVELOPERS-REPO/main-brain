## Answer
**MEDDIC – A quick cheat‑sheet for Account Executives**

| MEDDIC Element | What to uncover | Typical questions / data points |
|----------------|----------------|---------------------------------|
| **M – Metric** | The quantifiable business outcome the prospect cares about (e.g., cost‑savings, revenue lift, productivity gain). | *“What KPI will you use to measure success?”* |
| **E – Economic Buyer** | The person who controls the budget and can sign the deal. | *“Who ultimately approves the spend?”* |
| **D – Decision Criteria** | The formal set of requirements the solution must satisfy (features, compliance, ROI, integration). | *“What are the must‑have attributes for a solution?”* |
| **D – Decision Process** | The steps, timeline, and stakeholders involved from evaluation to sign‑off. | *“Can you walk me through the evaluation stages?”* |
| **I – Pain** | The specific problem or opportunity driving the purchase (inefficiency, risk, missed revenue). | *“What challenges are you trying to solve right now?”* |
| **C – Champion** | An internal advocate who believes in your solution and can influence the decision. | *“Who on your team will be most excited about this?”* |

**How to use the framework**

1. **Map each element early** – during discovery calls, capture concrete answers and document them in your CRM.  
2. **Validate and update** – as the sales cycle progresses, reconfirm the Economic Buyer and Decision Process; champions can change.  
3. **Prioritize** – a strong Metric and clear Pain give you leverage; a missing Economic Buyer or unclear Decision Process are red flags.  
4. **Leverage the Champion** – equip them with ROI data, battle‑cards, and messaging that aligns with the Decision Criteria.  

By systematically qualifying each MEDDIC component, you can gauge deal viability, forecast more accurately, and focus effort on opportunities with the highest probability of closing.
