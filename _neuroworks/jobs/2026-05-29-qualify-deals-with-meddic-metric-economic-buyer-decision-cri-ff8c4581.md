## MEDDIC Qualification Guide for Account Executives  

**Metric** – Define the measurable outcome the prospect expects (e.g., 30 % reduction in onboarding time, $200 k annual cost avoidance). Quantify the target and the timeframe, and capture how success will be tracked.  

**Economic Buyer** – Identify the individual with final budget authority. Record name, title, reporting line, and any financial KPIs they prioritize. Confirm whether you have direct access or need an introduction.  

**Decision Criteria** – List the explicit factors the prospect will use to compare solutions (price, feature set, integration depth, compliance, ROI, reference customers). Align each criterion with a strength of your offering.  

**Decision Process** – Map the evaluation workflow: steps (POC, RFP, vendor demo), stakeholders per step, approval gates, and estimated timeline. Highlight any required legal or security reviews.  

**Identified Pain** – Capture the specific business problem driving the purchase (e.g., manual data entry causing $50 k/month inefficiency). Quantify the impact and tie it to the Metric.  

**Champion** – Secure an internal advocate who will promote your solution, influence stakeholders, and help navigate obstacles. Document their motivations, level of influence, and preferred communication style.  

### Actionable Checklist  

1. **Gather Metrics** – Ask “What does success look like in numbers and by when?”  
2. **Confirm Economic Buyer** – Request a brief intro to the budget owner and note their decision‑making authority.  
3. **Document Decision Criteria** – Use a matrix to compare prospect requirements against your product features.  
4. **Outline Decision Process** – Create a timeline diagram showing each review stage and responsible party.  
5. **Validate Pain** – Quantify the pain point and link it directly to the prospect’s KPI.  
6. **Develop Champion Relationship** – Provide the champion with battle cards, ROI calculators, and regular status updates.
