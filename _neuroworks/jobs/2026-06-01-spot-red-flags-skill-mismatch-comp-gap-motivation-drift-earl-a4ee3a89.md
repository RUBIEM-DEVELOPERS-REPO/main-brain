## Talent Recruiter – Early Red‑Flag Checklist  

**Purpose** – Provide a concise, actionable framework that recruiters can apply during the first screening stages to surface three common risk areas: skill mismatch, compensation gap, and motivation drift.

### 1. Skill‑Fit Indicators  
- **Resume‑Job Description Gap** – Identify any required hard‑skill listed in the posting that is absent or only superficially mentioned on the candidate’s CV.  
- **Assessment Results** – Compare scores from any technical test or coding challenge against the benchmark for the role; a deviation of > 20 % signals a potential mismatch.  
- **Project Relevance** – Verify that the candidate’s recent projects use the same tools, languages, or frameworks as the position; a shift to unrelated tech stacks within the last 12 months is a red flag.

### 2. Compensation Gap Signals  
- **Current Salary vs. Target** – If the candidate’s current compensation is > 30 % lower than the role’s range and they request the top of the band, probe for hidden expectations (e.g., equity, bonuses).  
- **Salary History Disclosure** – Absence of disclosed salary history combined with a high desired salary may indicate unrealistic expectations.  
- **Market Benchmark Mismatch** – Cross‑check the candidate’s stated expectations against industry salary surveys for the same seniority and location; a discrepancy > 25 % warrants clarification.

### 3. Motivation‑Drift Warning Signs  
- **Frequent Role Changes** – More than 3 job changes in the past 5 years, especially without clear progression, can suggest low long‑term commitment.  
- **Misaligned Career Goals** – When the candidate’s stated objectives (e.g., “seeking management track”) conflict with the role’s scope (individual contributor), flag for deeper discussion.  
- **Passive Interest Indicators** – Minimal engagement with the company’s mission, products, or culture during the interview (e.g., no questions asked) may reflect low intrinsic motivation.

**Next Steps for Recruiters**  
1. Apply the checklist during resume review and initial phone screen.  
2. Document any red‑flag findings in the candidate’s ATS notes.  
3. Escalate candidates with multiple flags to the hiring manager for a decision on proceeding.
