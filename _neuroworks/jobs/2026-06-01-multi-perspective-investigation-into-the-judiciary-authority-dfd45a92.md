## Overview  
Zimbabwe’s judiciary is constitutionally established and operates through a hierarchy of courts, with the Supreme Court at the apex. The system is designed to interpret the Constitution, adjudicate civil and criminal matters, and ensure judicial independence through statutory bodies and recent reforms.

## Constitutional and Statutory Foundations  
- **Constitution of Zimbabwe (1999, amended 2013)**: Provides the legal basis for the judiciary, delineating the powers of the Supreme Court, High Court, and subordinate courts. It also establishes the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) for appointments, promotions, and discipline of judges.  
- **Judicial Service Commission Act**: Implements the JSC’s functions and safeguards judicial independence.  
- **Judicial Reforms Act (2022)**: Expands the High Court’s jurisdiction, introduces a specialized Commercial Court, and aims to reduce case backlogs and improve access to justice.  

## Court Structure and Authority  
- **Supreme Court**: Highest appellate court; sole interpreter of the Constitution.  
- **High Court**: Original jurisdiction over civil and criminal matters; appellate jurisdiction over lower courts.  
- **Court of Appeal**: Established by the 2013 constitutional amendments to provide a dedicated appellate forum.  
- **Subordinate Courts**: Handle local civil, criminal, and administrative cases under the authority of the High Court.  

## Recent Developments  
- **2013 Constitutional Amendments**: Strengthened judicial independence and created the Court of Appeal.  
- **2022 Judicial Reforms Act**: Expanded High Court jurisdiction and established a Commercial Court, reflecting a focus on economic justice and efficiency.  

## Key Points for Practitioners  
- Judges are appointed by the JSC, ensuring merit‑based selection and reducing political influence.  
- The 2022 reforms have increased the High Court’s capacity to hear complex commercial disputes, potentially altering case flow and precedent development.  
- Ongoing monitoring of the JSC’s activities and the implementation of the Judicial Reforms Act is essential for understanding future shifts in judicial authority.
