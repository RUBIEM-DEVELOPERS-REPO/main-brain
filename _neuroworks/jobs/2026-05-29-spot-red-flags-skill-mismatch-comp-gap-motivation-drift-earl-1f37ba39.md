### Overview  
Early detection of candidate issues—skill mismatch, compensation gaps, and motivation drift—prevents costly hiring mistakes. The following checklist consolidates actionable red‑flags derived from best‑practice recruiting principles and the peer‑reviewed draft provided in the evidence set [4].

### Checklist  

#### 1. Skill Mismatch  
- **Missing certifications or core tool proficiency** – candidate cannot demonstrate required credentials or familiarity with essential software/hardware.  
- **Portfolio gaps** – work samples lack projects relevant to the role’s key responsibilities.  
- **Interview gaps** – answers reveal insufficient depth in critical technical concepts or processes.  

#### 2. Compensation Gap  
- **Expectation outlier** – salary demand exceeds the market median for the role by more than 20 % without a clear justification (e.g., unique expertise).  
- **Equity resistance** – candidate unwilling to accept the organization’s internal equity structure or benefit package.  
- **Frequent compensation jumps** – history shows repeated large salary increases, which may signal instability or unrealistic expectations.  

#### 3. Motivation Drift  
- **Short‑tenure pattern** – career moves of less than 12 months or unrelated to the target role suggest a lack of long‑term commitment.  
- **“Paycheck‑only” mindset** – responses focus solely on compensation rather than growth, impact, or mission.  
- **Low enthusiasm for company vision** – candidate shows limited interest in the organization’s goals, culture, or development pathways.  

### Action Steps  

1. **Validate Skills** – administer a brief technical assessment or request reference checks focused on the identified competencies.  
2. **Benchmark Compensation** – compare the candidate’s expectations against up‑to‑date market data and internal salary bands.  
3. **Probe Motivation** – use scenario‑based questions that tie personal goals to the company’s mission and growth trajectory.  

Applying this checklist consistently during screening and interview stages will surface red‑flags early, enabling timely decisions on whether to advance or discontinue a candidate.
