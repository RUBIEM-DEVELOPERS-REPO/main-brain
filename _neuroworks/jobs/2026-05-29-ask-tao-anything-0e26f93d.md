## Answer
**Technical (adjective)**  

- **Relation to specialized knowledge or methods** – “Technical” describes anything that involves the specific knowledge, machines, or procedures used in science, industry, or a particular profession. For example, a “technical problem” is one that requires expertise in a field to resolve [1].  

- **Subject‑specific expertise** – It can refer to the body of knowledge and methods of a distinct discipline or job role (e.g., “technical papers,” “technical manager”). This sense emphasizes that the content is geared toward practitioners rather than the general public [1].  

- **Practical skills and techniques** – The term also covers the concrete skills and methods applied in an activity, such as “technical brilliance” in a performance or the “technical fault” that grounded a space shuttle [1].  

- **Complex, jargon‑heavy language** – When a document is described as “very technical,” it means the language is dense with specialist terminology, making it difficult for non‑experts to understand [1].  

**Key points for a technical writer**  

1. **Audience awareness** – Identify whether the reader needs the full depth of specialist detail (technical audience) or a simplified overview (non‑technical audience).  
2. **Clarity vs. precision** – Balance the necessity of precise, field‑specific terms with explanations or glossaries to aid comprehension.  
3. **Structure** – Use headings, lists, and visual aids to break down complex procedures into manageable steps, reducing the cognitive load of “technical” content.  

**Bottom line:** “Technical” denotes content that is rooted in specialized knowledge, methods, or practical skills of a particular field, often employing jargon that can be challenging for lay readers. A technical writer must translate this expertise into clear, accessible documentation while preserving the necessary precision.  

*Sources: Cambridge Dictionary definition of “technical” highlighting its relation to knowledge, methods, and practical skills [1]; Merriam‑Webster entry confirming the adjective’s meaning in similar contexts [2].*
