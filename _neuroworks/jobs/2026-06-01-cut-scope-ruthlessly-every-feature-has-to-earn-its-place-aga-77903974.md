## Scope‑Cutting Playbook for Product Managers  

### 1. Adopt a Binary Prioritisation Lens  
*Treat every candidate feature as a “keep” or “cut” decision.*  
- **Eliminate any item that does not meet a minimum score on a quantitative model** (see Section 2).  
- **Apply a hard cut‑off** (e.g., top 30 % of scores) to force discipline and avoid incremental creep.

### 2. Use a Proven Scoring Framework  

| Framework | Core Inputs | Why It Enables Ruthlessness |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------------------|
| **WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First)** | Cost of Delay (User‑value + Time‑criticality + Risk‑reduction) ÷ Job Size | Highlights high‑impact, low‑effort work; items with low WSJF are immediate candidates for removal. |
| **RICE** | Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort | Produces a comparable numeric value; set a minimum threshold to prune low‑scoring features. |
| **ICE** | Impact × Confidence × Effort | Simpler than RICE; useful for early‑stage ideas where data is sparse. |
| **MoSCoW (Must‑have, Should‑have, Could‑have, Won’t‑have)** | Qualitative categorisation | Enforce “Won’t‑have” as a permanent exclusion list; revisit only on major strategic shifts. |

*Implementation tip*: Populate a single spreadsheet with all pending features, calculate each metric, and sort descending. The bottom‑quartile becomes the “cut list.”

### 3. Enforce a “Kill‑Switch” Review Cycle  
1. **Quarterly Scope Review** – Re‑run the scoring model with the latest data.  
2. **Stakeholder Sign‑off** – Require at least two senior stakeholders to approve any feature that falls below the cut‑off.  
3. **Document Rationale** – Record why each cut was made (score, cost of delay, alignment). This creates institutional memory and reduces re‑consideration pressure.

### 4. Communicate the Rationale Clearly  
- Publish the scoring sheet and cut‑off rule in the product backlog view.  
- Highlight the **business impact** of the retained set (e.g., projected revenue, user‑growth).  
- Frame cuts as “resource reallocation to higher‑value outcomes” rather than “feature removal.”

### 5. Iterate and Refine  
- After each release, compare actual outcomes against predicted scores.  
- Adjust weighting factors (e.g., increase Time‑criticality if market windows tighten).  
- Continuously tighten the cut‑off threshold as the team matures in data‑driven decision‑making.

*Result*: A disciplined, evidence‑based process that forces every feature to earn its place against the next‑best alternative, eliminating low‑value work early and keeping the product roadmap lean.  

*Assumed: the product team has access to basic metrics (reach, effort, cost of delay) needed to populate the scoring frameworks.*
