## Report: Writing Outcome‑Focused Job Descriptions for Talent Recruiters  

**Purpose**  
Provide a concise guide that enables recruiters to craft job descriptions centered on measurable outcomes rather than generic buzzwords.  

**Key Recommendations**  

1. **State the Business Result First**  
   - Begin with the concrete impact the role must deliver, e.g., “Increase the qualified candidate pipeline by 30 % within six months” or “Reduce time‑to‑fill for technical positions from 45 to 30 days” [1].  

2. **Translate Results into Core Activities**  
   - Describe the specific actions that generate the outcome, such as “source, engage, and assess passive talent in the software‑engineering market” instead of vague phrases like “drive talent acquisition strategy” [1].  

3. **Quantify Expectations**  
   - Attach numbers, dates, or quality standards to each activity (e.g., “conduct 15 structured interviews per week” or “maintain a 90 % offer‑acceptance rate”) [1].  

4. **Link to the Organization’s Talent Definition**  
   - Use a clear definition of talent (e.g., “a natural skill or ability to be good at something”) and frame the role as applying that ability to achieve the stated outcomes [1].  

5. **Eliminate Jargon**  
   - Replace buzzwords such as “synergy,” “thought leader,” or “culture champion” with plain verbs and measurable targets, for example, “build relationships with hiring managers to align on role requirements” [1].  

6. **End with Success Metrics**  
   - List the KPIs that will be used to judge performance, such as “candidate satisfaction score ≥ 4.5/5” and “diversity hiring ratio ≥ 40 %” [1].  

**Template Example**  

> **Role:** Senior Technical Recruiter  
> **Outcome:** Grow the qualified engineering candidate pipeline by 30 % within 6 months while cutting average time‑to‑fill from 45 to 30 days.  
> **Core Activities:**  
> - Source and engage ≥ 50 passive candidates per week in target markets.  
> - Conduct 15 structured interviews weekly and provide hiring‑manager feedback within 24 hours.  
> - Maintain an offer‑acceptance rate of ≥ 90 %.  
> **Success Metrics:** Candidate satisfaction ≥ 4.5/5; diversity hiring ratio ≥ 40 %; quarterly pipeline growth ≥ 30 %.  

**Conclusion**  
By anchoring job descriptions in specific business results, quantifiable activities, and clear success metrics, recruiters attract candidates who understand the role’s purpose and can be held accountable for delivering measurable value.
