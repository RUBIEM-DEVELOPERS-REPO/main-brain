## Conducting Customer Interviews that Surface Past Behaviour  

**Objective** – Gather reliable, action‑based insights by prompting interviewees to recount concrete experiences rather than speculate about hypothetical futures.  

### Question Framework  

| Goal | Sample question (past‑behaviour focus) | Rationale |
|------|----------------------------------------|-----------|
| Identify real actions | “Can you walk me through the last time you **[performed the task / used a similar solution]**?” | Forces the interviewee to describe a specific episode, eliminating imagined scenarios. |
| Uncover triggers & context | “What prompted you to start looking for a solution the last time you faced **X**?” | Reveals the situational cues that actually drove the behaviour. |
| Map the decision flow | “When you decided to choose **Y**, what alternatives did you consider and why did you reject them?” | Shows the real evaluation criteria that mattered at the moment. |
| Measure frequency & effort | “How often do you currently do **Z**, and how much time does it take?” | Provides observable quantitative signals (frequency, duration). |
| Surface work‑arounds | “When the tool didn’t work the way you wanted, what did you do instead?” | Highlights pain points and improvisations that indicate unmet needs. |
| Capture outcomes & satisfaction | “After you completed **X**, what was the result for you? How did you feel about it?” | Links behaviour to tangible outcomes, helping gauge perceived value. |
| Validate assumptions | “You mentioned you usually do **Y** in the mornings—what does a typical morning look like?” | Checks the accuracy of any prior assumptions with real‑world routines. |

### Interview Process  

1. **Preparation** – Define the target behaviour and identify the most recent relevant user actions.  
2. **Warm‑up** – Begin with low‑stakes, factual questions (e.g., “When did you last use the product?”) to build recall confidence.  
3. **Core probing** – Apply the question framework above, iterating as needed to drill deeper into each step of the user’s journey.  
4. **Closing** – Ask for any additional anecdotes or work‑arounds not yet covered, and thank the participant for sharing concrete experiences.  

### Benefits  

- **Higher fidelity data** – Concrete recollections are less prone to bias than “what‑if” answers.  
- **Actionable insights** – Reveals actual decision criteria, frequency, and work‑arounds that can directly inform product improvements.  
- **Validated assumptions** – Enables rapid testing of hypotheses against real user behaviour.
