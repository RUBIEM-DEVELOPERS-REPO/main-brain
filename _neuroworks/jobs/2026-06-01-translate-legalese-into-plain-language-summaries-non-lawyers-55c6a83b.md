## Plain‑Language Contract Summaries for Non‑Lawyers  

**Purpose** – Convert dense contract clauses into clear, actionable points that anyone can understand and act on.

### 1. Identify Core Contract Elements  
Look for the five building blocks: **offer, acceptance, consideration, mutual assent, legality**【1†L13-L20】. Explain each in everyday terms, e.g., “what one side promises, what the other side gives, and that both agree it’s a real deal.”

### 2. Extract the “Who, What, When, Where, How Much”  
Pull out:  
- Parties involved  
- What is being exchanged  
- Deadlines or dates  
- Monetary amounts or other values  

Present this as a simple sentence: “You (the freelancer) will deliver the website by June 30 and get paid $3,000.”

### 3. Translate Common Legal Clauses  
| Legal term | Plain‑language equivalent |
|------------|---------------------------|
| **Force‑Majeure** | “If a natural disaster or war stops work, the deadline can be extended.” |
| **Indemnification** | “If someone sues because of your mistake, you’ll pay the costs.” |
| **Arbitration** | “Disagreements will be settled by a neutral third‑party, not a court.” |
| **Termination** | “Either side can end the agreement early if they give 30 days’ notice.” |

Use short, active sentences and replace Latin or legal jargon with everyday words【3†redacted】.

### 4. Highlight Required Actions  
Create a checklist of steps the reader must take, such as:  
- Sign the agreement  
- Deliver the specified work or product  
- Make the required payment  
- Provide any required notices  

This “to‑do” list ensures the reader knows exactly what is expected without needing to interpret additional language.
