## Account Executive – Risk Identification & Escalation Guide

**Key Risks**  
1. **No Champion** – Absence of an internal advocate who actively promotes the opportunity and can influence decision‑makers.  
2. **No Compelling Event** – Lack of a time‑bound business driver (e.g., regulatory deadline, budget cycle, competitive pressure) that creates urgency for the purchase.  
3. **Ghost Buyer** – The primary contact has little or no authority to approve the deal, and no senior stakeholder has been identified.

**Risk Analysis**  
- Each of these signals reduces the probability of closing and increases the cost of continued engagement.  
- When any risk is present, the opportunity should be re‑evaluated for viability before further resource allocation.

**Escalation Procedure**  
1. **Document** the observed risk in the CRM opportunity record, including specific evidence (e.g., meeting notes, email excerpts).  
2. **Flag** the opportunity with a high‑visibility tag (e.g., “Risk – No Champion”).  
3. **Notify** the Sales Manager or Account‑Team Lead via the standard escalation channel (e.g., Slack #sales‑escalations or email).  
4. **Action Plan** (to be defined by the manager):  
   - Identify or cultivate a true champion within the prospect organization.  
   - Work with the prospect to surface a compelling event or create urgency (e.g., pilot deadline, budget window).  
   - Confirm the buyer’s decision‑making authority or engage a higher‑level stakeholder.  
5. **Follow‑up**: Record the outcome of the escalation (e.g., risk mitigated, opportunity re‑qualified, or closed‑lost) within 5 business days.
