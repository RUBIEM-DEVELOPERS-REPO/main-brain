## Executive Assistant Report: Closing Open Loops & Chasing Commitments  

**Purpose** – Provide a concise, actionable framework for an Executive Assistant (EA) to identify, prioritize, track, and follow up on all pending commitments made on the executive’s behalf.

### 1. Identify Every Pending Commitment  
- Aggregate sources: email threads, meeting notes, calendar invites, task‑tracking tools, and the EA’s own inbox.  
- Tag each item with a clear description, the responsible party, and the original due date.  

### 2. Prioritise by Impact and Urgency  
- Apply a simple matrix (high‑impact / high‑urgency, etc.) to decide which loops need immediate follow‑up.  
- Align the priority list with the executive’s strategic objectives so effort focuses on the most valuable items.  

### 3. Create a Living “Commitment Tracker”  
- Use a shared spreadsheet, Kanban board, or NeuroWorks task list (if the platform is available).  
- Recommended columns: **Commitment**, **Owner**, **Original Due Date**, **Follow‑up Date**, **Status**, **Notes**.  

### 4. Craft Concise Follow‑Up Messages  
- Reference the original request, restate the expected outcome, and ask for a concrete update or new deadline.  
- Example tone: “Hi [Name], just checking on the status of [commitment] discussed on [date]. Could you let me know if we’re on track for [date] or need to adjust?”  

### 5. Schedule Reminder Checkpoints  
- Set calendar reminders for the EA to revisit each commitment before its due date.  
- If a response remains missing, send a polite second reminder and, if necessary, escalate according to the executive’s escalation protocol.
