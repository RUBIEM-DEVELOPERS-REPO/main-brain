## Maintaining Accurate Client Records and Ensuring Timely Policy Renewals  

1. **Data hygiene**  
   - **Audit and de‑duplicate** after each enrollment or sales cycle; merge duplicate client or policy rows and remove inactive entries.  
   - **Standardize key fields** (carrier, policy number, effective dates, contact information, beneficiaries, dependents) using drop‑down lists or validation rules.  
   - **Attach supporting documents** (signed applications, compliance forms, call recordings, notes) directly to the client record and tag them for easy retrieval.  
   - **Verify contact details** (email, phone, mailing address) at least quarterly and capture any updates in a short “record‑update” outreach.  

2. **Renewal alert system**  
   - Create tasks or calendar events 30–90 days before each policy’s expiration.  
   - Follow a multi‑step outreach cadence: initial reminder email, follow‑up call, secondary reminder, and final notice.  
   - Automate task creation and status tracking within the CRM to avoid manual oversights.  

3. **Interaction logging**  
   - Record the outcome of every client contact immediately, noting conversation points, next steps, and any required documentation.  

4. **Performance monitoring**  
   - Generate a monthly report showing renewal rates, lapses, and cross‑sell conversions.  
   - Review the data to refine scripts, outreach timing, and pipeline stages.  

5. **Cross‑sell opportunities**  
   - Use renewal conversations to ask about recent life or business changes that may create needs for additional coverage.  

### Daily/Weekly Checklist  
- Review upcoming renewals.  
- Verify and update contact information.  
- Schedule the next outreach step.  
- Log notes and attach relevant documents.  
- Update renewal status in the system.
