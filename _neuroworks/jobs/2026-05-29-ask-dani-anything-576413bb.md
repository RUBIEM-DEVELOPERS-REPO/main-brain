## Product Design Overview  

A **product** is any tangible or intangible offering created to satisfy a consumer’s need or desire. It may be a physical item, a service, a digital experience, or a conceptual solution [1].  

### Core Design Attributes  

| Attribute | Design Implication |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Market fit** | Ensure the solution addresses a real problem or genuine want in the target market [1]. |
| **Unique value proposition** | Articulate clearly why the product is distinct and superior to alternatives [1]. |
| **Quality** | Design decisions—materials, interaction, visual language—must deliver a high‑quality experience that builds trust [1]. |
| **Pricing & positioning** | Align perceived value created by design (aesthetic, usability, brand) with the price strategy [1]. |
| **Brand & experience** | Visual identity, tone, and the overall user journey form the product’s “brand” and drive loyalty [1]. |
| **Distribution & channels** | Consider delivery mechanisms (retail, app store, SaaS platform, etc.) to guarantee accessibility [1]. |
| **Customer feedback loop** | Implement ongoing research and testing to evolve the design with user needs [1]. |

### Product Types Relevant to Design  

- **Physical products** – ergonomics, material selection, and packaging are primary concerns.  
- **Non‑physical products** – interaction design, information architecture, and service flow dominate.  

By systematically addressing these attributes, a product designer can create solutions that are functional, desirable, and commercially viable.
