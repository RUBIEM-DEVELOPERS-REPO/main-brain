**Job Summary – Insurance Premiums, Deductibles & Coverage Conditions**

The role is responsible for designing, analyzing, and communicating the financial and policy elements of insurance products, with a focus on three core components:

| Component | What It Is | Key Responsibilities |
|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| **Premium** | The regular (usually monthly or annual) payment that keeps a policy active. In employer‑sponsored health plans the cost is split between employee and employer, the employee’s share being the “employee contribution”【1】. | • Calculate premium rates based on risk, market data, and underwriting guidelines.<br>• Explain premium structures to sales teams and policyholders.<br>• Monitor premium‑revenue performance and adjust pricing as needed. |
| **Deductible** | The amount the insured must pay out‑of‑pocket before the insurer begins to pay for covered expenses. It can be set per‑year, per‑service, or per‑procedure, and resets each policy period【1†L31-L36】【2†L9-L13】. | • Define deductible levels for different plan designs (e.g., low‑deductible/high‑premium vs. high‑deductible/low‑premium).<br>• Ensure deductible terms are clearly reflected in policy documents and system rules.<br>• Educate customers on how deductibles affect out‑of‑pocket costs and premium choices. |
| **Coverage Conditions** | The specific rules that determine which expenses are “covered” and therefore count toward the deductible, copays, coinsurance, and out‑of‑pocket maximums. Only covered expenses can be applied to the deductible; non‑covered services are excluded【2†L13-L16】. | • Draft and maintain coverage condition language (e.g., in‑network vs. out‑of‑network, preventive care exclusions).<br>• Coordinate with underwriting, legal, and actuarial teams to align conditions with risk appetite.<br>• Conduct compliance checks to ensure conditions meet state/federal regulations. |

### Typical Duties
1. **Product Development** – Build plan prototypes that balance premium levels, deductible amounts, and coverage conditions to meet market segments (e.g., high‑use vs. low‑use customers).  
2. **Financial Modeling** – Use actuarial tools to project how changes in premiums or deductibles affect loss ratios and profitability.  
3. **Customer Communication** – Create clear, jargon‑free explanations of premiums, deductibles, and coverage rules for sales collateral and member portals.  
4. **Regulatory Compliance** – Verify that deductible reset periods, premium disclosures, and coverage exclusions comply with state insurance statutes (e.g., South Carolina Department of Insurance guidance).  
5. **Claims Support** – Work with claims teams to ensure that deductible applications and coverage condition determinations are consistent and transparent.

### Desired Skills & Knowledge
- Understanding of **premium pricing** mechanics and cost‑sharing concepts (copays, coinsurance, out‑of‑pocket maximums)【1】.  
- Ability to explain **deductible structures** (annual vs. per‑service) and their impact on consumer out‑of‑pocket costs【1†L31-L36】【2†L9-L13】.  
- Familiarity with **coverage condition language** and exclusions, ensuring only eligible expenses reduce the deductible【2†L13-L16】.  
- Proficiency in actuarial/financial modeling, regulatory research, and clear written communication.

In short, the position bridges the financial design of insurance products (premiums & deductibles) with the legal/operational definition of what is covered, ensuring both profitability for the insurer and understandable value for the policyholder.
