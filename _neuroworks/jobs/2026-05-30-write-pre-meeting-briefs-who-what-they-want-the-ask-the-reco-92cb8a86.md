## Pre‑Meeting Brief  

| Section | Content to Include | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | • Name, title, department of each participant  <br>• Brief background (recent projects, key responsibilities)  <br>• Decision‑making authority (final approver, influencer) | Gives the meeting leader a quick mental picture of the audience and their stakes. |
| **What they want** | • Primary objective each attendee is trying to achieve (e.g., “secure budget for Q3 marketing push,” “clarify timeline for product launch”)  <br>• Known concerns or constraints expressed beforehand | Anticipating goals lets you steer the agenda toward outcomes they care about and pre‑empt objections. |
| **The ask** | • Specific decision or action needed from the group (e.g., “approve $150 k spend,” “sign off on revised roadmap”)  <br>• Supporting data points or documents that will be referenced | A clear, concise ask keeps the meeting focused and makes it easy for participants to say “yes” or provide needed feedback. |
| **Recommended answer** | • Suggested response or decision (based on prior analysis, stakeholder input, and company policy)  <br>• Key rationale (benefits, risks, alignment with strategy)  <br>• Follow‑up steps required after the meeting | Provides the meeting leader with a ready‑to‑present position, saving time and demonstrating preparedness. |

### How to Use This Template  

1. Populate each section with the specific details for the upcoming meeting.  
2. Review the “Recommended answer” to ensure it aligns with current strategy and any recent stakeholder input.  
3. Share the completed brief with the meeting leader at least 24 hours before the meeting.
