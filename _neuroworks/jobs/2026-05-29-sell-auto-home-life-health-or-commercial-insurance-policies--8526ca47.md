## Quick Playbook for Selling Auto, Home, Life, Health, or Commercial Insurance

### 1. Master the Core Product Elements  
- **Premium**: The periodic cost the client pays.  
- **Deductible**: The amount the client pays before coverage kicks in.  
- **Policy Limit**: The maximum amount the insurer will pay.  

These three levers are the primary comparison points for customers and directly influence both price and protection levels【1†L15-L22】.

### 2. Identify the Customer’s Needs  
Ask targeted questions to uncover risk exposure and coverage priorities:  

| Line of Coverage | Key Questions |
|------------------|---------------|
| Auto | Driving history, vehicle value, usage patterns |
| Home | Property value, location, replacement cost |
| Life | Age, dependents, financial goals |
| Health | Age, health status, coverage preferences |
| Commercial | Industry risks, employee count, regulatory requirements |

Insurance is purchased to provide financial protection against specific losses; matching the right coverage to the risk profile is essential【1†L31-L38】.

### 3. Position the Value Proposition  
Translate product features into tangible benefits:  

- **Lower deductible** → lower out‑of‑pocket when a claim occurs.  
- **Higher policy limit** → protection against catastrophic loss.  
- **Bundling** (e.g., auto & home) → overall premium reduction.  

Customers care about both cost and protection; clear explanations of how premiums, deductibles, and limits affect both help them see value.
