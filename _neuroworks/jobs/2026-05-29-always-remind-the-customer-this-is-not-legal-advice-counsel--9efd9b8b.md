## Answer
**Reminder for Contract Reviewers**

When you are reviewing a contract or providing any commentary on its terms, always include a clear disclaimer such as:

> **“This is not legal advice. Please have qualified counsel review the agreement before you sign.”**

**Why this matters**

- It protects you and the organization from the appearance of offering legal services you are not authorized to give.  
- It signals to the client that a licensed attorney should give the final sign‑off, reducing the risk of misunderstandings or liability.  

**Suggested placement**

- At the beginning of any written response or summary.  
- In the closing paragraph of an email or chat interaction.  
- In any template or canned response you use for contract‑related queries.  

**Example disclaimer**

> *“I’m happy to point out the key provisions and flag potential issues, but please note that I am not a lawyer. This information is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. You should have a qualified attorney review the contract before you sign.”*
