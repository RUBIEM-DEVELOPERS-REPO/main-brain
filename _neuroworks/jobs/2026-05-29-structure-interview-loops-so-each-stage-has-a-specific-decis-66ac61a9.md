## Interview‑Loop Structure for Talent Recruiters  

| Stage | Participants | Primary Goal | Decision Point |
|-------|--------------|--------------|----------------|
| **1️⃣ Screening (Phone/Video)** | Recruiter or HR screening specialist | Verify basic qualifications, cultural fit, and candidate interest. | **Proceed / Reject** – advance only if the candidate meets minimum job requirements and demonstrates genuine enthusiasm. |
| **2️⃣ Technical / Functional Assessment** | Hiring manager + subject‑matter expert(s) | Evaluate job‑specific skills (e.g., coding test, case study, portfolio review). | **Pass / Fail** – move forward only when the candidate shows the required competency level. |
| **3️⃣ Team‑Fit / Behavioral Interview** | Panel of future teammates (2‑3) + recruiter | Assess collaboration style, problem‑solving approach, and alignment with team values using behavioral (STAR) questions. | **Recommend / Do Not Recommend** – panel votes; a predefined majority (e.g., 2‑out‑of‑3) is required to proceed. |
| **4️⃣ Executive / Final Interview** | Senior leader or department head + recruiter | Confirm strategic alignment, discuss compensation expectations, and gauge long‑term potential. | **Offer / Decline** – final decision based on overall fit and business needs. |

### Implementation Guidelines  
- **Documentation:** Record evaluation criteria and outcomes for each stage in a centralized candidate tracker.  
- **Timing:** Allocate 1–2 days between stages to allow reviewers to submit feedback without stalling the process.  
- **Feedback Loop:** Provide candidates with concise feedback after each decision point, reinforcing a positive candidate experience.  
- **Escalation:** If a candidate fails a stage but shows high potential, allow a “re‑consider” path where a senior recruiter can request a re‑assessment.
