## Maintaining Accurate Client Records & Managing Policy Renewals  
**Insurance Sales Agent – Best‑Practice Guide**

### 1. Centralize Client Information  
- Store all client data in a single CRM or a structured spreadsheet.  
- Capture personal details, policy specifics (type, limits, deductible, premium, effective & expiration dates), and interaction notes.  
- Centralization prevents duplicate entries and provides a complete history for quick reference.  

### 2. Standardize Data Fields  
- Create a mandatory template with consistent formats for dates, phone numbers, and monetary values.  
- Consistency improves searchability and reduces errors when generating renewal notices.  

### 3. Tag Policies by Renewal Cycle  
- Add a “renewal‑date” column and a status flag (e.g., *Upcoming*, *Pending Review*, *Renewed*, *Lapsed*).  
- Tracking the term end date allows timely action before a policy lapses【1†L31-L38】.  

### 4. Automate Reminders  
- Set calendar alerts 7 and 30 days before expiration.  
- If the CRM supports workflow rules, automate email notifications to you and the client when a renewal is due.  

### 5. Conduct Pre‑Renewal Reviews  
- Review policy coverage, claims history, and client needs at least 60 days before renewal.  
- Adjust coverage or recommend new products as appropriate.  

### 6. Follow‑Up Communication  
- Send a renewal proposal at least 30 days before expiration.  
- Follow up with a phone call or email if no response is received within 7 days.  

### 7. Document All Interactions  
- Log every call, email, and meeting in the CRM.  
- Include outcomes, next steps, and any client objections.  

### 8. Perform Post‑Renewal Confirmation  
- Once a renewal is confirmed, update the status flag to *Renewed* and record the new effective date.  
- Send a confirmation letter or email to the client.  

### 9. Review and Audit  
- Conduct quarterly audits of client records to ensure accuracy and completeness.  
- Address any discrepancies immediately to maintain data integrity.
