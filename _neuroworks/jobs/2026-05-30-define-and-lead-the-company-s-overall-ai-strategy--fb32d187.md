**Purpose**  
Provide a concise framework that a Head of AI can use to define and lead a company‑wide artificial‑intelligence strategy.

**Key Components (high‑level)**  

1. **Vision & Business Alignment**  
   * Articulate a clear AI vision that supports the company’s overall mission and revenue goals.  
   * Map AI opportunities to core product lines, customer segments, and operational efficiencies.

2. **Market & Technology Landscape Assessment**  
   * Conduct systematic research on emerging AI models, tooling, and competitor initiatives.  
   * Identify high‑impact use‑cases where AI can create measurable value within 12‑24 months.

3. **Strategic Roadmap**  
   * Prioritize initiatives into short‑term pilots, mid‑term deployments, and long‑term innovation programs.  
   * Define milestones, success metrics (KPIs), and resource requirements for each phase.

4. **Organizational Structure & Talent**  
   * Build cross‑functional AI squads (research, engineering, product, ethics).  
   * Establish hiring, up‑skilling, and partnership plans to fill skill gaps.

5. **Governance & Ethics**  
   * Implement an AI ethics framework covering bias, privacy, security, and regulatory compliance.  
   * Set up an oversight board to review high‑risk projects and ensure responsible deployment.

6. **Infrastructure & Data Strategy**  
   * Choose scalable compute platforms (cloud, on‑prem, hybrid) and data pipelines.  
   * Enforce data quality, provenance, and governance standards.

7. **Performance Measurement & Continuous Improvement**  
   * Track ROI, model accuracy, and operational impact against defined KPIs.  
   * Iterate the roadmap based on feedback, emerging research, and market shifts.

**Leadership Actions**  
* Champion AI initiatives at the executive level and secure budgetary commitment.  
* Communicate progress and outcomes regularly to stakeholders.  
* Foster a culture of experimentation, learning, and ethical responsibility.
