### 1. Record Creation & Maintenance  
- **Centralize data**: Enter every client into a single CRM (e.g., HubSpot, Salesforce, or a dedicated insurance‑CRM).  
- **Standard fields**: Policy number, type, start/end dates, premium amount, payment method, renewal date, last contact, notes, and any special client requests.  
- **Data hygiene**: Run a weekly “data‑clean” routine: verify contact details, update addresses, and flag duplicate entries.  
  *Tools/Tips*: Use CRM import templates, set up validation rules (e.g., email format, mandatory fields).  
  *Owner/Deadline*: Agent – daily entry; Supervisor – weekly audit.  

### 2. Reminder & Automation Setup  
- **Renewal calendar**: Create a “Renewal” task for each policy 30 days before expiry.  
- **Automated alerts**: Configure email/SMS reminders to the agent 30, 15, and 7 days prior.  
- **Client self‑service portal**: Provide a link in the reminder for clients to view/renew online.  
  *Tools/Tips*: Use CRM workflow builder or Zapier to trigger reminders; integrate with a payment gateway for instant online renewals.  
  *Owner/Deadline*: Agent – set up workflow; IT – ensure integration.  

### 3. Communication Templates  
- **Initial Reminder (30 days)**: Friendly notice with renewal link.  
- **Follow‑Up (15 days)**: Highlight benefits of staying insured, mention any new coverage options.  
- **Final Reminder (7 days)**: Urgent tone, emphasize potential lapse consequences.  

### 4. Performance Tracking  
- **KPIs**: Renewal rate, average time to renewal, client satisfaction score.  
- **Reporting**: Monthly dashboard in CRM; review with management to refine processes.
