**Product (as a Product‑Manager’s reference)**  

A **product** is “something that can be seen or experienced, created to meet the needs or desires of consumers.” It may be a **physical item, a service, an experience, or even a concept** — the common thread is that it delivers value that customers are willing to pay for [1].  

Key attributes that a product manager must consider:  

| Aspect | What it means for the product |
|--------|------------------------------|
| **Market fit** | The product must solve a real problem or satisfy a genuine want in the target market. |
| **Unique value proposition** | Clearly articulate why the product is different and why customers should choose it over alternatives. |
| **Quality** | Deliver a level of performance, reliability, or service that builds trust and encourages repeat use. |
| **Pricing** | Set a price that reflects the product’s perceived value while staying competitive. |
| **Marketing & branding** | Use positioning, messaging, and brand assets to raise awareness and foster loyalty. |
| **Distribution & channels** | Choose the right channels (retail, online, partner networks, etc.) so the product is easily reachable. |
| **Customer feedback** | Continuously collect and act on user input to iterate and improve the offering. |

The term also appears in everyday English as “something that is made to be sold, usually produced by an industrial process” [2]. In broader usage, *product* can refer to the result of any process (e.g., “the product of many hours of training”) [2], but in a business context the definition above is the operative one.

**Types of products** (useful for roadmap segmentation)  

- **Physical products** – tangible goods (clothing, gadgets, cars).  
- **Non‑physical products** – services (insurance, consulting).  
- **Digital products** – software, apps, e‑books, online courses.  
- **Service‑as‑product** – offerings like food‑delivery or home‑cleaning that are packaged as purchasable solutions [1].

Understanding these dimensions helps a product manager shape strategy, prioritize features, and align cross‑functional teams around delivering a product that meets market demand and drives business success.
