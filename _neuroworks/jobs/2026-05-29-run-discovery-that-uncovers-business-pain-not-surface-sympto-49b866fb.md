## Discovery Call Framework for Uncovering Business Pain

1. **Set the context** – Begin by stating that the purpose of the call is to understand the client’s deeper business challenges, not merely the symptoms they are currently experiencing. This establishes intent and builds trust.

2. **Apply the “5 Why” technique** – When a symptom is mentioned (e.g., “sales are down”), ask “Why?” repeatedly (typically up to five times) until the underlying driver is revealed. This keeps the conversation focused on root causes rather than surface effects.

3. **Ask open‑ended, outcome‑focused questions**  
   - “What would success look like for your sales team in the next 12 months?”  
   - “What obstacles prevent you from achieving that success?”  
   - “How does this issue impact your bottom line?”  

   These prompts encourage the prospect to articulate strategic goals and the barriers that matter most to the business.

4. **Listen for pain signals** – Pay close attention to emotional cues such as frustration, urgency, or hesitation. Such signals often indicate deeper, unspoken pain points.

5. **Validate and summarize** – Restate the identified pain points in the prospect’s own language to confirm understanding and demonstrate active listening.

6. **Document and follow up** – Capture the pain points in a concise note and send a brief recap to the prospect within 24 hours. This reinforces professionalism and keeps momentum toward a solution discussion.  

By combining clear context setting, the 5 Why probing method, outcome‑oriented questioning, attentive listening, and prompt follow‑up, an Account Executive can reliably surface the true business pains that drive purchasing decisions.
