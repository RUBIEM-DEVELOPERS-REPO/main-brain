## Closing Open Loops & Chasing Commitments – Quick‑Start Report for the Executive  

**Purpose** – Ensure that every commitment made on the executive’s behalf is captured, prioritized, owned, and tracked so nothing falls through the cracks and the executive’s time is protected.

### 1. Capture Every Commitment  
- Log each action immediately after meetings, calls, or emails in a single “Commitments” list (e.g., a Notion table or the NeuroWorks task board).  
- Record **who** made the commitment, **what** was promised, **when** it is due, and attach any supporting documents.  
*Why:* Guarantees reliability and gives the executive confidence that follow‑up is owned. [1]

### 2. Prioritise by Impact & Deadline  
- Tag items with a priority level (High / Medium / Low) and a due‑date.  
- Align high‑impact items with upcoming strategic milestones in the executive’s calendar.  
*Why:* Not all loops are equal; focusing on high‑impact items maximises the executive’s limited time. [1]

### 3. Assign Ownership & Set Reminders  
- If the commitment belongs to the executive, create a calendar reminder (e.g., “Review contract — due May 31”).  
- If it belongs to a stakeholder, send a concise “action‑required” email restating the ask, deadline, and any needed resources.  
*Why:* Clear responsibility prevents “someone else will do it” paralysis. [1]

### 4. Track Status in Real‑Time  
- Update the task board each time a status change is received.  
- Provide the executive with a single‑pane‑of‑glass view of all open loops, enabling quick status checks before meetings.  
*Why:* Gives the executive immediate visibility into progress and outstanding items. [1]

### 5. Review & Close  
- Conduct a weekly “loop‑review” session with the executive to confirm completion, re‑prioritise any lingering items, and archive closed commitments.  
- Document lessons learned for continuous improvement of the process.
